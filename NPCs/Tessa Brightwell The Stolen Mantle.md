### The Truth She Buried

Marren Brightwell was the real thing. A genuine Lliiran lay priest with a true calling—kind, joyful, selfless. He possessed one of the few surviving original copies of the _Book of Exultation_, handwritten and blessed. People were drawn to him naturally. He made them feel _seen_.

Tessa was his wife. She loved him. She also _envied_ him.

When the Helmites came for Marren ten years ago, Tessa wasn't home. She came back to an empty house, a ransacked shrine room, and one thing the Helmites missed: the Book, hidden in a false panel Marren had shown only her.

She told herself she was protecting it. Preserving his legacy. But somewhere in the years that followed—reading his annotations, learning his prayers, repeating his words—something shifted. She stopped saying "Marren taught" and started saying "I believe." She stopped being his widow and started being his _replacement_.

The 29 followers don't know Marren. They know Tessa. She's the one who found them, gathered them, gave them hope. She's the one with the Book. She's the one who speaks with such conviction.

Tessa has told the story so many times—edited, softened, recentered—that she half-believes it herself. Marren was devout, yes. But _she_ was the strong one. _She_ kept the faith alive. The Book was meant for her. It must have been.

She's not evil. She's not even fully conscious of the lie. But she's built her identity on a foundation of theft and self-deception, and she's terrified—_terrified_—of being exposed as ordinary.

---

### Personality Now

**Publicly:** Warm, wise, maternal. She knows exactly when to touch someone's arm, when to speak softly, when to invoke Lliira's name. She's _good_ at this. Disturbingly good.

**Privately:** Anxious, controlling, paranoid about the Book. She doesn't let anyone else read it. She interprets it _for_ them. If someone questions her or remembers Marren too specifically, she redirects, reframes, or freezes them out.

**Her Tell:** When genuinely challenged, her warmth vanishes. A flash of something cold and hard before she smooths it over. She _needs_ to be right. She _needs_ to be special.

---

### The Powers

Here's the dark question: _Do her powers come from Lliira?_

**Option A — Yes, and it's tragic.** Lliira's blessing passes through the Book, not the person. Tessa has real divine magic despite being a fraud. The goddess of joy empowers a woman eaten by envy and insecurity. What does that say about faith?

**Option B — No, it's all her.** Tessa has low-level sorcerous ability she doesn't recognize—maybe wild magic, maybe shadow touched by Shar without knowing. She thinks it's Lliira. It isn't. When Lliira died, nothing changed for Tessa. That should tell her something. It won't.

**Option C — It's complicated.** She _had_ borrowed power through the Book. When Lliira died, it should have stopped. But the Lastbloom exists. Something survived. And Tessa, holding an original Book of Exultation, might be drawing on that ember without understanding what she's siphoning.

---

### What She Wants

- To get "her people" to safety (genuine)
- To be seen as their savior and spiritual leader (ego)
- To never, ever have anyone find out about Marren's real role (terror)
- To believe her own story (desperate)

---

### Potential Reveals

- Someone who knew Marren recognizes a phrase, a prayer, a passage and says "That's exactly how _he_ used to say it..."
- The Book has Marren's handwriting throughout—annotations, personal prayers, notes to Lliira. His voice is all over it. Tessa's isn't.
- If the paladin or Lastbloom-bearer gets close to the Book, they sense something: warmth that doesn't match Tessa. Joy that came from someone else.
- Under extreme stress, Tessa's mask slips. The maternal prophet vanishes. What's underneath is smaller, meaner, more frightened.

### Traits

**Stolen Mantle.** Tessa possesses the _Book of Exultation_, an original Lliiran holy text. While she carries it, she can cast spells as though she were a divine caster, though the source of this power is ambiguous. If separated from the Book for more than 24 hours, she loses access to her spellcasting until she recovers it.

**Mask of the Prophet.** Tessa has advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks made to appear pious, trustworthy, or spiritually authoritative. Creatures who have never seen her mask slip have disadvantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks against her.

**Desperate Conviction.** When Tessa drops below half her hit points or is publicly challenged about her legitimacy, her mask cracks. She loses the benefit of Mask of the Prophet until she finishes a long rest, and she has advantage on attack rolls but disadvantage on all Charisma checks for 1 minute.

### Spellcasting

Tessa is a 7th-level spellcaster. Her spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 15, +7 to hit with spell attacks). She has the following spells prepared:

**Cantrips (at will):** _friends_, _thaumaturgy_, _light_

**1st level (4 slots):** _command_, _charm person_, _sanctuary_

**2nd level (3 slots):** _calm emotions_, _suggestion_, _enthrall_

**3rd level (3 slots):** _beacon of hope_, _mass healing word_, _enemies abound_

**4th level (1 slot):** _compulsion_, _confusion_

### Actions

**Comforting Touch.** Melee Spell Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. _Hit:_ The target regains 11 (2d6 + 4) hit points. If Tessa chooses, she can instead make the target charmed by her until the end of its next turn (no save if the target is willing and unaware of her intentions).

**Words of the Joyous (Recharge 5–6).** Tessa speaks with borrowed conviction. Each creature of her choice within 30 feet that can hear her must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be charmed by her for 1 minute. While charmed, a creature regards Tessa as a trusted spiritual leader. A creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns or if Tessa or her allies harm it.

---

### Reactions

**Redirect Blame.** When Tessa would be targeted by an attack or harmful spell and a charmed creature is within 5 feet of her, she can use her reaction to make that creature the target instead.

### The Book of Exultation

_Wondrous item, artifact (unique)_

This hand-copied holy text of Lliira was written and annotated by Marren Brightwell over twenty years. It radiates faint warmth and smells of summer festivals and honey wine.

- A creature attuned to the Book can cast Lliiran spells (DM's discretion on list) using Charisma as their spellcasting ability.
- The Book's margins contain Marren's personal writings—prayers, doubts, reflections on joy. A careful reader (DC 14 Investigation or Religion check with an hour of study) realizes the voice in the annotations doesn't match Tessa's personality at all.
- If the Lastbloom and the Book are brought together, both resonate. The flower blooms slightly fuller. The Book's pages flutter to a specific passage: _"Joy is not owned. Joy is shared. Those who hoard it find only the memory of warmth."_

## Roleplaying Tessa

**Voice:** Warm, measured, deliberate. She speaks like she's giving a blessing even when she's asking someone to pass the salt. It's _almost_ too perfect.

**Mannerisms:** Touches people's hands and shoulders frequently. Makes extended eye contact. Always positions herself at the center of groups.

**When Challenged:** The warmth flickers. Her eyes go flat for just a moment. Then she smiles and reframes whatever was said. "I understand why you might feel that way. Grief speaks through us in strange ways."

**Breaking Point:** If directly accused of being a fraud with evidence, she doesn't crumble—she attacks. Verbally at first, twisting words, invoking guilt ("After everything I've sacrificed?"), making the accuser the villain. If that fails, she might do something desperate.

---

## Plot Hooks

- **Marren might be alive.** What if the Helmites didn't execute him? What if he's in a prison somewhere, or "reeducated," or turned informant? What happens when the real prophet meets the woman wearing his life?
- **A follower starts to see through her.** One of the 29 is smarter than Tessa thinks, or knew Marren before. They're watching. Doubting. Tessa notices them noticing.
- **The Lastbloom reacts to her.** When Tessa gets close to it, does it respond? Go dim? The flower might sense that her joy is hollow—or it might not care, because _any_ ember is better than darkness.
- **She could be redeemed.** Somewhere under the lies is the woman who loved Marren, who did keep the faith in her broken way, who isn't entirely lost. The question is whether she can face who she really is—ordinary, grieving, complicit—and find something genuine there.
