## Traits

**The Real Thing.** Unlike Tessa, Marren's divine power comes from genuine faith, not borrowed authority. His spells don't require the Book of Exultation — the Book amplifies him, but he has a direct divine connection. While he carries the Book, his spell save DC increases by 1 and his healing spells restore an additional 1d4 hit points.

**Broken But Not Gone.** Ten years of imprisonment have left their mark. Marren has disadvantage on initiative rolls and on saving throws against being stunned or incapacitated (trauma response — he freezes before he acts). However, once he takes his first action in combat, this trait ends for the remainder of the encounter. The prophet wakes up slow, but he wakes up.

**Aura of Genuine Joy (10 ft).** Marren radiates something Tessa never could — real warmth. Allies within 10 feet have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. This aura is subtle — it doesn't glow or announce itself. People near him just feel... steadier. Calmer. Like being near a warm fire in winter.

**Zealot's Focus.** Since his imprisonment, Marren's faith has narrowed to a burning point. He has advantage on saving throws against enchantment spells and effects. His mind has been tested by Accord interrogators for a decade. It doesn't bend easily anymore.

**The Voice They Remember.** When Marren reads from the Book of Exultation or speaks Lliiran prayers aloud, creatures who hear him feel the difference instinctively. Any creature that has previously heard Tessa perform the same prayers can make a DC 12 Wisdom (Insight) check — on a success, they realize Marren's version is the original. Tessa's was a copy.

## Spellcasting

Marren is a 9th-level spellcaster. His spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 15, +7 to hit with spell attacks; DC 16 and +8 while carrying the Book).

**Cantrips (at will):** _Sacred Flame_, _Spare the Dying_, _Thaumaturgy_

**1st level (4 slots):** _Bless_, _Cure Wounds_, _Guiding Bolt_, _Shield of Faith_

**2nd level (3 slots):** _Aid_, _Calm Emotions_, _Lesser Restoration_, _Prayer of Healing_

**3rd level (3 slots):** _Beacon of Hope_, _Mass Healing Word_, _Revivify_, _Spirit Guardians_

**4th level (3 slots):** _Death Ward_, _Freedom of Movement_, _Guardian of Faith_

**5th level (1 slot):** _Mass Cure Wounds_, _Greater Restoration_

_Note: Marren's spell selection reflects genuine faith — heavy on healing, protection, and restoration. No manipulation. No charm. No deception. Compare directly with Tessa's list (Command, Charm Person, Suggestion, Enthrall, Enemies Abound, Compulsion, Confusion) — the contrast tells you everything about who these two people are._

## Actions

**Quarterstaff.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 4 (1d6) bludgeoning damage. Marren is not a fighter. He will avoid melee if possible.

**Sacred Flame.** _Ranged Spell Attack:_ One creature within 60 ft. must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity save or take 13 (3d8) radiant damage. The flame is scarlet-gold — Lliira's colors.

**Lliira's Embrace (Recharge 5-6).** Marren speaks a prayer of pure, genuine compassion. Each creature of his choice within 30 feet regains 3d8 + 4 hit points and is freed from one of the following conditions: blinded, deafened, frightened, charmed, or poisoned. When he uses this ability, the air fills with the faint sound of distant music and the smell of summer — an echo of a festival that happened a long time ago.

**Martyr's Shield (2/day).** As an action, Marren can designate one creature within 30 feet. For 1 minute, whenever that creature takes damage, Marren can use his reaction to take half of that damage instead (this damage cannot be reduced or prevented). This is pure Ilmatari instinct bred into his faith — Marren will suffer for others without thinking.

---

## Reactions

**Endure For Others.** When a creature within 5 feet of Marren takes damage, he can use his reaction to grant that creature resistance to the triggering damage. Marren takes the same amount of damage (unreduced). He doesn't hesitate. He doesn't calculate. He just steps in.

## Personality

**Voice:** Quiet, rough, unhurried. He speaks like someone who's spent years with no one to talk to. His words are chosen carefully — not for effect (that's Tessa), but because he's forgotten how to be casual.

**Mannerisms:** Doesn't make eye contact easily. Touches the Book constantly — runs his fingers along the spine, the pages, like confirming it's real. Stands slightly apart from groups. Flinches at sudden movements (prison reflex).

**Faith:** Absolute. Burning. Narrowed by suffering into something diamond-hard and slightly terrifying. Marren doesn't doubt Lliira. He doesn't doubt the mission. He doubts *himself* — whether he's still worthy, whether ten years in a cell broke something essential. The Book in his hands feels like an answer he's afraid to trust.

**Toward Tessa:** Cold. Not cruel — cold. He sees what she's done. He sees the 30 people who call her "Prophet." He hasn't decided how to feel about it yet. Part of him is grateful she kept the faith alive. Part of him is furious she stole his life's work. Part of him doesn't recognize her anymore. The warmest thing he's said to her since reuniting: "You kept the Book safe. Thank you." It sounded like an accusation.

**Toward DaVonian:** This is the wound. His son is fifteen. Marren missed everything. He looks at DaVonian and sees a stranger with his wife's eyes. He wants to reach out. He doesn't know how. The longer he waits, the harder it gets.

**Toward the Party:** Watchful. Grateful they protected his wife and son, even if he can't say it well. If a PC earns his trust (through action, not words), he'll open up — and what's underneath the zealot shell is a deeply kind, deeply broken man who used to make people laugh.

---

## Combat Behavior

Marren avoids combat when possible. When forced:
1. First round: freezes (Broken But Not Gone trait) — describe the thousand-yard stare, the prison flashback
2. Once engaged: prioritizes healing and protection of allies above everything
3. Will use Martyr's Shield on whichever party member is in the most danger
4. Will use Endure For Others without hesitation — he'd rather take the hit
5. Sacred Flame and Spirit Guardians if enemies threaten the refugees
6. **Will not flee if refugees are in danger.** He'll die first. That's not bravery — it's ten years of guilt
