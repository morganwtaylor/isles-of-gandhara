
_Half-elf man, late 20s, dockworker and smuggler_

Corvin works the Kell's Landing harbor hauling cargo, mending nets, and doing whatever else pays. He's good with ropes, better with a knife, and best at not being noticed when he doesn't want to be.

**Appearance:** Lean and weathered, with sandy hair, sea-gray eyes, and a perpetual squint from years on bright water. He dresses like every other dockhand—salt-stained canvas, rope belt, bare feet or worn boots depending on the work. A thin scar runs along his left forearm (knife fight, he claims; the truth is more complicated).

**Personality:** Easy smile, easy lies, easy to underestimate. Corvin plays the cheerful nobody, the guy who knows a guy, the fellow who's just trying to get by. Underneath that, he's sharp, angry, and more committed than he lets on.

**Secret:** Corvin runs packages for the Dissenter Network—a loose smuggling operation that moves forbidden texts, hunted worshipers, and occasionally weapons through Ghandara's outer islands. He doesn't know who runs it (Mask's faithful, probably, or Tymora's). He doesn't ask. He just moves things and gets paid.

He also lost his sister to the Accord three years ago. She was a traveling musician who sang the wrong songs. He doesn't talk about it.

**When Lliira Dies:** Corvin is on the docks when the sky breaks. He watches with everyone else, but his jaw tightens where others gape. When the light fades, he spits into the harbor and mutters something under his breath. Then he notices the Helmite patrol's reaction—how some of them look pleased—and his easy smile comes back, but his eyes don't match.

Later, he'll find the players if they seem like the right sort. "Strange day. Strange days coming. You folks looking for work, or looking for trouble? Sometimes they're the same thing."

**Use Him For:**

- Hook into the resistance. Corvin can offer jobs, information, or connections to bigger players.
- Morally gray ally. He's not a hero. He'll sell out a small fish to protect a big one. But he won't betray people he respects.
- Street-level perspective. He knows how the Accord actually works in Kell's Landing—who takes bribes, which patrols are dangerous, where the gaps are.
