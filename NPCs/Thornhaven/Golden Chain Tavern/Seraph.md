## Seraph

_The Aasimar Judge_

**Race:** Aasimar (Protector) | **Age:** Unknown (appears 30s) | **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral

### Appearance

Tall and lean, with dark skin and eyes that shift between gold and white depending on the light. Silver-white hair cut short. No obvious weapons, but carries himself like someone who doesn't need them. Wears simple traveler's clothes—brown leather, grey wool—that seem deliberately plain, as if trying to downplay what he is.

It doesn't work. There's something _luminous_ about him, even when he's not trying. People notice Seraph. People remember Seraph.

### Personality

Seraph speaks rarely and chooses his words with care. When he looks at someone, he's not just seeing them—he's _assessing_ them. Weighing them. Most people find this uncomfortable. That's the point.

He's not cruel, but he's not kind either. He believes everyone carries the weight of their choices, and he's appointed himself the one who reads those weights. Whether this is a divine mandate or personal arrogance is unclear—possibly both.

### The Judgment

Seraph has the ability to sense the moral weight of a person's recent actions. Not mind-reading—he can't see specific deeds. But he can feel the shape of someone's conscience. Guilt. Pride. Grief. Conviction. He reads people like others read books.

When he "judged" Wolfric, he was doing exactly that. What he saw:

- Recent violence (Corus Thane's death)
- Genuine conviction (protecting the refugees)
- Faith in flux (the Ilmater connection strengthening)
- Something else—a potential he found interesting

He didn't condemn Wolfric. He was _curious_.

### Secret

Seraph is hunting someone. He's been tracking a specific individual across the Isles of Ghandara for months—someone who did something terrible, someone who escaped divine justice. He won't say who or what, but the hunt has brought him to Thornhaven.

He is not affiliated with the Accord. His authority comes from somewhere older.

### What He Wants

To complete his hunt. The party is a distraction—unless they become relevant. If Wolfric or the others cross paths with his quarry, Seraph will reappear. If they get in his way, he'll remove them. If they prove useful, he might offer assistance.

He's not an enemy. He's not a friend. He's a force of nature wearing a person's shape.

### Stat Hints

If needed in combat, Seraph functions as a mid-level divine warrior. Suggested base: **Aasimar Paladin 7** or **Celestial Warlock 8**. Key abilities:

- Radiant damage on attacks
- Healing Hands (Aasimar racial)
- Divine Sense or equivalent detection
- Flight (Radiant Soul, limited duration)
