
_Proprietor of the Golden Chain_

**Race:** Human | **Age:** Mid-50s | **Alignment:** Lawful Evil

### Appearance

Heavyset but not soft—the bulk of a man who was dangerous once and hasn't forgotten how. Bald head, thick grey mustache, gold rings on every finger. Always dressed well, always watching. His eyes are pale blue and unsettlingly still, like a cat deciding whether something is prey.

### Personality

Tonyult speaks softly and smiles often. Neither of these things are reassuring. He runs the Golden Chain with absolute authority and takes genuine pleasure in watching people struggle, scheme, and fail. He doesn't start trouble, but he certainly doesn't stop it—conflict is entertaining, and entertainment brings customers.

He's unfailingly polite, even when describing something horrible. "Such a shame about your friend. Would you like another drink?"

### Secret

The Golden Chain isn't just a tavern. Tonyult runs a discrete information brokerage and, when the price is right, facilitates less savory transactions. Escaped prisoners, smuggled goods, people who need to disappear—Tonyult knows everyone who passes through Thornhaven and sells that knowledge to whoever pays.

He has arrangements with both the Accord and the Dissenter networks. He's loyal to neither. He's loyal to gold.

### What He Knows

- He knew Nessandrle was more than a simple bard (hired them himself, though he won't say who paid)
- He recognized Karsa—or at least recognized _what_ Karsa is
- He knows Marren Brightwell's face from old wanted posters
- He noticed the party's reaction to the bard's magic and found it _interesting_

### What He Wants

Information about the party. Who are they? Why did they resist the enchantment? Where are they going? This is valuable, and Tonyult collects valuable things.

### After the Fight

Tonyult won't call the guards. That would be boring, and bad for business. Instead, he'll have the bodies (living or dead) removed quietly. If Karsa survived, Tonyult might offer him a drink and a conversation. If Nessandrle survived, Tonyult will want to know why they failed.

If the party returns to the Golden Chain, Tonyult will greet them like old friends. He has questions. He might even have answers—for a price.
