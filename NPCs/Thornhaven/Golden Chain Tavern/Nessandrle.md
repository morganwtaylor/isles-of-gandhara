## Nessandrle

_The Bard with the Silver Tongue_

**Race:** Half-Elf | **Age:** Late 20s | **Alignment:** Neutral Evil

### Appearance

Pretty in a deliberate, practiced way. Copper hair worn long, green eyes that crinkle when they smile, fingers callused from years of lute strings. Dresses like a traveling performer—colorful but road-worn, charming but not wealthy. The kind of person you'd invite to play at your tavern without thinking twice.

That's the point.

### Personality

Warm, engaging, instantly likeable—and none of it is real. Nessandrle is a mask worn over something cold and calculating. They've learned exactly how to make people trust them, and they use that skill like a blade.

They don't enjoy cruelty for its own sake, but they don't shy from it either. People are tools. Music is a tool. Everything is leverage.

### The Magic

Nessandrle is a College of Glamour bard with additional training in subtle enchantment. Their specialty is influence that doesn't feel like magic—suggestions that seem like the target's own ideas, emotional manipulation woven into melody.

**Key Abilities:**

- _Enthralling Performance_ (Glamour Bard feature): Charm creatures who watch them perform
- _Mantle of Inspiration_: Grant allies temporary HP and movement
- _Suggestion_, _Charm Person_, _Calm Emotions_ as subtle castings
- High Deception and Performance skills

The lute is their arcane focus. Without it, they can still cast, but their most subtle enchantments require the music.

### Secret

Nessandrle works for **The Compliance Ministry**—a covert branch of the Accord dedicated to identifying and neutralizing dissent before it becomes rebellion. They're not a soldier or an inquisitor; they're a scout. Their job is to find groups that might cause trouble and either report them or neutralize them quietly.

The ship full of Lliiran refugees? The party that killed a Helmite in Kell's Landing? That's exactly what Nessandrle was sent to find.

Their orders were to identify, evaluate, and if possible, neutralize the threat without drawing attention. The enchantment in the tavern was supposed to make the party docile, agreeable, easy to lead somewhere private for questioning—or disposal.

It didn't work.

### After the Fight

**If Nessandrle is dead:** Their body carries evidence of Accord affiliation (a small token, coded documents, a sending stone). This could be valuable information—or dangerous to possess.

**If Nessandrle is alive:** They're a professional. They won't break easily, but they will negotiate. They know things: Accord movements, Ministry operations, who else might be hunting the party. The question is whether the party can trust anything they say.
