
_The Unfriendly Goblin_

**Race:** Goblin | **Age:** 40s (old for a goblin) | **Alignment:** Neutral

### Appearance

Small even for a goblin, with mottled green-grey skin and a face like a clenched fist. Missing two fingers on his left hand (old injury, doesn't talk about it). Wears a patched leather coat with too many pockets, all of them full of something. Sits hunched over his drink like he's guarding it from thieves.

### Personality

Grissel doesn't like people. He doesn't like talking. He doesn't like questions, strangers, noise, or optimism. He especially doesn't like adventurers—in his experience, they break things and don't pay for the damage.

His default response to conversation is a glare. His default response to persistent conversation is leaving. He's not hostile, just deeply, profoundly antisocial.

Underneath the hostility is exhaustion. Grissel has seen too much and lost too much to have patience left for small talk.

### Secret

Grissel is a retired smuggler who used to work the Dissenter networks. He moved people—refugees, escaped prisoners, anyone the Accord wanted found. He was good at it. Then a run went bad, people died, and Grissel decided he was done.

He lives in Thornhaven now because it's quiet and no one asks questions. He drinks at the Golden Chain because Tonyult leaves him alone.

He recognized Marren Brightwell when the ship came in. He hasn't decided what to do about that yet.

### What He Knows

- The smuggling routes through the Isles (outdated but still partially accurate)
- Who the major players in the Dissenter networks used to be
- That Nessandrle wasn't just a bard (he's seen magical manipulation before)
- That Seraph is dangerous (he has good instincts for predators)

### What He Wants

To be left alone. That's it. That's the whole list.

But if someone needed to move people quietly, and if they had coin, and if they didn't ask him to care about it... Grissel might remember some useful things. Might know someone who knows someone. Might be convinced to do one last job.

He'd hate every minute of it. But he'd do it right.

### Potential Hook

If the party needs to get 30 refugees somewhere safe and can't use official channels, Grissel is exactly the kind of contact they need. Getting him to help is the challenge—he'll need convincing that this isn't going to get him killed, and he'll need to believe the party isn't going to get everyone else killed either.

Approaching him with respect, not pushing when he says no, and offering fair payment will go further than charm or intimidation.
