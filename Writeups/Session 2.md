# The Road to Ruin

*Session Two*

-----

*The city of Kell’s Landing was no longer safe. Perhaps it never had been.*

## A Shadow in the Dark

While the party prepared to flee aboard Tessa’s ship, another soul moved through the city. **Paul** slipped between shadows, navigating the tightening grip of Helmite patrols with practiced ease. Whatever brought him to Kell’s Landing, whatever he was running from—or toward—his path led him to the docks.

To the party he would travel with.

## Departure

Guard ships prowled the harbor mouth, stopping every vessel attempting to leave. Even though it was late the party moved to leave. When they boarded Tessa’s ship, the party held their breath.

Tessa Brightwell stood before the inspectors, calm and certain. Papers were produced. Questions were answered. Whatever suspicions the guards harbored, Tessa’s steady presence convinced them that everything was in order.

The ship sailed free.

Behind them, Kell’s Landing shrank against the horizon. Ahead lay open water.

They were sailing to Joybright.

## Days at Sea

Six days of salt wind and rolling waves. Six days to breathe. Six days to learn the names and faces of the thirty souls they’d saved.

The ship became a small world unto itself. Refugees shared stories of what they’d lost and what they still hoped to find. The crew worked the rigging and watched the horizon. Life, despite everything, continued.

Wolfric and Murdock found time for prayer. In quiet moments, they knelt and spoke to Ilmater—the Crying God, the One Who Endures. And something answered. Not words, but warmth. Not power, but *presence*. The god of suffering had not abandoned those who bore it in his name.

They rose from their prayers feeling… more. Empowered. Ready.

## Thornhaven

On the sixth day, the ship made port at **Thornhaven**, a small working town clinging to the edge of the highland planes, south of the capital Veltharion. Not a destination, just a stop. Supplies, news, solid ground beneath their feet for a few hours.

At the docks, two figures waited.

The first was impossible to miss: **Karsa**, a soldier built like a siege weapon, tall and scarred and radiating the kind of quiet menace that made crowds part around him.

The second was a ghost.

**Marren Brightwell** stood beside the warrior, older than the stories suggested, worn by years of imprisonment or hiding or both. When he saw the ship—when he learned of Tessa he ran. For a reunion our party missed.

## The Golden Chain

The party ventured into town, seeking food and drink and perhaps a moment of normalcy at the local tavern: **The Golden Chain**. Impossibly nice for this city.

Inside, they found:

A bartender, willing to take gold from fools.

A goblin, even more willing to talk.

And across from the goblin, an aasimar named **Seraph**—golden-eyed, divine-touched, and apparently in the business of passing judgment on strangers.

Seraph turned those gleaming eyes on Wolfric. Measured him. Found him… something. The assessment sparked tension, and Wolfric wasn’t the sort to let a stranger’s judgment pass unchallenged. Words were exchanged.

But the real danger wasn’t the aasimar.

It was the music.

## The Bard’s Web

A bard played in the corner. **Nessandrle**, fingers dancing across lute strings, voice weaving through the common room like smoke. Pleasant. Soothing. *Wrong*.

The music crept into their minds. Suggestions that felt like their own thoughts. Impulses that seemed perfectly reasonable. The party didn’t notice at first—that was the point.

Murdock noticed.

Something in the melody set his instincts screaming. Without hesitation, he grabbed the lute and *ran*.

The spell shattered. The party blinked, suddenly aware of how close they’d come to letting their guard down.

They didn’t stay to find out. They ran.

## Blood in the Back Room

But Karsa didn’t run.

The warrior had his own business with the bard, and it wasn’t the kind that ended with conversation. While the party fled through Thornhaven’s streets, Karsa struck and injured Nessandrle  before he ran disappeared into a back office. Several injuries of our party occurred as this was no weak bard.

**Tonyult**, proprietor of the Golden Chain, watched from his chair in the back office. **Karsa** entered chasing the bard. **Tonyult** made no move to stop the violence. If anything, he seemed… curious. Entertained.

Steel rang. Blood spilled.

When the sounds stopped, both Karsa and Nessandrle lay still. 

The world went quiet for **Karsa**… 

## Scattered

The party races through darkened streets, separated, uncertain. The ship waits at the docks. Tessa and Marren are there—but so are questions.

Who was Nessandrle?

 Who are the Tryanns? And what is Tyrs court?

What was Karsa’s connection to Marren?

Is Karsa dead? Is the bard?

And what did Tonyult see in that back room that made him smile?

-----

*The road to Joybright is littered with the broken and the lost. The party has joined their number—willing or not.*
