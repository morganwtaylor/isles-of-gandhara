# Isles of Ghandara — Campaign Bible v0.1

## The Premise

Forty years ago, the gods imposed the Divine Accord—a coalition of deities who declared mortal autonomy a threat to cosmic balance. What began as "guidance" became control. Temples are seats of power. Divine law supersedes mortal law. The faithful are rewarded, the defiant are broken.

Not all gods agreed. Lliira, goddess of joy and freedom, spoke openly against the Accord. She was executed publicly twenty-three years ago in the skies above the city of Joybright—her greatest holy city, now renamed Accordfall. The message was clear: even gods kneel or die.

The Isles of Ghandara exist at the edge of the Accord's reach—a scattered archipelago where enforcement is inconsistent, smugglers run dissenter networks, and the old ways linger in hidden shrines. But the Orthodoxy's grip is tightening.

---

## The Divine Accord (Oppressors)

**The Architects** — True believers who shaped the Accord

- **Bane** — The Accord's iron voice. Tyranny made righteous.
- **Helm** — The dutiful hand. Believes mortals must be protected from themselves.
- **Tyr** — The legal framework. Convinced through cosmic jurisprudence that the Accord is lawful.

**The Opportunists** — Gods exploiting the new order

- **Shar** — Cultivates despair in the Accord's shadow.
- **Loviatar** — Pain is more exquisite when it's sanctioned.
- **Cyric** — Lies flow easier when truth is regulated.

**The Complicit** — Gods who fell in line

- **Sune** — Beauty requires order, she tells herself. Her temples feel hollow.
- **Lathander** — The Morninglord has dimmed. Rumors persist he was forced to witness—or participate in—Lliira's execution.
- **Eldath** — Peace at any cost. She weeps but does not act.

---

## The Dissenters

**Active Resistance**

- **Tymora** — Lliira was her closest friend. She's furious, reckless, and funding mortal rebellions through luck and happenstance.
- **Mask** — God of thieves. Authority is meant to be undermined. Runs smuggler networks and spy cells.
- **Eilistraee** — Already outcast, already fighting. Her faithful move through the underdark and surface both.

**Quiet Opposition**

- **Selûne** — Goddess of change. This stagnation is an abomination. Works in cycles, in patience.
- **Ilmater** — Will not cause suffering. Accepts his own punishment rather than enforce.

**Wild Cards**

- **Kelemvor** — Neutral by duty, but Lliira's soul passed through his realm. Something changed in him.
- **Mystra** — Magic is meant to be free. Is she subtly seeding mortals with power?

## The Execution of Lliira

he sky above Joybright became a stage.

- **Bane** pronounced sentence for sedition against divine order
- **Helm** restrained her—duty over love, a choice that haunts him
- **Loviatar** administered the killing blow, slowly
- **Lathander** was present—witnesses disagree whether he watched in chains or stood freely among the executioners

Lliira did not beg. Some say she laughed. Some say she wept for those watching. Her divine spark was extinguished. Her body became light, then ash, then nothing.

Joy has not felt the same since. Festivals exist but ring hollow. Laughter in public draws glances. The color scarlet—her sacred hue—is quietly forbidden.

## The Isles of Ghandara

An archipelago of seven major islands and dozens of minor ones, weeks by ship from the Sword Coast. Historically independent, now nominally under the Accord.

**Characteristics**

- Maritime culture—fishing, trade, piracy, smuggling
- Diverse population—humans, half-elves, halflings, tieflings, genasi common
- Syncretic religious traditions that predate the Accord
- The Accord's enforcers (Helmite paladins, Tyrran judges, Banite inquisitors) arrived fifteen years ago but remain concentrated in major ports
- The outer islands and open sea remain relatively free—for now

**Key Locations (Detail as Needed)**

_Veltharion_ — Largest island, seat of Accord authority. The High Citadel of Helm overlooks the capital city of Accord's Rest (formerly Velthar).

_The Shattered Isle_ — Volcanic, dangerous, largely lawless. Smugglers and dissenters operate openly.

_Miravel_ — Fertile, pastoral. A Sunite temple here has become strangely influential. Something is off.

_The Drowned Teeth_ — Reef-riddled islands where ships vanish. Rumors of a sea-god who never signed the Accord.

_Joybright Reef_ — All that remains of a small island that sank the night Lliira died. Pilgrims still visit in secret.

## Paladins in This World

Paladins are the Accord's most visible enforcers—knights-inquisitor who hunt dissenters, unsanctioned divine casters, and heretics. They are respected, feared, and given broad authority.

**Helmite Paladins** — The Watchers. Professional, disciplined, rarely cruel but utterly uncompromising.

**Tyrran Paladins** — The Scales. Judges and executioners. They believe in process—trials, evidence, sentencing—but the laws they enforce are unjust.

**Banite Paladins** — The Black Fists. Enforcers who relish authority. Less common in Ghandara but their presence is growing.

**For Your Paladin Player**

- Are they one of the above, experiencing doubt?
- A secret dissenter wearing the mask of an enforcer?
- Oath-bound to a dead goddess? (Oath to Lliira's memory—mechanically Devotion or Redemption, flavored as heresy)
- Something else entirely?

## Session 1 — Starting Small

The players begin in **Kell's Landing**, a mid-sized port town on the island of Veltharion. Not the capital, but not the frontier either. The Accord has presence here—a Helmite chapterhouse, a Tyrran courthouse—but life mostly goes on.

**Tone**: Normal life under occupation. People work, drink, worship. But there are small signs—a tavern that used to host festivals now feels muted, an old woman clutching a faded scarlet ribbon, a street preacher praising Helm a little too loudly.

**Opening Hook Options**

_The Job_ — Simple work. Escort cargo, investigate missing fishermen, clear out a cave. Something that establishes the party and lets you seed details about the world.

_The Witness_ — One of the players sees something they shouldn't. A secret Lliiran shrine. An Accord officer taking a bribe. A dead body with a forbidden symbol carved into it.

_The Arrival_ — A new Banite inquisitor has arrived in town. Everyone's on edge. Something's about to break.

**Building Toward the Inciting Incident**

The "god-killing kickoff" works best if the players have had a session or two to settle in. Let them feel the world, meet NPCs, develop opinions. Then drop the hammer:

A dissenter god is killed—or a major divine servant is publicly executed—or one of the party witnesses a divine manifestation that changes everything.

Who dies to kick things off? Some options:

- A mortal saint or chosen of Tymora, killed in Ghandara as a message
- Ilmater, finally pushed too far and martyred
- A demigod or divine scion the party has come to know
- An avatar of a dissenter god, struck down in front of them
