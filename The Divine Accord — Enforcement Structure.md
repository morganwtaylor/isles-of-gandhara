
## Overview

The Divine Accord maintains control through a tripartite enforcement system, each branch reflecting the nature of its patron deity. Where Helmites patrol, Tyrrans judge, and Banites terrify. The further one travels toward Accord's Rest, the tighter and more overlapping this grip becomes.

---

## The Three Pillars of Enforcement

### Helmites — The Watchers

_"The Watcher's eyes never close."_

**Role:** Patrol, arrest, protection, visible presence

**Function:** The Helmites are the Accord's most common face—armored figures at checkpoints, patrols walking in pairs, guards standing eternal vigil at doors. They maintain order through presence and, when necessary, force. They do not judge; they apprehend. They do not sentence; they deliver.

**Ranks:**

|Rank|Role|Authority|
|---|---|---|
|**Watcher**|Rank-and-file|Patrols, checkpoints, guard duty|
|**Vigilant**|Squad leader|Arrests, shrine raids, investigations|
|**Ever-Seeing**|Commander|Chapterhouse authority, regional coordination|
|**The Lidless**|Inner circle|Doctrine, divine interpretation|
|**High Watcher**|Supreme authority|Currently Varanthas Morn|

**Day-to-Day Duties:**

- Street patrols (always in pairs, always visible)
- Checkpoint enforcement (harbor, gates, district boundaries)
- Contraband inspection (Lliiran symbols, forbidden texts, certain songs)
- Warrant execution (arrest and detention, not trial)
- Protection details (officials, faithful, shipments)
- Standing watch (shrines, courts, government buildings)
- Dissenter suppression (raids, confiscation, arrests)

**Presence in Ghandara:** Chapterhouses in every significant port. The Unsleeping Bastion in Kell's Landing houses approximately forty Helmites under Commander Serelith Vane.

---

### Tyrrans — The Scales

_"Justice is blind, but the law sees all."_

**Role:** Courts, prosecution, legal authority, sentencing

**Function:** Where Helmites arrest, Tyrrans convict. They provide the Accord's veneer of legitimacy—trials, evidence, due process. The laws they enforce are unjust, but they are _laws_, properly codified and procedurally applied. This makes the oppression feel orderly, even reasonable.

**Ranks:**

|Rank|Role|Authority|
|---|---|---|
|**Advocate**|Legal clerk|Documentation, warrants, minor proceedings|
|**Justicar**|Local judge|Minor infractions (contraband, curfew, unlicensed worship)|
|**Magister**|Regional judge|Serious cases (sedition, harboring dissenters, heresy)|
|**High Arbiter**|Appellate judge|Appeals, precedent, policy interpretation|
|**The Blind Council**|Supreme judiciary|Final authority on Accord law|

**Day-to-Day Duties:**

- Issuing warrants (Helmites execute, Tyrrans authorize)
- Conducting trials (formal proceedings, witnesses, evidence)
- Pronouncing sentences (fines, imprisonment, execution, exile)
- Maintaining legal records (who was charged, convicted, punished)
- Interpreting Accord law (what counts as heresy, sedition, etc.)
- Advising Helmite commanders on legal boundaries

**Presence in Ghandara:** Courthouses in major settlements. In Kell's Landing, the Hall of Balanced Scales handles local matters. Complex cases are referred to Accord's Rest.

**Tension with Helmites:** Tyrrans view Helmites as blunt instruments who sometimes overstep. Helmites view Tyrrans as slow and procedural while dissenters escape. Both resent Banites.

---

### Banites — The Black Fists

_"Obedience through strength. Order through fear."_

**Role:** Inquisition, terror, special operations, breaking resistance

**Function:** The Accord's teeth. Where Helmites maintain and Tyrrans legitimize, Banites _crush_. They are called in when dissent persists, when cells prove stubborn, when an example must be made. Their authority can supersede local Helmite and Tyrran leadership. Their presence means escalation.

**Ranks:**

|Rank|Role|Authority|
|---|---|---|
|**Fist**|Enforcer|Raids, interrogations, "examples"|
|**Dread Knight**|Paladin-elite|Hunting dissenter cells, field command|
|**Thought-Taker**|Specialist cleric|Mental intrusion, confession extraction|
|**Autarch**|Regional inquisitor|Override authority over local enforcement|
|**The Iron Circle**|Inner priesthood|Direct connection to Bane's will|

**Day-to-Day Duties:**

- Interrogation (methods that Helmites won't use)
- Hunting dissenter networks (infiltration, tracking, elimination)
- Making examples (public punishment, strategic cruelty)
- Overriding local authority (when results matter more than process)
- Advising on "difficult" prisoners (those who won't break)
- Maintaining the Black Threshold (fortress-prison in Accord's Rest)

**Presence in Ghandara:** Minimal in outer regions—perhaps a single "advisor" attached to a Helmite chapterhouse, watching, waiting. Growing presence in major ports. In Accord's Rest, they maintain the Black Threshold, where prisoners of particular interest are taken. Few return.

**Relationship to Others:** Helmites distrust them (too cruel, too willing to bypass procedure). Tyrrans resent them (they make the law look like a joke). Everyone fears them. That's the point.

## The Shadow Enforcers — Opportunist Gods

The Architects built the Accord's machinery. The Opportunists oil it with darker tools.

### Sharrans — The Whispering Dark

_Shar, Mistress of the Night_

**Role:** Informants, despair cultivation, secret police

**Function:** Sharrans don't wear uniforms. They listen in taverns. They befriend the grieving. They note who sighs too heavily when the Helmites pass. They cultivate despair—not to break people, but to make them brittle. Brittle people betray their friends.

**Methods:**

- Embedded informants in communities
- "Grief counselors" who report dissent
- Cultivating hopelessness to weaken resistance
- Identifying who still has enough spirit to be dangerous

---

### Loviatans — The Pain Artists

_Loviatar, Maiden of Pain_

**Role:** Specialist interrogators, torture, breaking the stubborn

**Function:** When Banite methods prove insufficient, when a prisoner holds something truly valuable, the Loviatans are called. They are artists. They take their time. They administered Lliira's execution—slowly—and they remember it as their finest work.

**Methods:**

- "Consultation" with difficult prisoners
- Extended interrogation (days, weeks if needed)
- Breaking symbols of resistance (making martyrs scream)
- Training Banite interrogators in refined techniques

---

### Cyricans — The Lie Weavers

_Cyric, Prince of Lies_

**Role:** Disinformation, entrapment, agents provocateur

**Function:** Truth is dangerous to the Accord. Cyricans ensure that truth is hard to find, that lies spread faster, that dissenters are drawn into traps of their own making. They pose as fellow rebels, spread false rumors of safe houses, and ensure that trust is a liability.

**Methods:**

- Agents provocateur (pretending to be dissenters to identify real ones)
- Disinformation (false rumors about dissenter movements)
- Document forgery (creating evidence of crimes)
- Sowing distrust within resistance networks

## Enforcement Gradient by Region

|Distance from Capital|Primary Presence|Secondary|Feel|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Outer Islands / Frontier**|Minimal Helmite outpost|None|Relative freedom, smuggler networks thrive|
|**Minor Ports**|Small Helmite garrison|Visiting Justicar|Light occupation, careful compliance|
|**Mid-Tier Ports** (Kell's Landing)|Helmite chapterhouse|Tyrran courthouse, occasional Banite advisor|Occupation with civil face|
|**Major Cities** (Veltharion coast)|All three in force|Competing jurisdictions, Sharran informants|Bureaucratic oppression, constant surveillance|
|**Accord's Rest** (Capital)|Helmite Citadel, Tyrran High Courts, Banite Fortress|Full shadow network|Totalitarian theocracy, no space to breathe|

## The Complicit Gods' Faithful

Not all who serve Accord-aligned gods are enforcers. Some simply... comply.

### Sunites — Beauty in Chains

_Sune, Lady Firehair_

The temples still stand. The services still occur. But something is hollow. Sunite priests preach that beauty requires order, that chaos mars perfection. They host approved gatherings—elegant, restrained, joyless. Some truly believe. Others are trying to survive. A few remember when beauty meant freedom, and weep in private.

### Lathanderites — The Dimmed Dawn

_Lathander, the Morninglord_

The Morninglord's temples feel like they're waiting for something. The dawn services are muted. The priests speak of renewal and hope, but their eyes say otherwise. Rumors persist that Lathander was present at Lliira's execution—whether as witness or participant, no one knows. His faithful don't ask. They're afraid of the answer.

### Eldathans — Peace at Any Cost

_Eldath, the Quiet One_

Eldath's followers preach peace, acceptance, non-resistance. They tend gardens. They heal wounds. They do not fight. They believe—or tell themselves—that compliance prevents greater suffering. Some serve as mediators between citizens and enforcers. Some provide comfort to those the Accord has broken. They call it mercy. Dissenters call it collaboration.

## The Banner of the Divine Accord

**Design:** A vertical tricolor of black, steel gray, and deep crimson. In the center, three interlocking symbols arranged in a triangle: Bane's black hand (authority), Helm's staring eye (vigilance), and Tyr's balanced scales (law). The symbols are rendered in gold, connected by a thin golden chain.

**Symbolism:**

- **Black** — Bane's dominion, the iron foundation of order
- **Steel gray** — Helm's unwavering duty, the shield against chaos
- **Crimson** — Tyr's justice (officially); dissenters whisper it represents divine blood
- **The golden chain** — Unity of purpose, divine authority bound together

**Usage:** Flown above courthouses, chapterhouses, and government buildings. Accord officers wear a smaller version as a badge or tabard. Ships operating under Accord authority fly it from the mainmast.

**Folk Names:** "The Three-Eyed Banner" or, bitterly in private, "The Chains"

## Using This in Play

**Escalation:** When players cause trouble, escalate through the hierarchy. First Helmite attention, then Tyrran warrants, then Banite interest. Each step should feel heavier.

**Jurisdictional Friction:** Helmites, Tyrrans, and Banites don't always agree. Players can exploit gaps, play factions against each other, or find sympathizers within each.

**The Human Element:** Not every enforcer is a true believer. Brother Fell tends the shrine and remembers better days. A Justicar might be disgusted by what she's become. A Banite Fist might be in too deep to see a way out. These cracks are where stories happen.

**The Shadow:** Sharrans, Loviatans, and Cyricans should be felt before they're seen. Rumors. Disappearances. The sense that someone is always listening. Use them sparingly but memorably.
