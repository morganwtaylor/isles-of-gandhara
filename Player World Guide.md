# The Way Things Are

Forty years ago, the gods changed the rules.

Three deities—Bane, Helm, and Tyr—formed the **Divine Accord**, a coalition that declared mortal autonomy a threat to cosmic balance. What started as "guidance" became law. What started as law became control. Temples became seats of government. Divine decree superseded mortal choice. The faithful were rewarded. The defiant were broken.

Not every god agreed. Most fell in line anyway. A few resisted. One refused loudly enough that the Accord made an example of her.

Session 1, **Lliira**—goddess of joy and freedom—was executed in the skies above her holy city of **Joybright**. Bane pronounced sentence. Helm held her still. Loviatar delivered the killing blow. Slowly.

The message was clear: even gods kneel or die.


---

# The Isles

The Isles of Ghandara are an archipelago far from the Sword Coast, months by ship. 3 major islands and dozens of minor ones. Maritime culture, diverse peoples, and religious traditions older than anything on the mainland.

The Accord's started here. In defiance of the large following of Lliraa followers. The Gods despised Joybright and the pilgrimage that happened from around the world.

## The Major Islands

**Ghandara** — The largest island. Seat of Accord authority. Home to the capital, the ruins of Joybright, highland clans, and most of the population. This is where the campaign takes place.

**Dunmara** — Southwestern archipelago. Rocky, wind-scoured, stubbornly traditional. The clans here signed oaths to the Accord but interpret them creatively.

**Skerrad** (The Shattered Isle) — Southeastern volcanic island. Dangerous, largely lawless. Smugglers and dissenters operate openly.

---

# Ghandara — The Main Island

A landmass split by a great inland sea called the **Mirthwater**. Highland mountains, dense forests, fertile vales, and a coastline dotted with ports. Approximately 185,000 people live here under varying degrees of Accord control.

## The Regions

### The Northern Reaches

Cold, remote, frontier territory. Dense forests, rocky coasts, long winters. The Accord's presence is thin—a watchtower here, a waystation there.

**Key settlements:** Northern Rest (fishing town, end of the world), Springvale (the "loyal" vale, checkpoint on the northern road), Frostholm (remote, practically ungoverned).

---

### The Briarholt Expanse

Central-eastern forests dominated by the Thornwood. The logging industry makes this region vital—thornwood timber builds ships across the Isles. Merchant guilds hold real power here.

**Key settlements:** Briarholt (second city, ~12,000, logging capital), Death's Edge (mining town, something the Accord is interested in), Millford (timber processing), Verdant Crossing (trading post).

---

### The Highland Clans

Mountain territory where old ways persist. The Geast Range and its people have never been truly conquered. The clans signed oaths to the Accord but govern themselves in practice.

**Key settlements:** Duncraig (Clan Mòr's seat, closest thing to a highland capital), Stonewatch (guards the main pass), Eaglerest (never acknowledged the Accord at all).

**The major clans:** Clan Mòr (largest, nominally compliant), Clan Varn (gatekeepers), Clan Fenn (isolated bog-dwellers), Clan Aeris (openly defiant), Clan Dunne (broken, scattered, remembering).

---

### The Accord's Heart

The capital region. Prosperous, orderly, thoroughly controlled.

**Key settlements:** Accord's Rest (~45,000, the capital, formerly Mirahaven), Helmgate (fortified checkpoint), Piety's Rest (monastery-town, "re-education" facilities), Goldfield (agricultural estates).

**Accord's Rest** is dominated by the **High Citadel of Helm**, the **Hall of Scales** (Tyrran courts), and the growing **Black Fist Garrison** (Banite). Beautiful city. Hollow festivals. Fear dressed as piety.

---

### The Four Vales

Fertile lowlands. Agricultural heartland. Once there were four vales—Springvale, Summervale, Autumnvale, and Wintervale.

**Key settlements:** Summervale (~5,000, farming community), Autumnvale (~6,000, Ilmatari church still operates), Harvestholm (market town).

_"Spring and Summer, Autumn's gold / Winter's tale is never told."_

# The Powers That Be

## The Divine Accord

Three gods built it. The rest fell in line or paid the price.

**Bane** — God of tyranny. The Accord's architect. Believes absolute authority is the only path to order. His faithful—the **Black Fists**—serve as inquisitors and enforcers. Their presence in Ghandara is small but growing.

**Helm** — God of protection. The Accord's dutiful hand. Believes mortals need to be protected from themselves. His faithful—the **Helmites**—are the most common Accord presence: patrols, checkpoints, chapterhouses. Professional, disciplined, rarely cruel, utterly uncompromising.

**Tyr** — God of justice. The Accord's legal framework. His faithful—the **Tyrrans**—run the courts. Trials are procedurally correct. The laws they enforce are not.

---

## How Enforcement Works

**Helmites** are the visible hand. They patrol, inspect, arrest. They're the armored figures at checkpoints and on street corners. Most genuinely believe they're helping people.

**Tyrrans** are the legal machine. They try cases, pass sentences, provide legitimacy. The process is fair. The outcome is usually predetermined—the laws criminalize any deviation from approved worship.

**Banites** are the iron fist. They arrive when dissent persists. They don't patrol or judge—they hunt. Their authority can override local Helmite and Tyrran leadership. When Banites show up, things escalate.

**Sentencing for crimes against the Accord:**

- Unlicensed worship → Fines, confiscation, mandatory approved services
- Forbidden materials → Months to years imprisonment
- Active dissent → Extended imprisonment, forced labor, family separation
- Sedition → Public trial, execution or life imprisonment
- Heresy → Death

## The Other Gods

**The Complicit** — Gods who fell in line. Sune (beauty requires order, she tells herself). Lathander (the Morninglord has dimmed since the execution). Eldath (peace at any cost).

**The Opportunists** — Gods exploiting the new order from the shadows. Shar (cultivates despair). Loviatar (pain is more exquisite when it's sanctioned). Cyric (lies flow easier when truth is regulated).

**The Dissenters** — Gods who resist, openly or quietly.

- **Tymora** — Lliira's closest friend. Furious. Funds mortal rebellions through luck and "coincidence."
- **Mask** — God of thieves. Runs smuggler networks and spy cells. Authority is meant to be undermined.
- **Selûne** — Works in cycles, in patience. Change is coming.
- **Eilistraee** — Already outcast, already fighting.
- **Ilmater** — Will not cause suffering. Accepts his own punishment rather than enforce. His temples remain open because the Accord considers his faithful harmless.

# What Life Is Like

## Under the Accord

Most people comply. Not because they believe, but because the alternative is worse. You attend the approved services. You display the right symbols. You don't hum the wrong songs. You don't wear scarlet.

In the cities, Helmite patrols walk in pairs. Checkpoints control movement. Contraband lists grow longer every year. In the vales, Tyrran tax collectors count every bushel. In the highlands, the clans do what they've always done and dare the Accord to stop them.

There are still good days. People still fall in love, raise children, share meals, tell jokes. But there's a ceiling on happiness now—an unspoken understanding that too much joy draws attention. Festivals are approved, scheduled, supervised. Laughter is fine. Laughter that sounds like freedom is not.

## The Forbidden

Things the Accord has banned or restricted:

- Scarlet ribbons, flowers, or cloth (Lliiran association)
- Unauthorized holy symbols (any dissenter god)
- The _Book of Exultation_ (Lliira's holy text)
- Certain songs (the list grows regularly)
- Dancing masks (traditional Lliiran festival gear)
- Any depiction of Lliira herself
- Unlicensed worship of any kind
- Gathering in groups for unapproved religious purposes

## The Resistance

It exists. Scattered, careful, and always in danger. Smugglers move people and contraband through forest paths. Gamblers fund operations through divine luck. Thieves run spy networks in the undercities. And in quiet temples to the Suffering God, priests preach endurance and mean something more than patience.

Nobody talks about the resistance openly. But if you know whom to ask, and you ask the right way, doors open that the Accord doesn't know about.

**Seraph** — Aasimar encountered at the Golden Chain tavern in Thornhaven. Divine-touched, golden-eyed, and apparently in the business of judging strangers. Not Accord. Something else.

There's more going on than the Accord wants you to see. Something survived in Joybright. Something stirs in the highlands. Something old sleeps beneath Death's Edge. And somewhere, in a cracked cobblestone or a faded ribbon or a prayer whispered in the dark, joy refuses to die.
