**The Book of Exultation**

_Wondrous item, artifact (unique)_

An original holy text of Lliira, goddess of joy, written in her divine hand — or through direct divine inspiration channeled through her most faithful. The Book is a physical vessel of Lliira's teachings, prayers, hymns, and philosophy of joy as sacred resistance. It predates the Divine Accord by centuries.

This particular copy was possessed by **Marren Brightwell**, a genuine Lliiran lay priest with a true calling, who annotated it extensively over twenty years with his own personal prayers, doubts, reflections, and notes to Lliira. Marren's handwriting fills the margins — intimate, earnest, unmistakably his voice.

When Marren was arrested by Helmites ten years ago for possessing the Book, his wife **Tessa** found it hidden in a false panel he had shown only her. She kept it. Read it. Learned from it. And gradually used it to build a false identity as a Lliiran prophet, co-opting Marren's words, prayers, and spiritual authority as her own.

**Tessa currently possesses the Book.** Marren does not yet know the full extent of how she has used it.

---

## Appearance

A hand-bound volume, worn but not deteriorating. The cover is soft leather dyed deep scarlet — Lliira's sacred color — now faded to a warm rust. The pages are thick vellum, slightly yellowed, filled with flowing script in two distinct hands:

- **The original text:** Elegant, rhythmic, written with a joy that bleeds through the ink. Reading it aloud, even silently, evokes warmth. This is the divine voice — Lliira's teachings captured in mortal language
- **Marren's annotations:** Smaller, messier, crowded into margins and between lines. Personal prayers ("Joybringer, I don't know if I'm doing this right"), theological questions, notes on passages that moved him, arguments with the text itself. His voice is warm, uncertain, deeply human

The Book smells faintly of summer festivals and honey wine. It radiates warmth to the touch, like something alive.

## Properties

**Divine Source.** The Book of Exultation is a genuine artifact of Lliira. A creature attuned to the Book can cast Lliiran-associated spells using Charisma as their spellcasting ability. The extent of this power is tied to the wielder's connection to joy — not performance of joy, but genuine experience of it.

**Stolen Mantle.** Tessa Brightwell has used the Book as her spellcasting source for years. While she carries it, she functions as a 7th-level divine spellcaster (see Tessa's stat block). If separated from the Book for more than 24 hours, she loses access to her spellcasting until she recovers it. The open question: does the Book empower her because of Lliira's residual divinity, because of the Book's own nature, or because Tessa has convinced herself so thoroughly that belief itself generates power?

**The True Voice.** A careful reader who spends at least 1 hour studying the Book (DC 14 Investigation or Religion check) realizes the annotations don't match Tessa's personality. The voice in the margins is warmer, less polished, more uncertain — it's someone else's spiritual journey. A second check (DC 16) or comparison with hearing Marren speak confirms: these are Marren's words. The Book is saturated with his faith, not Tessa's.

**Joy Resonance.** The Book responds to genuine joy in its vicinity. In moments of real laughter, celebration, or hope, the pages seem to glow faintly at the edges. In moments of despair or cruelty, the warmth fades and the Book feels like ordinary leather and paper.

**Lliiran Spells (DM's Discretion):**
The Book contains prayers and rituals that function as the following spells when cast by an attuned wielder:
- _Cantrips:_ Friends, Thaumaturgy, Light
- _1st level:_ Command, Charm Person, Sanctuary, Bless
- _2nd level:_ Calm Emotions, Suggestion, Enthrall, Aid
- _3rd level:_ Beacon of Hope, Mass Healing Word, Enemies Abound, Hypnotic Pattern
- _4th level:_ Compulsion, Confusion, Freedom of Movement
- _Higher level (locked):_ The Book contains passages that seem to describe more powerful rituals — large-scale celebrations, mass blessings, something called "The Last Dance." These are inaccessible without deeper understanding of Lliira's faith or a stronger connection to her divine remnant

## Interaction with Other Artifacts

**The Lastbloom + The Book of Exultation:**
When brought within 30 feet of each other, both resonate. The Lastbloom blooms slightly fuller, its scarlet deepening. The Book's pages flutter on their own to a specific passage:

> _"Joy is not owned. Joy is shared. Those who hoard it find only the memory of warmth."_

This passage is in the original divine hand, not Marren's annotation. It is a direct message — perhaps to Tessa, perhaps to whoever reads it. The implications are devastating for anyone who has been hoarding the Book's power for themselves.

If both artifacts are brought to **Joybright**, something unprecedented may happen. The Book is Lliira's voice. The Lastbloom is Lliira's ember. Joybright is where she died. Three fragments of a dead goddess, converging at the site of her death — this is endgame territory.

## Plot Significance

### The Fraud
The Book is the physical evidence of Tessa's stolen identity. Marren's voice is unmistakably present throughout. Anyone who knew Marren — or who hears him speak and then reads the annotations — will recognize the truth. Tessa's authority is built on this Book, and the Book itself is the proof that her authority is borrowed.

### The Power Question
Where does Tessa's divine magic actually come from? Options:
- **The Book itself** — Lliira's artifact empowers whoever holds it, regardless of worthiness. This makes the Book dangerous — anyone could wield it
- **Residual faith** — Tessa has spent so long performing Marren's devotion that she's generated a kind of secondhand faith. Real enough to channel, hollow enough to crack
- **The Lastbloom's ember** — If Lliira's seed of power (the Lastbloom) is still generating divine energy, the Book might be drawing on that remotely, using Tessa as an unwitting conduit
- **Something darker** — Shar, goddess of loss and despair, feeds on hollow faith. Has she been empowering Tessa without Tessa knowing, nurturing a fraud that will eventually shatter the refugees' hope?

### The Transfer
What happens when Marren holds the Book again? Does its power surge — the real priest reunited with the real text? Do spells work differently? Does the Book *feel* different in his hands versus Tessa's?

What happens if neither of them hold it — if a PC attunes to it? Does the Book choose a new voice?

### Forbidden Material
The Book of Exultation is on the Helmite confiscation list. Possession is a serious crime under Accord law:
- **Possession of forbidden materials:** Imprisonment (months to years), property seizure
- **If identified as an original text:** Escalation to sedition charges, possible death sentence
- The Book must be hidden at all times when near Accord territory

## Roleplaying the Book

The Book isn't just an item — it's a character. It carries Marren's twenty years of faith and Lliira's divine voice. When someone reads it aloud, describe:
- The warmth that spreads through the room
- The way people lean in, not because of magic but because the words are *true*
- The faint smell of summer and honey wine
- The sense that someone joyful wrote this, and that joy hasn't fully died

When Tessa reads from it, her performance is polished and moving. When Marren reads from it, it's rougher, less practiced — but *real*. The refugees will feel the difference even if they can't articulate it.
