
# The Lastbloom

_Wondrous item, artifact (requires attunement by a creature capable of genuine compassion)_

A single scarlet flower that never wilts. Warm to the touch. Holding it brings the echo of a laugh, the ghost of a smile, the ache of something joyful now lost. A fragment of Lliira's final gift—not her consciousness, not her power, but a seed. The last ember of a dead hearth.

---

## General Properties

**Indestructible.** The Lastbloom cannot be destroyed by mundane means. Fire doesn't burn it. Blades pass through it. It can be hidden but not unmade.

**Hope's Beacon.** The flower glows faintly (dim light, 5 feet) when in the presence of someone who genuinely _needs_ hope. Not wants. Needs.

**Joy's Refuge.** While carrying the Lastbloom, you can't be magically compelled to feel despair or hopelessness. You still feel your own grief, but it can't be weaponized against you.

---

## Dormant State

_Current_

**Memory of Joy.** Once per long rest, you can touch a willing creature and let them experience a vivid memory of their happiest moment. This has no mechanical effect but can:

- Break someone out of despair
- Calm a panicked creature
- Remind someone what they're fighting for
- Potentially shift an NPC's attitude or resolve

---

## Awakened State

_Unlocks after a significant act of joyful defiance_

**Fearless Heart.** You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.

**Memory of Joy (Improved).** The touch can now end the frightened or charmed condition on a willing creature.

**Calm Emotions.** Once per long rest, you can cast _calm emotions_ (spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + Charisma modifier) without expending a spell slot. Affected creatures don't feel suppressed—they feel _lighter_. The emotional effect lingers even after the spell ends.

**Hymn of the Lastlight.** Once per long rest, as an action, you can speak words of genuine encouragement, hope, or defiance. Up to six creatures of your choice within 30 feet who can hear you gain temporary hit points equal to your level + your Charisma modifier. Creatures currently feeling despair, grief, or hopelessness gain double the temporary hit points instead.

---

## Exalted State

_Unlocks when joy becomes an act of revolution_

**Unbreakable Spirit.** You are immune to being frightened and to any effect that causes despair or hopelessness.

**Lliira's Last Laugh.** Once per long rest, when you or an ally within 60 feet would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to invoke the flower's power:

- The target drops to 1 hit point instead of 0
- Every hostile creature within 30 feet of the target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency + your Charisma modifier) or be stunned until the end of their next turn
- Affected creatures hear, just for a moment, the sound of genuine, defiant, unbreakable laughter

**Bloom.** You can plant the Lastbloom and perform a 10-minute ritual. For the next 24 hours, the area within 100 feet of the flower gains the following effects:

- Creatures have advantage on saving throws against fear, charm, and despair effects
- Acts of genuine celebration, laughter, or kindness cause small scarlet flowers to bloom temporarily in cracks and corners
- The Accord's divination magic cannot penetrate the area
- After 24 hours, the Lastbloom can be retrieved; the small flowers fade, but something lingers

---

## The Catch

The Lastbloom responds to genuine joy, hope, and compassion. It cannot be used cynically.

A wielder who:

- Acts with deliberate cruelty
- Crushes hope in others
- Uses the flower purely as a tool without emotional investment

...finds its properties going dormant. The flower doesn't judge—it has no consciousness—but it fades, like embers without fuel.

**It doesn't demand heroism. It doesn't demand victory. It only asks that the wielder, in whatever small way they can, keeps joy alive.**

---

## Advancement Triggers

|State|Trigger (DM Discretion)|
|---|---|
|**Dormant → Awakened**|A significant act of joyful defiance: throwing a forbidden festival, openly celebrating in Accord territory, using joy as a weapon against despair, making a genuine sacrifice to preserve hope for others|
|**Awakened → Exalted**|Joy becomes revolution: the party sparks widespread resistance through hope, the Lastbloom is used in a place of great significance (Joybright, Wintervale), or the wielder fully commits to becoming a symbol of what Lliira represented|

## Notes for the Druid

Since your druid carries this, consider:

- **Narrative tie-in:** How does a druid connect to a dead goddess of joy? Perhaps through the natural cycle—the idea that even in death, seeds remain. The druid understands that nothing truly ends; it transforms.
- **Roleplay hook:** The flower might "react" to the druid's wild shapes, glowing brighter when they take joyful forms (a playful animal) versus predatory ones.
- **Mechanical note:** The Charisma-based DCs may be suboptimal for a druid. If you want, you could allow Wisdom instead since druids key off that stat. Alternatively, keep it Charisma to represent that this isn't about the druid's power—it's about their capacity for genuine emotional connection.
