**The Lastbloom**

_Wondrous item, artifact (requires attunement)_

A single scarlet flower, seemingly fresh-cut, that never wilts. It appeared in Kell's Landing at the moment of Lliira's death—or perhaps it was always there, waiting. It feels warm to the touch, and holding it brings a faint sense memory: the echo of a laugh, the ghost of a smile, the ache of something joyful now lost.

**Discovery**

The flower is growing from cracked cobblestones in an unremarkable alley or courtyard. When a player first touches it, they feel a pulse of warmth and hear—not with their ears—a single exhaled breath. Relief? Farewell? A blessing? The flower comes free easily, roots and all, and the roots curl gently around the holder's fingers once before going still.

Anyone with divine sensitivity (paladin, cleric, or even strong intuition) recognizes this immediately: something of Lliira is in this flower. Not her consciousness. Not her power. But a fragment. A seed. The last ember of a dead hearth.

**Properties**

The Lastbloom's abilities are subtle at first and grow as the campaign progresses—as joy is rediscovered, as hope returns, as the wielder commits to what it represents.

_Dormant State (Initial)_

- The flower cannot be destroyed by mundane means. Fire doesn't burn it. Blades pass through it. It can be hidden but not unmade.
- While carrying the Lastbloom, you can't be magically compelled to feel despair or hopelessness. You still feel your own grief, but it can't be weaponized against you.
- Once per long rest, you can touch a creature and let them feel a moment of genuine joy—a memory of their happiest moment, vivid and real. This has no mechanical effect but can break someone out of despair, calm a panicked creature, or remind someone what they're fighting for.
- The flower glows faintly (dim light, 5 feet) when in the presence of someone who genuinely needs hope. Not want. _Need_.

_Awakened State (Mid-Campaign, After a Significant Act of Joyful Defiance)_

- You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
- The memory-of-joy touch can now end the frightened or charmed condition on a willing creature.
- Once per long rest, you can cast _calm emotions_ without expending a spell slot. When you do, creatures who fail their save don't feel suppressed—they feel _lighter_. The effect lingers emotionally even after the spell ends.
- _Hymn of the Lastlight_: Once per long rest, you can speak a few words of genuine encouragement, hope, or defiance. Up to six creatures who can hear you gain temporary hit points equal to your level + your Charisma modifier. If any of those creatures were feeling despair, grief, or hopelessness, the temporary hit points are doubled for them.

_Exalted State (Late Campaign, When Joy Becomes an Act of Revolution)_

- You are immune to being frightened and to effects that cause despair or hopelessness.
- _Lliira's Last Laugh_: Once per long rest, when you or an ally within 60 feet would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to invoke the flower's power. The target instead drops to 1 hit point, and every hostile creature within 30 feet of them must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or be stunned until the end of their next turn as they hear, just for a moment, the sound of genuine, defiant, unbreakable laughter.
- _Bloom_: You can plant the Lastbloom in the ground and perform a 10-minute ritual. For the next 24 hours, a 100-foot radius around the flower becomes a sanctuary of joy. Creatures within the area have advantage on saving throws against fear, charm, and despair effects. Acts of genuine celebration, laughter, or kindness within the area cause small scarlet flowers to bloom temporarily in cracks and corners. The Accord's divination magic cannot penetrate this area. After 24 hours, the Lastbloom can be retrieved; the small flowers fade, but something lingers—a feeling, a possibility, a memory that this place was happy once and could be again.

**The Catch**

The Lastbloom responds to genuine joy, hope, and compassion. It cannot be used cynically. A wielder who acts in cruelty, who crushes hope in others, or who uses the flower as merely a tool finds its properties going dormant. Not judging—the flower has no consciousness—but fading, like embers without fuel.

It doesn't demand heroism. It doesn't demand victory. It only asks that the wielder, in whatever small way they can, _keeps joy alive_.

**Plot Implications**

- The Accord will eventually learn something survived. They'll hunt for it.
- Other dissenters—Tymora's faithful, Mask's networks—will hear rumors. Some will want to protect it. Some will want to use it. Some will want to see if it's real.
- Is this just a fragment? Or could it be more? Could joy—could _Lliira_—grow back from a single seed?

That's not a question with an answer yet. That's what the campaign is for.

**Who Gets It?**

This could go to the paladin as the obvious divine-touched choice—but consider: what if it chooses someone unexpected? The rogue who pretends not to care. The fighter who lost something and stopped laughing years ago. The character who needs it most, not the one most qualified.

Who among your five players present today would make the most interesting carrier for the last ember of joy?
