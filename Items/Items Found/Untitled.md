# The Book of Exultation

_Wondrous item, artifact (requires attunement)_

---

## Nature and Origin

The Book of Exultation is an original holy text written entirely in Lliira's divine hand. Not divinely inspired — _divine_. Every word was placed on the page by the goddess of joy herself, over centuries of direct authorship. The teachings, prayers, hymns, parables, and philosophy within are Lliira's voice made physical. The Book predates the Divine Accord by centuries. It is, in every meaningful sense, a piece of a goddess.

**School of Magic:** The Book radiates **Evocation** magic — overwhelmingly so. A _detect magic_ cast on it reveals an evocation aura so intense it drowns out everything else within 30 feet. This is not the subtle magic of enchantment or illusion. This is raw divine power expressed as _creation_ — the same school that produces light, healing, and radiant destruction. Lliira's joy was never passive. It was generative, creative, explosive. The Book reflects that.

A secondary **Abjuration** aura is detectable with a DC 18 Arcana check — the Book's self-protective properties (indestructibility, anti-divination, return to wielder) are woven through the binding itself. Lliira built the lock as well as the treasure.

**This particular copy** was possessed by **Marren Brightwell**, a genuine Lliiran lay priest with a true calling, who annotated it extensively over twenty years with personal prayers, doubts, and reflections in the margins. His handwriting — smaller, messier, deeply human — is distinct from Lliira's elegant script. When Marren was arrested by Helmites ten years ago, his wife **Tessa** found the Book hidden in a false panel. Over the following decade, she used it to build a false identity as a Lliiran prophet, co-opting Marren's words and spiritual authority as her own.

**Paul currently possesses the Book.** The Book is choosing him.

---

## Appearance

A hand-bound volume in soft leather dyed deep scarlet — Lliira's sacred color — now faded to warm rust. The pages are thick vellum filled with flowing script in two distinct hands:

- **The original text:** Elegant, rhythmic, written with a joy that bleeds through the ink. The letters shimmer faintly scarlet in candlelight. Reading it aloud evokes warmth. This is the divine voice — every word placed by Lliira's own hand
- **Marren's annotations:** Smaller, messier, crowded into margins. Personal prayers, theological questions, arguments with the text. His voice is warm, uncertain, deeply human. A man talking to his goddess and not always agreeing with her

The Book smells faintly of summer festivals and honey wine. It radiates warmth to the touch, like something alive. The warmth intensifies when the Book is read with genuine feeling, and dims when it is read as performance.

---

## Attunement

The Book of Exultation can be attuned to by any creature with a Wisdom of 13 or higher. Attunement requires reading aloud from the Book for 1 hour. During this hour, the reader experiences vivid emotional memories — not their own, but fragments of thousands of celebrations, dances, and moments of pure joy from across centuries of Lliiran worship. Festivals in cities the reader has never visited. Songs in languages they don't speak. The feeling of dancing with strangers and meaning it.

Upon attunement, the Book _knows_ its wielder. Not consciously — but it resonates differently depending on the wielder's relationship with joy. A wielder consumed by guilt, despair, or deception finds fewer pages legible. A wielder who actively seeks and shares joy discovers passages that weren't visible before.

**Tessa** was attuned for years but never read the Book honestly. She read it as performance. Entire sections remained hidden from her — she didn't know they existed.

### Loyalty

The Book of Exultation bonds to its attuned wielder. If the Book is lost, stolen, or separated from its wielder by any means, it **returns**. Within 24 hours, the Book reappears within 5 feet of the attuned creature — found in a pack that was already searched, slipped between bedrolls, resting on a windowsill that was empty a moment ago. The Book does not teleport dramatically. It simply _is there_, as if it had never left. As if it was always exactly where it was meant to be.

This return cannot be prevented by mundane means. Locking the Book in a vault, dropping it in the ocean, burying it — the Book returns. Only an _antimagic field_, a deity's direct intervention, or the willing relinquishment of attunement by the wielder can sever the bond.

If the attuned wielder dies, the Book remains where it fell — but it dims. The warmth fades. The pages become harder to read, the text blurring as if seen through tears. It waits. It has waited before. It is patient.

## Properties (All States)

**Indestructible.** The Book cannot be destroyed by mundane means. Fire doesn't burn it. Blades don't cut it. Water doesn't damage the pages. It can be hidden, locked away, or stolen — but not unmade. Only a deity or an act of divine magnitude could destroy it.

**Lliira's Warmth.** While attuned, you are immune to magical effects that impose despair, hopelessness, or sorrow (such as the _symbol_ spell's hopelessness effect, a banshee's wail of grief, or Sharran despair abilities). You still feel your own natural grief and sadness — the Book doesn't suppress emotion, it prevents weaponized despair.

**Joyful Presence.** While you carry the Book, you emanate a subtle aura of warmth. Creatures within 10 feet who are friendly or neutral toward you feel slightly more at ease. You have advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks made to comfort, inspire, or calm others.

**The True Voice.** A careful reader who studies the Book for 1 hour (DC 14 Investigation or Religion) realizes the annotations don't match Tessa's personality — they belong to someone else. A DC 16 check or hearing Marren speak confirms: these are Marren's words, not Tessa's.

**Divine Authorship.** Any creature capable of casting _detect magic_ or _identify_ recognizes the original text as divine in origin — written by a deity, not transcribed by mortal hands. The emotional weight of this realization is significant: this is not a holy book _about_ a goddess. This is a holy book _by_ a goddess. She sat down and wrote it, word by word, with the same hands that danced at the Festival of Endless Joy.

## Dormant State

_Initial — available upon attunement_

**Divine Spellcasting.** The Book grants access to divine evocation magic drawn from Lliira's residual power. While attuned, you can use the Book as a spellcasting focus and gain the following:

- You know the cantrips _light_, _thaumaturgy_, and _friends_
- You have 4 first-level and 3 second-level spell slots, which recharge on a long rest
- **1st level spells:** _bless_, _charm person_, _healing word_, _sanctuary_
- **2nd level spells:** _aid_, _calm emotions_, _lesser restoration_, _enthral_
- Your spellcasting ability for these spells is Charisma (spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA mod, spell attack = proficiency + CHA mod)

**Words of Comfort.** As an action, you can read a passage from the Book to a creature within 5 feet. The creature regains hit points equal to 1d8 + your Charisma modifier. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Festival's Memory.** Once per long rest, you can read aloud from the Book for 1 minute. Up to 6 creatures who can hear you gain the benefits of a short rest (spending Hit Dice, recovering features) without actually resting. The reading fills them with warmth and the ghost-memory of a celebration they never attended.

## Awakened State

_Triggered by a significant act of joyful defiance or the recovery of a lost Lliiran tradition_

The Book's pages shift. New passages become legible — hymns, rituals, prayers that were hidden before. The scarlet cover deepens in color. The warmth intensifies. Marren's annotations glow faintly when the Book is opened, as if his faith is being honored alongside Lliira's words.

**Enhanced Spellcasting.** Your spell slots expand and you gain access to more powerful magic:

- You now have 4 first-level, 3 second-level, 3 third-level, and 1 fourth-level spell slots
- **3rd level spells:** _beacon of hope_, _mass healing word_, _dispel magic_, _remove curse_
- **4th level spells:** _freedom of movement_, _aura of purity_

**Hymn of Exultation.** Once per long rest, you can sing or read a Lliiran hymn as an action. For the next 10 minutes:

- You and all allies within 30 feet gain temporary hit points equal to your level + Charisma modifier
- Affected creatures have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, frightened, or possessed
- The area fills with faint golden light and the distant sound of festival music. Creatures of evil alignment within the area feel uneasy (disadvantage on Concentration checks)

**Unbind.** Twice per long rest, you can use an action to touch a creature and end one of the following conditions on it: charmed, frightened, paralyzed, or stunned. When you do, the target hears a laugh — warm, bright, defiant — and knows for one fleeting moment what it feels like to be truly free.

**Joy is Contagious.** When you restore hit points to a creature using the Book's spells or features, one additional creature within 10 feet of the target regains hit points equal to your Charisma modifier. Joy shared is joy multiplied.

## Exalted State

_Triggered by a profound act of joy in the face of overwhelming darkness — a celebration where celebration should be impossible, a dance in the shadow of the Accord, or the rekindling of a lost Lliiran tradition at great personal cost_

The Book blazes with inner light. The scarlet cover is vivid, unmarked, as if brand new. New chapters appear — not Marren's annotations, but something deeper. Lliira's own voice, writing directly to whoever holds the Book now. The pages are warm enough to feel through gloves.

**Full Spellcasting.** Your spell slots expand to their full potential:

- 4 first, 3 second, 3 third, 3 fourth, 2 fifth, and 1 sixth-level spell slots
- **5th level spells:** _greater restoration_, _mass cure wounds_, _circle of power_, _dawn_
- **6th level spells:** _heal_, _heroes' feast_

**The Last Dance.** Once per long rest, you can perform a 1-minute ritual that invokes Lliira's ultimate gift. Choose a point within 60 feet. A 40-foot radius burst of golden light and festival energy erupts:

- All allies in the area regain hit points equal to 6d8 + your Charisma modifier
- All allies in the area are freed from any curse, disease, poison, blindness, or deafness
- All hostile creatures in the area must make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or be stunned for 1 round and frightened of you for 1 minute as they witness a vision of Lliira's last festival — thousands dancing, singing, refusing to kneel
- For the next hour, the area is consecrated ground. Undead and fiends have disadvantage on attack rolls within it. Joy lingers. Small scarlet flowers bloom in cracks and corners

**Lliira's Voice.** You can cast _sending_ at will, but only to deliver messages of hope, comfort, or encouragement. The recipient hears the message in a voice layered with yours and something older — warm, laughing, unbroken. Creatures who receive these messages are immune to magical fear and despair for 24 hours.

**Goddess's Chosen.** Your Charisma score increases by 2, to a maximum of 24. You have advantage on all Charisma saving throws. When you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to instead drop to 1 hit point and emit a burst of radiant joy — each creature of your choice within 20 feet regains hit points equal to your Charisma modifier. Once you use this feature, it can't be used again until you finish a long rest.

**The Unwritten Chapter.** The final pages of the Book are blank. They always have been. In the Exalted state, words begin to appear — slowly, one line at a time, written in the divine hand. This is Lliira's unfinished story. The words that appear respond to what is happening in the campaign. They are prophecy, guidance, and question:

> _"What grows from a single ember?"_ _"The dancer's feet remember even when the music stops."_ _"You are reading this because I trusted you would. Do not make me wrong."_

The DM writes these entries as the campaign demands. The Book is becoming something new — not just Lliira's record, but the story of what comes after.

## The Joybright Convergence

_What happens when the Book comes home_

The Book of Exultation was written in Joybright. Lliira composed it in the Sunken Temple — her personal sanctum within the holy city — over the span of centuries. Bringing the Book back to Joybright is not just symbolically significant. It is the return of a goddess's voice to the place where she last spoke.

### Entering Joybright (Any State)

When the Book crosses the city boundary:

- The Book grows physically warm — hot to the touch but not painful. The holder feels a heartbeat through the leather. Not theirs
- Every page becomes legible. Hidden passages, concealed chapters, text that was invisible to Tessa for a decade — all of it opens. The Book holds nothing back within these walls
- The evocation aura intensifies. A _detect magic_ cast on the Book in Joybright reveals an aura that fills the entire building the wielder is standing in. The Book is _resonating_ with the city

### Entering the Sunken Temple

When the Book is brought into the Sunken Temple — where it was written — the convergence accelerates:

- The Book opens itself. The pages turn without being touched, settling on a passage that has never been visible before. It reads:

> _"You brought me home. I wrote these words in this room, at this table, looking out at the water. I could hear the Festival from here. I could always hear it. I wrote between songs. I danced between pages. This Book is not a record of joy. It is joy itself, pressed into vellum and bound in the color of my heart."_

> _"I am dead. I know this. I felt it happen. It hurt less than I expected and more than I can describe. But I wrote this Book knowing that one day I might not be here to read it aloud. So I wrote it for you. Whoever you are. Whatever brought you here."_

> _"Read it. Read it aloud. Read it to anyone who will listen. And when you are done, do not close the cover. Leave it open. Joy was never meant to be shut away."_

- The Book advances one tier immediately (if not already Exalted)
- If the Lastbloom is also present, both artifacts advance one tier
- The golden Echo on the temple altar responds — it pulses in rhythm with the Book's heartbeat. Voice and ember, reunited in the grave

### The Convergence: All Three Fragments United

When the Book (voice), the Lastbloom (ember), and Joybright (grave) are all present in the Sunken Temple simultaneously, the Book reaches its **final state.**

The Unwritten Chapter fills. Not one line at a time — all at once. Lliira's divine hand writes furiously across blank pages, the ink appearing wet and golden, the words pouring out as if she has been holding her breath for twenty-three years:

> _"You came. I wasn't sure anyone would."_

> _"I can't come back. What dies divinely stays dead. The other gods made sure of that — Bane's sentence, Helm's chains, Loviatar's blade. There is no loophole. There is no resurrection. I am gone."_

> _"But."_

> _"I wrote this Book. I planted that flower. I loved this city. And love — real love, the kind that makes you dance with strangers and sing when you're afraid and laugh at funerals because the dead would want you to — love doesn't need a goddess to survive. It just needs someone willing to carry it."_

> _"So here is my last gift. Not power. Not divinity. Something better."_

> _"Permission."_

> _"You have permission to be joyful. In the dark. In the danger. When the Accord says kneel and the Inquisitor says confess and the world says that happiness is too small a thing to die for."_

> _"It isn't small. It was never small. It is the biggest thing there is."_

> _"Now close this Book. Not because joy should be shut away — but because you don't need it anymore. You never did. Everything I wrote in these pages, you already knew. I just reminded you."_

> _"Go. Dance. Sing. Laugh. Defy every god who ever told a mortal that joy was dangerous."_

> _"They were right. It is."_

> _"That's the point."_

## Final State: The Open Book

_Campaign endgame — what the Book becomes after the Convergence_

After the Convergence, the Book of Exultation undergoes a permanent transformation. It is no longer an artifact in the traditional sense. It is something new.

**The Book Cannot Be Closed.** Literally. The cover will not shut. The pages remain open, always displaying a different passage — whichever one the nearest person most needs to read. This is not a curse or a limitation. It is Lliira's final instruction: joy was never meant to be shut away.

**The Spellcasting Remains.** The wielder retains all Exalted state spellcasting and features. The Book's power is not diminished — if anything, it is more focused now, channeled through a wielder the Book has fully accepted.

**The Unwritten Chapter Continues.** New words still appear. But they are no longer Lliira's. They are the wielder's. The Book is writing _their_ story now — recording their acts of joy, defiance, and compassion in the same divine script Lliira used. The wielder is not becoming a god. They are becoming an _author_ — continuing a story that Lliira started and trusted someone else to finish.

**Sanctuary.** While the Open Book is in Joybright, the city becomes a permanent sanctuary:

- The Accord's divination magic cannot penetrate the city limits
- Creatures within the city have advantage on saving throws against fear, charm, and despair
- Acts of genuine celebration cause scarlet flowers to bloom permanently in stone and soil
- The laughter is constant now — not ghostly, not echoing, but _present_. As if the city has remembered how to be alive
- The Weeping Watch garrison can no longer comfortably remain. The joy is too strong. Soldiers either leave or begin to question everything they were told

**The Accord's Response.** A permanently awakened Joybright — shielded from scrying, radiating joy, becoming a beacon for every dissenter and secret pilgrim on the Isles — is the single greatest threat to the Divine Accord's control of Ghandara. The Convergence doesn't end the campaign. It _escalates_ it. Inquisitor Thorne will come. The Accord will mobilize. And the party will have to defend not just a city, but an _idea_.

**Campaign Endgame Possibilities:**

|Path|What Happens|
|---|---|
|**Defend Joybright**|The party fortifies the city, recruits allies (highland clans, dissenter networks, defecting Helmites), and prepares for the Accord's assault. The final battle is fought on sacred ground with the Open Book as the heart of the defense|
|**Spread the Joy**|The party carries the Open Book to other locations — Wintervale, Accord's Rest, Death's Edge. Each place the Book visits, joy kindles. The campaign becomes a revolution, city by city, with the Book as the spark|
|**Confront the Accord**|The party takes the fight to the source. Accord's Rest. The Hall of Scales. The Black Threshold. The Open Book is the weapon — not because it deals damage, but because it _proves_ that joy survived. Bane sentenced joy to death. Joy refused|
|**The New Festival**|The party holds a Festival of Endless Joy in Joybright for the first time in twenty-three years. Refugees, pilgrims, defectors, dissenters — they come from across the Isles. The Accord watches. The question is no longer "can joy survive?" The question is "can the Accord survive joy?"|

## Interaction with Other Artifacts

**The Lastbloom + The Book of Exultation:** When brought within 30 feet of each other, both resonate visibly. The Lastbloom blooms fuller, its scarlet deepening. The Book's pages flutter to a specific passage:

> _"Joy is not owned. Joy is shared. Those who hoard it find only the memory of warmth."_

When both are present:

- The Lastbloom's state advances one tier (Dormant → Awakened, Awakened → Exalted) while within 30 feet of the Book
- The Book's healing spells and features restore an additional 1d6 hit points while within 30 feet of the Lastbloom
- If both artifacts are brought to **Joybright** together — the site of Lliira's death — the Convergence triggers (see above)

**The Book + Accord Divination:** The Book actively resists Accord scrying. While carrying it, you cannot be targeted by divination magic cast by servants of Bane, Helm, or Tyr unless the caster succeeds on a DC 20 ability check using their spellcasting ability. The Book has been hiding for twenty-three years. It knows how.

## The Catch

Like the Lastbloom, the Book responds to genuineness. A wielder who reads from it as performance — for manipulation, for control, for power without feeling — finds the text dimming. Pages become illegible. Spells fail. The Book doesn't punish — it simply stops responding, the way a fire goes out when you stop feeding it.

**Tessa** wielded the Book for ten years and never advanced it past Dormant. She read Lliira's words every day and never _heard_ them. The Book gave her exactly as much as she was willing to genuinely receive: just enough to keep the lie alive. Not because the Book was cruel. Because the Book was honest.

---

## The Fraud — Plot Significance

The Book is the physical evidence of Tessa's stolen identity. Marren's voice is unmistakable throughout the annotations. Anyone who knew Marren — or who heard him speak and then reads the margins — will recognize the truth.

**Now that Tessa, Marren, and DaVonian are all dead,** the fraud is academic. But the _legacy_ matters. Paul holds a Book that three people died for — one who wrote in its margins with genuine faith, one who performed those words as a mask, and one who was too young to understand any of it. What Paul does with the Book defines whether those deaths meant something.

## Roleplaying the Book

The Book isn't just an item — it's a character. When someone reads aloud, describe:

- The warmth that spreads through the room
- The way people lean in — not magic, but because the words are _true_
- The faint smell of summer and honey wine
- The evocation aura pulsing gently — not threatening, but _alive_
- The sense that someone joyful wrote this, and that joy hasn't fully died

When **Tessa** read from it: polished, moving, practiced. A performance. The Book gave her just enough. When **Marren** read from it: rougher, less practiced — but _real_. The refugees felt the difference even if they couldn't articulate it. When **Paul** reads from it: the Book responds. Warmth floods his hands. New passages appear. For just a moment, he hears distant music and laughter. A voice that isn't words says: _welcome back._

## Selected Passages

_For use when Paul reads aloud at the table. These are Lliira's words, written in her divine hand._

### The Opening (First Page — Always the First Page)

> I am Lliira, and I was not born from thunder or from war. I was born the first time a mother laughed at her child's face. I was born when two strangers danced without knowing each other's names. I was born in the space between the held breath and the helpless smile.

> I am not the strongest of my kind. I am not the wisest. I have no armies and no fortresses and no laws carved in stone.

> I have this: when everything else is taken from you — your home, your name, your safety, your gods — joy is the last thing to die. It dies hard. It dies fighting. And sometimes, if you are very brave and very foolish, it does not die at all.

> This book is for the brave and the foolish. You will need both.

### On Prayer

> You do not need to kneel. You do not need to clasp your hands or close your eyes or speak in any particular tongue. You do not need a temple or a priest or permission.

> You need only this: mean it.

> A prayer spoken while dancing is heard. A prayer whispered while laughing is heard. A prayer wept into a pillow at midnight when the world is too heavy and the morning is too far away — that prayer is heard loudest of all.

> I am listening. I am always listening. Even when I am not here, the listening remains.

### On Grief

> You will lose people. I cannot prevent this. No god can, and any god who claims otherwise is selling certainty they do not possess.

> Grieve them. Grieve them loudly. Grieve them with the same energy you loved them with — messily, completely, without dignity or restraint. Dignity is for temples. Grief is for hearts.

> And when the grief has had its time — not when it is finished, because it is never finished, but when it has had its _time_ — find something to celebrate. A sunset. A meal. The fact that you are still breathing and the world has not ended despite every indication that it should have.

> That is not disrespect to the dead. That is the highest honor. The dead do not want your silence. They want your noise.

### On Resistance

> Joy is not the absence of suffering. Anyone who has danced until their feet bled knows this. Joy is what you choose when suffering says _sit down_. It is the defiant act of standing, of moving, of refusing to let pain have the last word.

> Tyrants fear joy because joy cannot be governed. You can chain a body. You can silence a voice. You can burn every book and break every instrument and forbid every song.

> You cannot stop a human being from finding, in the darkest corner of the darkest cell, something — _anything_ — to smile about.

> That smile is mine. That smile is _yours_. And no law written by any god or mortal can take it from you.

### On Dancing

> I could have chosen any sacred act. Meditation. Fasting. Pilgrimage. Study. All worthy. All valid.

> I chose dancing because dancing requires you to be in your body. Not in your head, where fear lives. Not in your past, where grief waits. Not in your future, where worry paces. In your body. Right now. Feet on the ground. Arms in the air. Moving because moving is what alive things do.

> You do not need to be graceful. You do not need to be practiced. You need only to move.

> The gods who sit on thrones will never understand this. The gods who stand in judgment will never feel it. But you — you who are reading this, you who are human and mortal and temporary and _perfect_ — you know. You have always known.

> Move.

### The Blessing (For Reading Over Others)

> May your laughter outlive your fear. May your feet remember the steps when your mind forgets the song. May the ones you love know that you love them, because you told them, because you showed them, because you danced with them on an ordinary day for no reason at all. May you find, in the ruins of what was taken, the seed of what will grow. And may you never, ever, under any circumstance, apologize for being happy.

### Marren's Annotation (Found in the Margin Beside "On Resistance")

_In Marren's hand, smaller, messier, pressed into the margin:_

> She makes it sound easy. It isn't easy. Some days resistance is just getting out of bed. Some days the smile she talks about is the hardest thing I've ever done. But I do it. I do it because she asked. And because the alternative — letting them win, letting the silence be permanent — is worse than anything they could do to me.

> I hope whoever reads this after me is braver than I am. But if they aren't, if they're just as scared and just as tired, that's enough too. Lliira didn't ask for heroes. She asked for dancers. And dancers don't need to be brave. They just need to start moving.
