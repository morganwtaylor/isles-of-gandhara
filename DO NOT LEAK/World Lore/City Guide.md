# Isles of Ghandara — World & City Guide

---

# PART ONE: THE WORLD YOUR PLAYERS KNOW

_This section is player-safe. It represents what a reasonably informed resident of Ghandara would know — tavern talk, common knowledge, things you'd learn traveling the island._

---

## The Divine Accord

Forty years ago, the gods reorganized. What they call the Divine Accord — a coalition of deities who declared mortal autonomy a threat to cosmic balance. Three gods built it: Bane, Helm, and Tyr. They called themselves the Architects.

**Bane** is the Accord's iron voice. He believes mortals left to govern themselves will always choose destruction. His faithful are few in Ghandara but growing — the Black Fists, inquisitors who arrive when the Helmites and Tyrrans aren't enough.

**Helm** is the Accord's dutiful hand. The Watcher. God of guardians and protectors. His faithful are the most common Accord presence — armored paladins and soldiers who patrol every significant settlement. Before the Accord, a Helmite at your door meant you were safe. Now it might mean you're under arrest. Most Helmites believe they're still protecting people. The ones who doubt have learned to stay quiet.

**Tyr** is the Accord's legal framework. God of justice and law. His courts process dissent through proper channels — meticulous trials, evidence weighed, witnesses heard. Then the accused is convicted, because the laws criminalize any deviation from approved worship by definition. The process is fair. The outcome is predetermined.

Other gods joined. Some because they believed (Sune told herself beauty requires order). Some because they were afraid. Some because they saw opportunity in the new order (Shar, Loviatar, Cyric slid into the Accord's shadow like parasites into a wound).

Not everyone fell in line. Tymora funds resistance through luck and coincidence. Mask runs smuggler networks. Selûne watches and waits. Ilmater endures.

### The Execution of Lliira

Twenty-three years ago. The sky above Joybright became a stage.

Lliira, goddess of joy and freedom, opposed the Accord from the beginning. She spoke against it publicly. She encouraged her faithful to resist. And every decade, her worshipers gathered at Joybright — the holy city on the Mirthwater — for the Festival of Endless Joy. Weeks of celebration that reaffirmed everything the Accord was trying to suppress.

The Accord came to end it permanently.

Bane pronounced sentence. Helm restrained her — duty over love. Loviatar administered the killing blow. Slowly. Lathander was present. Whether he watched in chains or stood freely among the executioners, something in him broke.

Lliira did not beg. Some say she laughed. Her divine spark was extinguished. Her body became light, then ash, then nothing.

Joy has not felt the same since. Festivals exist but ring hollow. Laughter in public draws glances. The color scarlet — her sacred hue — is quietly forbidden.

## The Island of Ghandara

The largest landmass in the archipelago. Home to roughly 185,000 souls living under varying degrees of divine authority. The Mirthwater — a great inland sea — splits the island roughly in half, with the Accord's capital on the eastern shore and the ruins of Joybright visible across the water on the western side.

The climate is temperate maritime. Colder in the north where the Thornwood stretches endlessly, milder in the southern ports and the fertile vales. The Geast Range dominates the western interior — old mountains where the highland clans have lived since before written history.

---

## The Geast Range & The Highland Clans

_"The Accord rules the lowlands. The clans rule the peaks."_

The Geast Range — "geast" means "spirit" or "ghost" in the old tongue — is the mountain spine of western Ghandara. Jagged peaks, deep valleys, ancient stone, and older customs. The clan system predates the Accord, predates the current gods' interest in the Isles, predates written history. The highlanders didn't ask permission to live here and they're not asking permission to stay.

The Accord has never committed sufficient forces to truly control the highlands. Helmite patrols go up the Highland Track and come back having seen very little. The clans maintain the roads (barely), pay whatever oaths they've technically agreed to (interpreted creatively), and otherwise carry on as they always have.

### The Clans

**Clan Mòr — Duncraig** The largest and most powerful clan. Duncraig sits in the northern highlands, the closest thing the clans have to a capital. Thane Bràghad Mòr is in his sixties — massive, gray, sharp as the mountains he rules from. He signed the Accord's oaths in the old language and interprets them by clan law, which is to say he technically complied while agreeing to almost nothing. The Accord thinks he cooperates. He thinks he made a temporary arrangement with occupiers.

Population roughly 3,000. Caer Dun — the seat — overlooks the northern valleys. Helmites are stationed here and feel outnumbered.

**Clan Varn — Stonewatch** Southwest of Duncraig, guarding the main western approach to the highlands. Thane Alastair Varn is in his forties — practical, watchful, grudgingly tolerant of the Accord's existence. Everyone who enters the highlands through the main pass is noted by Clan Varn. Population roughly 700.

**Clan Aeris — Eaglerest** High in the southern mountains of the Geast Range. Accessible only in summer. Thane Skara Aeris is in her thirties — lean, scarred, inherited her father's defiance after he died rather than sign the Accord's oaths. She has never acknowledged the Accord's authority. Bràghad calls her reckless. She calls him a collaborator. Population roughly 400.

**Clan Fenn — Blackmoor** Far west, near the coast. Bog territory, older dialect, old practices. "The Fenn" rules — a title, not a name, and the identity of the current Fenn is not widely shared outside the clan. No Accord patrol has entered Blackmoor in five years. The last one that tried came back with stories they wouldn't repeat. Population roughly 500.

**Clan Dunne — Coldwell** South of the Geast Range. A broken clan with long memories. There is no Thane — Elder Moira Dunne, in her eighties, speaks for the survivors. What happened to break Clan Dunne is a story told in whispers. Moira keeps the stories alive. She is waiting for something. Population roughly 250.

**Highcairn** East of the Geast Range, north of Coldwell. Not a town — a sacred meeting ground. Neutral territory where feuds pause and clans gather to resolve disputes that can't be settled by sword. The standing stones here predate the clans themselves. Roughly 200 people maintain the ground year-round. No weapons may be drawn within sight of the stones.

**Cairnmoor** Central highlands, situated in the triangle between Duncraig, Stonewatch, and Highcairn. Where Highcairn is ceremonial, Cairnmoor is practical — the crossroads settlement where day-to-day clan business actually happens. Trade, news, disputes too small for Highcairn, travelers passing through. A relatively new settlement by highland standards, which in clan terms means it's only been there a few centuries.

### Notable Highland Landmarks

**The Standing Stones of Eildh** — Pre-human standing stones deep in the Geast Range. The carvings have never been deciphered. Accord surveyors sent to map them have a habit of not returning.

**Caer Bràn** — A ruined fortress high in the peaks. Something is sealed inside. The clans don't go there. They don't talk about why.

**The Weeping Pass** — A mountain pass connecting the highlands to the Joybright coast. It avoids every Accord checkpoint on the island. The clans know about it. They don't volunteer this information.

## The Highland Track

The main route from the eastern lowlands into the Geast Range. It branches off the Accord Road west of Briarholt and climbs into clan territory. The clans maintain it — barely. In summer it's passable by horse and cart. In winter, only on foot, and only if you know the way.

The Accord technically claims jurisdiction over the Highland Track. In practice, Helmite patrols use it to reach Duncraig and come back having seen nothing interesting. The clans allow this because it's easier than fighting about it, and because the patrols never leave the track.

Travelers on the Highland Track pass through Cairnmoor, where Clan Mòr's people will note their business with polite curiosity that is absolutely intelligence-gathering.

---

## The Accord Road

The main highway of Ghandara. It runs from Accord's Rest — the capital — north through Helmgate to Briarholt and onward to Springvale in the Northern Reaches. Paved in the capital region, graded earth beyond. Patrolled by Helmite riders, with toll stations and checkpoint inspections at regular intervals.

Traveling the Accord Road means being seen, documented, and questioned. Cargo is inspected. Papers are checked. The road is well-maintained, safe from bandits (the Accord doesn't tolerate competition), and absolutely monitored.

Most people who want to move freely use the Forest Ways through the Thornwood instead. But the Forest Ways don't have bridges, patrols, or guarantees.

---

## Helmgate

_Grid: G3 — On the Accord Road, north of Accord's Rest_

A fortified checkpoint town. Population roughly 3,500. This is where the Accord Road narrows through a valley, and the Accord has built walls across it. Every traveler heading north or south on the main road passes through Helmgate.

The inspection is thorough. Cargo manifests checked against harbor records. Travel documents examined. Questions asked about destination, purpose, and religious affiliation. Contraband searches — the list of forbidden items grows regularly. Scarlet cloth, unauthorized holy symbols, certain songs in written form, dancing masks, any depiction of Lliira.

The garrison is large — several hundred Helmites, plus Tyrran clerks handling the paperwork. A small Banite "advisory" presence that everyone pretends to ignore.

For most travelers, Helmgate is an inconvenience. For anyone carrying something they shouldn't, it's a wall.

---

## Joybright

_Grid: E3–F3 — Western shore of the Mirthwater, facing Accord's Rest_

The holy city that was.

Joybright was built on the western coast of the Mirthwater, directly across from what is now Accord's Rest. On a clear day, you can see one shore from the other — fifteen, maybe twenty miles of water between the capital of the Accord and the corpse of the city it killed.

Before the execution, Joybright was Lliira's greatest holy city. The Festival of Endless Joy drew pilgrims from across Faerûn every decade. Strangers danced with strangers. Sorrow went there to heal. The music never stopped.

The music stopped twenty-three years ago.

The buildings still stand. They don't decay — nobody knows why. The streets are empty but not ruined. Dust doesn't settle the way it should. Paint doesn't peel. The festival lanterns still hang from their hooks, unlit, unrotted.

An Accord garrison — the Weeping Watch, roughly sixty soldiers — prevents pilgrimage. It's considered a punishment posting. The soldiers hear laughter at night. They request transfers quickly. Captain Delia Sworn has been there three years and hasn't smiled in any of them.

The Pilgrim's Path — the old road from Accord's Rest around the Mirthwater to Joybright — is officially closed. People still walk it at night.

### Nearby

**Greyshore** (~1,100) — Fishing village north of Joybright. The catch has been poor since the execution. Fishermen say the sea itself is grieving.

**Mistfall** (~800) — Fog-shrouded coastal settlement south of Joybright. A former pilgrim waystation. The old hostel stands empty, beds still made.

**Sorrow's End** (~500) — Southern coast village. Fishing, kelp harvesting, and a community-wide determination not to look north.

**The Weeping Stones** — Coastal cliffs near Joybright where salt deposits create the appearance of tears streaming down the rock face. Secret pilgrim site. The Accord knows people visit but hasn't been able to stop them without posting guards on every cliff.

## Briarholt

_Grid: G2–G3 — Central-eastern Ghandara, Thornwood region_

Second city of Ghandara. Population roughly 12,000. The logging capital — thornwood timber from the surrounding forest builds ships across the Isles. Where Accord's Rest runs on authority, Briarholt runs on commerce.

The Timber Guild holds real power here. The Accord governs on paper — there's a Helmite garrison, a Tyrran courthouse — but when disputes arise, Timber Guild arbitration settles most of them faster and to more people's satisfaction than the Accord's courts. Guildmaster Rowan Ashford plays the Accord's game, says the right things, files the right paperwork, and protects guild interests with the quiet ferocity of someone who knows exactly where the bodies are buried.

The city sits at the edge of the Thornwood, the great forest that dominates the central-eastern interior. Logging camps push deeper into the trees every year. The forest is hard to patrol — Helmites can watch the roads, but the Forest Ways are unmarked paths known only to loggers, smugglers, and people who prefer not to be found.

Dissenter sympathizers operate in the logging camps. It's easy to hide things in a forest.

### Briarholt Settlements

**Millford** (~1,200) — River town for timber processing. Sawmills run day and night. Workers are underpaid and resentful.

**Verdant Crossing** (~900) — Trading post at the forest road junction. Tilla Bramblefoot, a halfling, runs the inn and knows everyone's business.

**Ashwick** (~450) — Charcoal-burning settlement. Rough workers, Timber Guild oversight. Don't ask about travelers passing through.

**Hollowbrook** (~350) — Officially a woodcutter's camp. Quiet, small, unremarkable. The kind of place you pass through without thinking about it.

### Notable

**Whisper's Hollow** — An abandoned village in the Thornwood. Accord records from the Pacification are sealed. The buildings are preserved as if the inhabitants simply vanished one day.

**The Scarred Oak** — An ancient tree deep in the Thornwood. A lightning scar on its trunk bears an uncanny resemblance to a laughing face.

---

## Springvale

_Grid: F2 — Northern Ghandara, end of the Accord Road_

Population roughly 4,000. The "loyal" vale — positioned on the main northern road, it's where the Accord Road terminates and the wilder Northern Reaches begin. There's a Helmite waystation and Tyrran toll collectors. The locals comply, smile at patrols, and teach their children to do the same.

Springvale is what successful occupation looks like. Not broken, not rebellious — just quietly obedient. Whether this reflects genuine acceptance or the deepest kind of survival instinct depends on who you ask and how much they trust you.

---

## The Northern Reaches

_Grid: C1–F1, D2–E2 — Cold, remote, frontier territory_

Dense forests, rocky coasts, long winters. The great Thornwood dominates the interior. The Accord's grip is weakest here — too far, too few people, too little worth controlling.

**Northern Rest** (~1,500) — End-of-the-world fishing town. Last stop before open sea. A single elderly Helmite "keeps the peace" by being universally ignored. Smugglers use it as a waypoint between the Isles and the open ocean.

**Frostholm** (~600) — Remote village near the northern passes. Ice fishing, fur trapping. Technically under Accord law. Practically ungoverned.

**Northwatch Tower** (~40) — Accord watchtower on the northern coast. Lonely punishment posting. The garrison drinks heavily and writes reports that nobody reads.

### Notable

**The Oldgrove** — A stretch of ancient forest where Accord surveys consistently fail. Mapping parties enter, walk for hours, and emerge where they started. Druid territory, though the druids have never confirmed or denied this.

**Frostmere** — A frozen lake in the northern interior. Locals say something sleeps beneath the ice. They leave offerings on the shore in winter and don't fish there.

**The Warden's Stones** — Pre-human standing stones on the northern coast. The carvings are weathered beyond reading. Locals leave offerings. The stones are warm to the touch, even in winter.

---

## Autumnvale

_Grid: H5 — Southeastern Ghandara, the Four Vales_

Population roughly 6,000. One of the Four Vales — the fertile lowlands that form Ghandara's agricultural heartland. Autumnvale is known for strong community, large families, and the Church of Enduring Light — an Ilmatari temple tended by Brother Aldous.

The Accord watches Autumnvale closely but hasn't moved against it. The Ilmatari faithful preach patience, compassion, and endurance. They don't fight. They don't organize. They just... endure. The Accord considers them harmless.

The vale produces grain, wool, and people who know how to suffer without breaking. Whether that's obedience or something else entirely is a question the Accord hasn't thought to ask.

---

## Summervale

_Grid: G4–H4 — Eastern Ghandara, the Four Vales_

Population roughly 5,000. The vale that was broken during the Pacification — the period ten to fifteen years ago when the Accord transitioned from missionaries to enforcers. Families were torn apart. Children of "unfit" worshipers were taken to "proper" institutions. Records were sealed. The fields are productive. The people are hollow.

Summervale doesn't talk about what happened. Not because they've forgotten, but because the people who ask questions tend to attract attention from people who don't like questions.

### Other Vale Settlements

**Harvestholm** (~1,800) — Market town between the vales. Grain silos, livestock pens, Tyrran factors. Population doubles at harvest.

**Millbrook** (~700) — Quiet village. Apple orchards, cider production. Too small to garrison. Too small to matter.

**Shepherd's Cross** (~400) — Crossroads village for drovers. Helmite waystation — an easy posting that nobody's worried about.

### The Wintervale Question

There were once Four Vales. Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter.

_"Spring and Summer, Autumn's gold / Winter's tale is never told."_

The children's rhyme is all most people know. Maps made after the Pacification show empty hills where Wintervale was. The land today is grassland — no wildflowers, no trees, no wildlife. Locals don't go there. Some claim to see festival lights on winter nights, but locals claim a lot of things after enough drink.

Ask about Wintervale in the wrong company and the conversation ends. Ask in the right company and you might hear a name: Elara Dawnlight. A Lliiran priestess. A shrine that wouldn't fall.

But that's just a story. Wintervale is just empty hills.

---

## Death's Edge

_Grid: H2 — Northeastern coast, Briarholt Expanse_

Population roughly 800. A mining town on the northeastern cliffs. Iron, copper, and — recently — something else. The mine has been active for decades, unremarkable, producing ore that goes to Thornhaven's shipyards.

Six months ago, a Banite overseer arrived. Nobody requested one. Nobody knows exactly why he's here. The mining operation has been partially restricted — certain shafts are off-limits now, accessible only to Accord-authorized personnel.

Workers die at uncomfortable rates. The overseer files reports. The reports go to Accord's Rest.

Death's Edge is a long way from anywhere important. That's probably why whatever's happening there hasn't attracted more attention. Or it's why it was chosen.

---

## Thornhaven

_Grid: G5 — Southern coast_

Population roughly 7,000. Ghandara's shipbuilding center. Thornwood timber arrives from Briarholt and becomes vessels — fishing boats, merchant ships, and increasingly, Accord patrol craft. Master Korran Deepkeel, a dwarf, runs the Shipwright Guild and is caught between guild loyalty and Accord pressure to prioritize military construction.

A Banite "observer" has been inspecting naval construction. Nobody invited him. He takes notes.

Thornhaven is busy, practical, and determinedly focused on work. The kind of town where people keep their heads down and their hands busy because busy hands don't attract attention.

---

## Kell's Landing

_Grid: F5–G5 — Southern coast, campaign starting point_

Population roughly 8,000. A trade hub and the party's starting point. Not the capital, not the frontier — somewhere in between, where the Accord has real presence but life mostly goes on.

**The Unsleeping Bastion** — The Helmite chapterhouse. A squat stone fortress near the harbor, always lit, always manned. Commander Serelith Vane runs it with forty Helmites. Professional, humorless, thorough.

**The Salt Court** — Tyrran courthouse. Magistrate Aldwin Cross, in his sixties, has been judging cases for thirty years. He applies the law. Only the law. In the current climate, that's almost kindness.

**The Scaled Anchor** — The main tavern. Gresh, a half-orc, sees everything from behind the bar and comments on nothing.

**Harbormaster's Tower** — Elena Wavecrest controls the harbor and takes bribes from the right people.

**The Nets** — The lower district. Three-Coin Mira's territory — a Tymoran gambler whose luck funds things the Accord would rather not know about.

**Temple Row** — Approved temples in a tidy line. An empty lot where a Lliiran shrine once stood. Nobody builds on it. Nobody talks about why.

**The Broken Tower** — An Ilmatari sanctuary. Technically underground, technically unofficial. The Accord knows it exists and considers it harmless.

### Southern Port Settlements

**Tidecrest** (~1,500) — Fishing port and naval waystation. Polite to Helmite sailors. Quiet about everything else.

**Saltmere** (~400) — Tidal community. A market forms at low tide on ground that's technically outside any jurisdiction. Smugglers and undocumented trade.

**Anchor's Rest** (~600) — Where sailors retire. Good place to find someone who knows hidden coves, forgotten routes, and how to keep their mouth shut.

**The Widow's Reach** (~30) — A peninsula lighthouse. The keeper died six months ago. The light still burns. Investigators sent to look into it don't report back.

## Accord's Rest

_Grid: G4 — Eastern shore of the Mirthwater_

Population roughly 45,000. The capital. Formerly Mirahaven, renamed when the Accord established it as their seat of power in Ghandara. Beautiful architecture, orderly streets, hollow festivals, and fear dressed as piety.

Most travelers won't visit Accord's Rest unless they have business there — and business there means business with the Accord. The city is prosperous, thoroughly controlled, and the kind of place where you watch every word because someone else certainly is.

**The High Citadel** — Helmite fortress-temple on the heights above the city. The Eye of Helm stares from the highest tower. Dungeons seven levels deep.

**The Hall of Scales** — Tyrran courthouse complex. Public trials. Swift sentencing.

**The Black Fist Garrison** — Banite compound, eastern edge of the city. Growing. An inquisitor named Malrek Thorne arrived six months ago. He speaks softly and asks questions that make even Accord faithful nervous.

**The Memorial of Order** — Public square. Statues of Helm, Tyr, and Bane. An eternal flame "in memory of those lost to chaos." Locals avoid lingering.

From certain vantage points in Accord's Rest, you can see across the Mirthwater to Joybright. The ruins. The empty streets. The festival lanterns that still hang from their hooks.

The capital stares at the city it killed. On quiet nights, the soldiers on watch say they can hear laughter across the water.

## How the Accord Works

If you live in Ghandara, here's what the Accord means for your daily life:

**Helmite patrols** walk the streets in pairs. Armored, visible. The point is that you see them.

**Checkpoints** at harbors, gates, and district boundaries. Cargo checked, travelers questioned, contraband confiscated.

**The Tyrran courts** handle everything from theft to heresy. The process is fair. The laws are not.

**Approved worship** means temples to Accord-sanctioned gods. You can worship Helm, Tyr, Sune, Lathander, Eldath. You need a license. You need to register your shrine. The registration forms ask for names and addresses.

**Forbidden items** include scarlet ribbons or cloth, unauthorized holy symbols, the Book of Exultation, certain songs (the list grows regularly), dancing masks, and any depiction of Lliira.

**Sentencing** ranges from fines for unlicensed worship to death for heresy. Most people never see the worst of it. Most people are careful enough not to.

The further you are from Accord's Rest, the lighter the grip. The outer islands and open sea are relatively free. The highlands answer to the clans. The northern forests are too vast to patrol. But in the cities and the vales, the Accord is the air you breathe — invisible until you try to move against it.

# PART TWO: DM EYES ONLY

_Everything below this line is information the players do not have. Secrets, hidden agendas, plot threads, and the truth behind the things described above._

---

## The Truth About Wintervale

Wintervale didn't just resist. It _succeeded_, briefly. Elara Dawnlight — a Lliiran priestess — channeled enough divine power to hold off the Banite assault for three days. Something happened during that stand that the Accord has worked to erase from every record.

**Elara didn't die.** She transcended, or was taken, or became something else. The land isn't empty — it's _waiting_. The festival lights on winter nights are real: echoes of a celebration that never ended, playing in a place adjacent to reality. Elara Dawnlight is still celebrating somewhere just out of reach.

**Campaign use:** Wintervale is a beacon. If the Lastbloom is planted there, or if enough joy is kindled in that place, the echo might bleed through. Wintervale could return. Or something could come _out_.

---

## What's at Death's Edge

Six months ago, miners struck a chamber. Ancient, pre-human, sealed with magic predating the gods' current forms. The Accord sent scholars, priests, and finally Inquisitor Thorne. The chamber contains a fragment of something _old_. Old enough to remember when gods could die — because it killed some of them, long ago.

**This is why Thorne is really in Ghandara.** Not for dissenters — for a _weapon_. Bane wants the ability to kill gods permanently, without the messy coalition effort that Lliira's execution required. If he gets it, no dissenter god is safe.

**The ticking clock:** If the party doesn't act, Bane gets his weapon. If Bane gets his weapon, the campaign shifts from resistance to survival.

---

## The Joybright Anomaly

Joybright isn't just haunted — it's _protected_. Lliira's death released a final gift into the city. The buildings don't decay because joy preserves them. The laughter at night is real — echoes of ten thousand festivals.

And something is waking up. Not Lliira — she's truly dead. But divinity leaves marks. Enough worship in one place for enough centuries creates _possibilities_. The Accord's garrison isn't there to keep people out. It's there to keep something in.

**Campaign use:** If the Lastbloom reaches Joybright, if the party can kindle genuine joy in the city, the something might fully awaken. A new entity? A fragment of Lliira reborn? A spirit of joy itself, without a god's form but with a god's power? This is endgame territory.

---

## Bane's Actual Plan

The Accord was always Bane's tool. Helm and Tyr provide legitimacy and manpower, but they also _limit_ him. Bane's long game:

1. **The Death's Edge weapon** — A means to kill gods without coalition. Once Bane has this, he doesn't need consensus.
2. **Discredit Helm** — Maneuvering Helmites into positions where they must commit atrocities that fracture the order. The more Helmites doubt, the weaker Helm becomes.
3. **Subvert Tyr** — Banite agents within the Tyrran courts push legal precedents that expand Banite authority. Each "emergency measure" becomes permanent.
4. **Eliminate dissenters permanently** — Lliira was the test case. Tymora, Mask, Selûne are next.
5. **The endgame** — A world where Bane is the _only_ god that matters. Not through worship — through _dependency_.

---

## Ilmater's Counter-Plan

Ilmater has been watching. Suffering. Enduring. For forty years, he's accepted punishment rather than enforce the Accord. The other gods think he's broken.

He's not broken. He's been _paying attention_.

His faithful — the **Unbroken** — are positioned across Ghandara. Brother Aldous in Autumnvale. The Broken Tower keeper in Kell's Landing. Others, unnamed, in every vale and village where suffering exists and someone chooses to bear it for others.

Their theology: suffering should be endured, not inflicted. The Accord inflicts suffering constantly. Therefore, opposing the Accord doesn't violate Ilmater's teachings — it fulfills them.

When the time comes — when the party has earned it — Ilmater will act. Not with force. With _moral authority_. The weight of a god who has suffered everything the Accord has inflicted and refused to break.

---

## Helm's Secret

Commander-General Aldric Vorn, the highest-ranking Helmite in Ghandara, has heard Helm's voice exactly twice. The second time, three years ago, it said only two words: _"I'm sorry."_

He has told no one.

## NPC Hidden Agendas

### Accord Leadership

**Commander-General Aldric Vorn** (High Vigilant, Accord's Rest) — Not cruel. Utterly convinced the Accord is necessary. Impeccable logic, devastating results. Haunted by Helm's apology. Doesn't know what to do with it.

**High Magistrate Seraphina Cole** (Tyrran, Accord's Rest) — Severe, precise. "The law is clear." Troubled by cases involving children of dissenters. Duty wins. Every time. For now.

**Inquisitor Malrek Thorne** (Banite, Accord's Rest / Death's Edge) — Handsome, soft-spoken, terrifying. His real mission is the Death's Edge weapon. Everything else — the dissenter investigations, the political maneuvering — is cover.

**High Watcher Varanthas Morn** (Supreme Helmite, Sword Coast) — Present at Lliira's execution. Has not smiled since. Whether this reflects doubt or satisfaction, no one knows. Not even him.

### Kell's Landing

**Commander Serelith Vane** (Helmite, Unsleeping Bastion) — Professional, humorless, committed to "protecting mortals from themselves." Not looking for reasons to arrest people — but she'll find them if they're there.

**Magistrate Aldwin Cross** (Tyrran, Salt Court) — Thirty years of judging. Applies only the law. In the current climate, this is an act of quiet mercy — he sentences exactly what the statute requires, never more. He knows what he's doing.

**Brother Aldric** (Helmite Vigilant) — Young, earnest, by-the-book. Genuinely believes he's helping. Hasn't had to make a hard choice yet. When he does, it'll define him.

**Sister Maren** (Helmite Watcher) — Gate duty. Tired. Does her job, goes home, doesn't ask questions she doesn't want answered. Has "failed to notice" more minor infractions than she can count.

**Brother Fell** (Helmite, retired) — Was a Helmite before the Accord. Tends the Bastion's shrine. His eyes go troubled during services. He remembers what the Order was supposed to be.

**Three-Coin Mira** (Tymoran, The Nets) — Resistance funding through gambling luck. Sister arrested for a Lliiran hymnal. Smile doesn't reach her eyes. Dangerous, dedicated, running on fury.

### Highland Leaders

**Thane Bràghad Mòr** (Clan Mòr, Duncraig) — Playing a long game. The Accord thinks he cooperates. He thinks he's waiting them out. His heir has vanished — he suspects the Accord but can't act openly.

**Thane Skara Aeris** (Clan Aeris, Eaglerest) — Convinced rebellion is coming. Actively preparing for it. She may be right, but she may also get a lot of people killed.

**Elder Moira Dunne** (Clan Dunne, Coldwell) — Eighty years old. Remembers everything. Waiting for the right moment to share what she knows about Wintervale.

**"The Fenn"** (Clan Fenn, Blackmoor) — Identity unknown. Practices old magic. The bog has its own rules. Accord patrols don't come back from Blackmoor, and nobody asks why.

### Dissenter Network

**The Whisper** (Maskarran, unknown location) — Leader of the Beneath, the smuggler/spy network under Accord's Rest. Identity unknown, possibly multiple people. The Accord has executed three claimed Whispers. The network keeps operating.

**Brother Aldous** (Ilmatari, Autumnvale) — Gentle voice, calloused hands. Preaches patience. Part of the Unbroken network. Denies knowledge of everything while knowing everything.

**Corvin Ashdale** (Kell's Landing) — Half-elf dockworker, dissenter smuggler network contact. Introduced in session.

### Merchants

**Guildmaster Rowan Ashford** (Timber Guild, Briarholt) — Plays the Accord's game. Protects guild interests. Has secrets the party can leverage or that can be leveraged against them.

**Master Korran Deepkeel** (Shipwright Guild, Thornhaven) — Caught between guild loyalty and Accord pressure. Could go either way.

**Factor Lyanna Goldworth** (Merchant Consortium, multiple locations) — Knows everything that moves across the island. Sells information to anyone who pays. No loyalty except to coin.

### Session NPCs

**Tessa Brightwell** — Sailed from Chult with 30 Lliiran pilgrims seeking the Festival. Husband Marren arrested ten years ago. Hasn't prayed since. Scarlet thread in her apron ties.

**Marren Brightwell** — Tessa's husband. Found at Thornhaven with Karsa. How he's free is a question with uncomfortable answers.

**Nessandrle** — Compliance Ministry bard. Glamour College. Sent to neutralize the party. Status unclear after tavern fight. Cyrican influence.

**Seraph** — Aasimar at the Golden Chain. Not Accord-affiliated — something older. Judges moral weight. Hunting someone specific.

**Karsa** — Soldier traveling with Marren. Down after fighting Nessandrle.

## The Shadow Apparatus (What Players Don't See)

**Sharrans** — "Grief counselors" embedded in communities. They befriend the despairing, note who still has spirit, and report everything. They cultivate hopelessness to weaken resistance.

**Loviatans** — Specialist interrogators. Called in for prisoners who hold something truly valuable. They are artists. They take their time.

**Cyricans** — Disinformation, entrapment, agents provocateur. The Compliance Ministry operates with heavy Cyrican influence. They pose as fellow dissenters, spread false rumors about safe houses, forge evidence.

## Secret Locations

**The Beneath** (Under Accord's Rest) — Officially sewers. Actually Maskarran smuggling network, safe houses, spy ring. The Whisper commands. The Accord has tried to clear it three times. It fills back up.

**The Twilight Market** (Accord's Rest) — Semi-legal evening market. Licensed goods above board, everything else below. Raided occasionally, always reforms.

**The Sunken Temple** (Joybright) — Partially collapsed temple. Helmite wards on the doors have been replaced four times. Something keeps breaking them from _inside_.

**The Last Festival Ground** (Joybright) — A field where only uniform grass grows. Anyone with divine sensitivity hears distant music.

**Piety's Rest** (~2,000, near Accord's Rest) — Monastery-town. Religious schools and "re-education" facilities. Some who enter don't emerge.

**The Widow's Reach** (Southern coast) — Lighthouse. Keeper dead six months. Light still burns. Investigators don't report back. Something is maintaining the light, and it isn't human.
