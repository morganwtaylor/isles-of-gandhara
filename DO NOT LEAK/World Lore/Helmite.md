 **The Helmite Order in the Age of Accord**

---

**Helm, the Watcher**

God of guardians, protectors, and vigilance. He Who Sees. The Eternal Sentinel.

Helm is duty incarnate. He does not sleep, does not waver, does not question the charge he is given. He stood guard at the gates of the celestial planes while other gods fought and schemed and fell. He has never abandoned a post. He has never failed a task assigned to him.

This is his greatest virtue. It is also the wound at the center of his faith.

Helm held Lliira while she died. He restrained a goddess of joy and freedom so that Loviatar could kill her slowly. He did not do this out of cruelty or hatred. He did it because he was commanded to. Because the Accord required it. Because duty, to Helm, supersedes love, conscience, and doubt.

His faithful believe this was righteous. Most of them. The ones who don't are learning to stay quiet.

---

**The Helmite Creed**

_"The Watcher's eyes never close. The Watcher's grip never falters. The Watcher's duty never ends."_

Core tenets as taught in Helmite doctrine:

- **Duty is sacred.** A task accepted is a task completed. Abandoning a charge is the gravest sin.
- **Vigilance protects.** Evil flourishes when the watchful sleep. Complacency is the enemy.
- **Protection requires strength.** The weak cannot guard the weak. Power in service of protection is holy.
- **Order enables safety.** Chaos breeds danger. Structure, hierarchy, and law create the conditions for security.
- **Obedience honors trust.** Those who give orders bear responsibility; those who follow demonstrate faith.

Before the Accord, Helmites were bodyguards, castle wardens, sentinel knights who protected the vulnerable. They were respected, even loved. A Helmite at your door meant you were safe.

Now a Helmite at your door might mean you're under arrest.

**The Order's Structure**

**Watchers** — Rank-and-file faithful. Guards, soldiers, patrol officers. They walk beats, man checkpoints, stand at doors.

**Vigilants** — Experienced Helmites who have proven their dedication. Squad leaders, gate captains, shrine guardians. Most Helmite paladins hold this rank or higher.

**Ever-Seeing** — Senior officers. They command chapterhouses, oversee regions, coordinate with Tyrran judges and Banite authorities. Political as well as martial.

**The Lidless** — Elite inner circle. Said to have received visions directly from Helm. They set doctrine, interpret the god's will, and answer only to the High Watcher.

**The High Watcher** — Supreme authority of the Helmite faith. Currently **Varanthas Morn**, a grim older human who was present at Lliira's execution and has not smiled since. Whether this reflects doubt or satisfaction, no one knows.

**Helmites in Ghandara**

The Helmite chapterhouse in Kell's Landing is called the **Unsleeping Bastion**—a squat stone fortress near the harbor, always lit, always manned. Approximately forty Helmites are stationed there, a mix of Watchers, Vigilants, and a single Ever-Seeing commander.

**Commander Serelith Vane** — Ever-Seeing, female half-elf, has served the Bastion for twelve years. Professional, humorless, and deeply committed to the Accord's mission of "protecting mortals from themselves." She is not cruel, but she will not hesitate. She believes doubt is a luxury guardians cannot afford.

**What Helmites Do (Day-to-Day)**

**Patrol and presence.** Helmites walk the streets in pairs, armored, visible. They're not hiding. The point is that you see them and remember the Accord is watching.

**Checkpoint enforcement.** Harbor entry, city gates, district boundaries. They check cargo, question travelers, look for contraband (which now includes Lliiran symbols, unlicensed religious materials, certain songs).

**Protection details.** They still guard people—Accord officials, wealthy faithful, important shipments. This is considered honorable duty.

**Arrest and detention.** When the Tyrran courts issue warrants, Helmites execute them. They bring people in. They don't conduct trials—that's Tyrran jurisdiction—but they handle the physical enforcement.

**Standing watch.** Shrines, courthouses, government buildings. Helmites stand at doors, silent and alert, for hours. They're very good at it.

**Dissenter suppression.** This is the new duty, and it sits uneasily on some. Raiding hidden shrines. Confiscating forbidden materials. Arresting people for worshiping the wrong gods. Some Helmites do this with righteous conviction. Others do it because they were ordered to.

**What Helmites Can Do (Mechanical Abilities)**

Helmite training emphasizes endurance, awareness, and unflinching resolve. Their divine gifts reflect this.

**Common Helmite Abilities (Available to Faithful/Trained)**

- **Watchful Rest.** Helmites can remain alert during long rests, gaining the benefits of rest while maintaining awareness. They sleep with their eyes open—literally, for the devout.
- **Sentinel's Endurance.** Advantage on Constitution saves against exhaustion from lack of sleep, standing watch, or environmental exposure while on duty.
- **The Watcher's Gaze.** Trained Helmites can spend an action studying a creature. They learn if it is armed, armored, and hostile. Higher-ranking Helmites may detect concealed weapons or disguises.

**Helmite Paladin Abilities (Oath of the Watchers/Crown, Reflavored)**

Most Helmite paladins swear oaths that emphasize vigilance and protection. Common oath features:

- **Aura of the Sentinel.** Allies within 10 feet (30 feet at higher levels) gain a bonus to initiative and can't be surprised while the paladin is conscious.
- **Vigilant Rebuke.** When a creature the paladin can see attacks an ally within 30 feet, the paladin can use a reaction to grant disadvantage on the attack roll.
- **Helm's Reproach.** Channel Divinity option. As an action, the paladin targets one creature within 30 feet that has attacked an ally. That creature must make a Wisdom save or be unable to willingly move closer to that ally and have disadvantage on attacks against them for 1 minute.
- **Unyielding Guard.** At higher levels, Helmite paladins can use a reaction to reduce damage dealt to an adjacent ally, taking some of the damage themselves.
- **The Lidless Vigil.** High-level ability. The paladin cannot be surprised, blinded, or put to sleep by magical means. They can sense invisible creatures within 30 feet.

**Helmite Clerics (Protection/Order Domain)**

Helmite clerics focus on warding, shielding, and bolstering allies. Common spell preparations:

- _Shield of faith, sanctuary, warding bond_ (low level)
- _Hold person, zone of truth, magic circle_ (mid level)
- _Banishment, death ward, wall of force_ (high level)

**Domain Ability: Interpose.** When an ally within 5 feet is attacked, the cleric can use a reaction to become the target instead.

**Channel Divinity: Helm's Protection.** As an action, create a 10-foot radius zone centered on the cleric. Allies in the zone gain +2 AC and advantage on saves against being moved or knocked prone. Lasts 1 minute or until the cleric moves.

**Helmite Equipment and Symbols**

**The Staring Eye** — Helm's holy symbol. An unblinking eye, often depicted on a gauntlet or shield. Helmites wear this prominently.

**Watchplate** — Standard Helmite armor is practical half-plate or splint, always well-maintained, usually bearing the eye symbol on the breastplate or pauldron. The color is steel gray with accents of dark blue.

**The Vigil Candle** — A thick candle that burns for exactly 8 hours. Helmite tradition holds that if the candle gutters before its time, evil is near.

**Sentinel's Cord** — A braided cord worn at the wrist, given when a Helmite completes their first year of service. Each additional five years adds another strand.

**Forbidden Items Helmites Confiscate:**

- Scarlet ribbons, flowers, or cloth (Lliiran association)
- Unauthorized holy symbols (any dissenter god)
- The _Book of Exultation_ (Lliira's holy text)
- Certain songs (a list exists; it grows regularly)
- Dancing masks (traditional Lliiran festival gear)
- Any depiction of Lliira herself

**The Fracture Within**

Not every Helmite is comfortable with what the Order has become. The faith has always been about protecting people. Now it's about controlling them. For some, the logic holds: control _is_ protection. Mortals left to their own devices make dangerous choices. The Accord is a shield against chaos.

For others, the logic is crumbling.

**Signs of Doubt (For NPCs or a Helmite PC):**

- They hesitate a half-second too long before following orders
- They "fail to notice" minor infractions
- They ask questions about _why_ instead of just _how_
- They remember what the Order was like before and can't stop comparing
- They heard Lliira's execution described and something in them went cold

**The Dangerous Question:**

_"What happens when duty requires evil?"_

Orthodox Helmite doctrine says: duty is never evil if the authority is righteous. The Accord is righteous. Therefore duty to the Accord is righteous.

But Helm held a goddess of joy while she was tortured to death.

If that was righteous, what does righteousness mean?

If it wasn't—if even Helm can be made to serve cruelty—then what is the Order actually protecting?
