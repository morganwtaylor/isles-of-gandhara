# ACT 1: THE WEEPING STONES

## Paul's Attunement

**Duration:** 1 hour of reading aloud. This is a table moment — don't rush it.

**What Paul Experiences:**

- Vivid emotional memories flood through him — not his own. Fragments of thousands of celebrations, dances, moments of pure joy across centuries
- Festivals in cities he's never visited. Songs in languages he doesn't speak. The feeling of dancing with strangers and meaning it
- The Book _knows_ him. It resonates with his genuine faith in a way it never did for Tessa. Entire sections that were hidden for a decade become visible

**What the Alcove Does:**

- The salt-stain tears on the cliff walls start running fresh — not salt, **warm water**. The stones weep with joy, not grief, for the first time since Lliira died
- The alcove warms. The smell of honey wine and summer festivals fills the space
- Faint golden light traces old carvings on the walls — **pilgrim prayers** scratched into the rock over centuries. Hundreds of them. This place has been holy longer than anyone knew
- The Lastbloom (if Lil Moon is present) blooms wider. Scarlet deepens. Both artifacts sing to each other

**The Reveal — The Pilgrim Map:** Once attunement completes, the golden light settles into one specific carving. Not a prayer — a **map**. A pilgrim route scratched into the stone showing a hidden path from the Weeping Stones directly into Joybright, bypassing the main approaches. An old pilgrim's road that predates the Accord.

> The Book lit it up because Paul is the first genuine seeker to attune here.

**Tactical value:** A way into the city that avoids the garrison's main watch points. **Spiritual value:** Centuries of faithful came before them. They're not alone.

**If Lastbloom is within 30 ft of Book:** The Lastbloom advances one tier (Dormant → Awakened). This is enormous:

- Advantage on frightened saves
- Can end frightened/charmed
- Calm Emotions 1/long rest
- Hymn of the Lastlight (temp HP to allies)

# ACT 2: THE HIDDEN PATH INTO JOYBRIGHT

## The Pilgrim's Road

The map reveals a narrow coastal path that descends from the Weeping Stones, follows the cliff base through sea caves at low tide, and emerges inside Joybright's eastern residential quarter — behind the garrison's main sight lines.

**Atmosphere — describe this carefully:**

- The path is worn smooth by centuries of pilgrim feet
- Small offerings line the route — coins, flowers (dried but not rotted), prayer beads, carved wooden dancing figures
- As they get closer, the Lastbloom glows brighter. Lil Moon can feel a compass-pull toward the city center
- The Book grows warm in Paul's hands. Pages flutter on their own
- They can hear it before they see it — **faint laughter**. Distant. Musical. Heartbroken but real

**What Joybright Looks Like (IMPORTANT — adjust from old notes):** This is NOT decades of quiet preservation. This is a **freshly sacked city**:

- Festival lanterns torn down and smashed in the streets
- Temple doors broken open, sacred objects scattered
- Signs of recent violence — bloodstains not yet washed by rain, broken furniture dragged into barricades
- Some buildings burned. Smoke stains on stone
- Bodies may have been removed but the evidence of death is everywhere

**BUT — the city is resisting:**

- Golden light seeps through cracks in damaged walls
- Smashed lanterns flicker with phantom light
- Flowers — scarlet, impossible — push up through broken cobblestones
- The laughter is real. Faint, wounded, but _present_
- Buildings the Accord smashed are not rebuilding, but the damage won't _settle_. Cracks fill with warmth. Broken stone hums

The garrison is spooked. They broke this city and it won't stay broken.

# ACT 3: THE THREE-WAY FIGHT

## How It Starts

The party enters through the eastern residences via the pilgrim path. They move through the quiet streets toward the city center. The Lastbloom and Book are both reacting — pulling them toward the Sunken Temple on the western cliff.

**To reach the Sunken Temple, they must cross the Festival Square** — the most patrolled area.

### The Guard Encounter

A **Square Patrol** spots them. Four Helmite soldiers, nervous, weapons drawn.

> "Halt! By order of the Accord, this is a restricted zone! Identify yourselves!"

These soldiers are not fanatics. They're scared conscripts on a punishment posting in a city that whispers to them at night. They've been told to keep pilgrims out. They've never actually had to fight anyone here.

**Social options (before combat):**

|Approach|DC|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|Bluff — "We're reinforcements"|DC 14 Deception|Easier if they have any Helmite gear from previous encounters|
|Intimidate|DC 12|These soldiers are already terrified. Pushing them hard could cause a rout|
|Persuade — "We're not your enemy"|DC 16 Persuasion|Hard sell. They have orders|
|Show the Book / Lastbloom|No roll|Immediate escalation. Soldiers panic. One runs for the garrison|

**If social fails or combat starts:**

Initiative. But the fight doesn't stay a 2-way fight for long.

### The Sorrowsworn Emerge

**Trigger: Round 2 or 3 of combat.** The violence, fear, and grief concentrated in a freshly massacred city reaches critical mass. The negative emotions **birth monsters**.

> The air goes cold. Not gradually — instantly, like a door opening onto winter. The shadows between buildings _stretch_. The faint laughter that haunts the streets goes silent. Something is feeding.

> The soldiers feel it first. One drops his sword. Another screams. Because the shadows behind the party are moving, and they have arms, and those arms are reaching.

**What spawns depends on what emotion is dominant:**

Deploy **2-3 Sorrowsworn** from the options below, scaling to party strength. The recommended mix for a hard, climactic fight: **1 Lonely + 1 Wretched swarm + 2-3 Lost**.

## JOY WEAKNESS (ALL SORROWSWORN)

This is the mechanic that makes the fight winnable:

> **Genuine Joy Within 30 ft:** All Sorrowsworn have **disadvantage on all attack rolls and saving throws** while any creature within 30 feet is experiencing genuine joy.

**What counts as genuine joy:**

- Paul reading from the Book of Exultation
- Paul using Hymn of Exultation or The Last Dance
- The Lastbloom's Hymn of the Lastlight
- Murdock playing the lute with genuine feeling (Performance DC 14)
- Any PC doing something genuinely joyful — singing, dancing, laughing, celebrating in the face of horror
- A soldier putting down their weapon and choosing hope

**What doesn't count:**

- Forced or fake happiness
- Calm Emotions (suppresses emotion — the opposite of what's needed)
- Mechanical buffs without narrative weight

**When joy triggers, describe it:**

> The golden light intensifies. The scarlet flowers in the cobblestones bloom wider. The Lonely _flinches_. Its grip loosens. The shadows thin. For a moment — just a moment — you can hear the laughter again. Not ghostly. Not distant. _Here._

## THE FIGHT — ROUND BY ROUND GUIDE

### Setup

- **Festival Square.** Open plaza, ~100 ft across. Central fountain. Ransacked market stalls provide half cover. Festival stage at north end
- **Initial combatants:** 4 Helmite Watchers + 1 Vigilant (Sera) + 1 Acolyte vs. party
- **Sorrowsworn spawn points:** Shadows between buildings on south and east edges of square

### Round 1: Guard Confrontation

- Guards engage party. Sera orders surrender. Acolyte reaches for sending stone
- If party attacks first, guards fight defensively (Dodge action for soldiers, Sera attacks)
- If party tries to talk, Sera gives them one round — then the Acolyte sends the alert

### Round 2-3: The Temperature Drops

> Read aloud: _"The air changes. You've been in Joybright long enough to feel its rhythm — the warmth, the phantom laughter, the golden light in the cracks. All of it stops. The laughter cuts off mid-note. The flowers in the cobblestones close. Something cold and heavy settles over the square like a wet blanket. The soldiers feel it too. The youngest one drops his sword._

> _And from the shadows between two burned-out shops, something reaches._"

- Deploy **The Lonely** first. It emerges from the darkest shadow and immediately targets the most grief-stricken person in the square — probably a soldier
- **Soldiers panic.** DC 12 WIS save or frightened. Most fail. Two flee. Sera holds
- The fight is now three-way. Everyone for themselves

### Round 4-5: Escalation

- Deploy **2 Lost** from opposite side of square. Stumbling, weeping, dangerous
- Deploy **The Wretched** from cracks in the ground, beneath stalls, from the fountain
- The guards are overwhelmed. Sera is trying to protect her people. She's losing
- The Acolyte is healing soldiers, not fighting the party anymore
- **This is when the party realizes:** swords aren't enough. The Lonely is regenerating. The Lost keep coming. The Wretched swarm over everything

### The Turn — Joy as a Weapon

The party needs to figure out (or be nudged toward) that **joy is the answer**:

- The Book is warm in Paul's hands. Pages flutter to "On Resistance": _"Joy is what you choose when suffering says sit down"_
- The Lastbloom is blazing — Lil Moon can feel it trying to _push outward_, to share its warmth
- If Murdock plays the lute, the Sorrowsworn nearest to him flinches
- If anyone does something genuinely joyful, the golden light returns to the cobblestones around them and the Sorrowsworn recoil

**When Paul reads from the Book or uses Hymn of Exultation:**

> _The golden light erupts from the Book like sunrise. The laughter returns — not ghostly, not distant, but HERE, in this square, as if a thousand festivals are happening simultaneously in an echo chamber. The Lonely screams — not in pain, but in confusion, like a creature of darkness encountering light for the first time. Its grip on the soldier loosens. The shadows thin._

All Sorrowsworn in 30 ft: **disadvantage on everything.**

### The Retreat to the Temple

Even with joy weakening them, the Sorrowsworn are numerous and the city's grief is deep. The fight pushes the party west — toward the Sunken Temple.

**The city guides them:**

- Golden light traces a path along the street — leading west
- Doors that should be blocked swing open
- The Lastbloom pulls physically toward the temple
- The Book's pages turn on their own, all pointing the same direction
- The faint laughter grows louder in the direction of the temple — as if something there is _calling_ them

**As they move through the city fighting, describe:**

- Sorrowsworn emerging from every grief-soaked shadow
- But the golden light spreading behind the party — wherever Paul walks reading from the Book, flowers bloom and shadows retreat
- Soldiers who see this: some flee, some follow. Sera follows
- The Sunken Temple's iron doors come into view — cracked wards leaking golden light, humming audible from the street

# ACT 4: THE SUNKEN TEMPLE & THE CONVERGENCE

## Reaching the Temple

The Helmite wards on the doors are **cracked from the inside**. Golden light pours through the fractures. The humming is a vibration you feel in your bones.

**Breaking the wards:**

- DC 16 Strength check to force the doors
- OR the Lastbloom dissolves the wards on contact (automatic)
- OR Paul presses the Book against the doors and reads aloud — the wards shatter

The doors swing inward. Golden-scarlet light floods out. The air smells of summer and celebration.

**Interior:**

> The temple interior is untouched — the one place in Joybright the Accord couldn't break. Pews draped in scarlet silk. An altar covered in fresh flowers — scarlet, gold, white — that should have died but haven't. Candles burning without consuming wax. And on the altar, hovering inches above the surface, a sphere of golden light the size of a fist.

> It's warm. It's pulsing. It's _laughing_.

**This is the Echo.** Not Lliira — she's truly dead. A fragment of her divinity, preserved where she was most loved, fed by the building's own memory. An ember so hot it's almost a flame.

**The Sorrowsworn cannot enter.** The temple threshold is consecrated ground. They press against the doorway, reaching, wailing, but they can't cross. The party has a moment of safety.

But only a moment. The Sorrowsworn are massing outside. The grief of an entire murdered city is coalescing. If the party doesn't act, the things will overwhelm the wards through sheer weight of sorrow.

---

## THE CONVERGENCE — Step by Step

This is the endgame sequence. Run it with weight. Take your time.

### Step 1: The Book Comes Home

When Paul brings the Book across the temple threshold:

> _The Book grows hot — not painful, but alive. You feel a heartbeat through the leather. Not yours. Every page becomes legible at once. Hidden passages, concealed chapters, text invisible for a decade — all of it opens. The Book holds nothing back within these walls._

> _The evocation aura doesn't just radiate — it fills the entire temple. Detect magic would be blinding. The Book is resonating with the building it was written in._

**Mechanical:** All of Paul's spell slots restore. All features recharge. The Book is at maximum power.

### Step 2: The Book Opens Itself

> _The pages turn without being touched. They settle on a passage that has never been visible before. The handwriting is neither Marren's nor Tessa's. It is elegant, rhythmic, written with a joy that bleeds through the ink. Lliira's own hand:_

Read this to Paul's player aloud:

> _"You brought me home. I wrote these words in this room, at this table, looking out at the water. I could hear the Festival from here. I could always hear it. I wrote between songs. I danced between pages. This Book is not a record of joy. It is joy itself, pressed into vellum and bound in the color of my heart."_

> _"I am dead. I know this. I felt it happen. It hurt less than I expected and more than I can describe. But I wrote this Book knowing that one day I might not be here to read it aloud. So I wrote it for you. Whoever you are. Whatever brought you here."_

> _"Read it. Read it aloud. Read it to anyone who will listen. And when you are done, do not close the cover. Leave it open. Joy was never meant to be shut away."_

**Mechanical:** The Book advances to Exalted (if not already). If the Lastbloom is present within 30 ft, it also advances one tier.

### Step 3: The Lastbloom Meets the Echo

If Lil Moon brings the Lastbloom to the altar:

> _The flower goes wild. It opens wider than it ever has — not just a flower now, but a living vessel of an emotion so powerful it borders on divinity. The scarlet is so deep it's almost black. The golden sphere on the altar pulses faster, brighter, in rhythm with the Lastbloom's heartbeat._

> _They recognize each other. Voice and ember. The Book and the Flower. Lliira's words and Lliira's last spark._

### Step 4: Paul Reads Aloud — The Last Dance

This is the moment. Paul needs to read from the Book. Aloud. In the temple where it was written. With the Lastbloom blazing and the Echo pulsing and an army of grief-monsters pressing against the doors.

**Tell Paul's player:** "The Book is open. The words are there. What do you do?"

If Paul reads aloud — and he will — the Convergence triggers:

> _The Unwritten Chapter fills. Not one line at a time — all at once. Golden ink appears across blank pages, wet and shining, the words pouring out as if someone has been holding their breath since the day they died:_

Read this to the table. All of it. Slowly:

> _"You came. I wasn't sure anyone would."_

> _"I can't come back. What dies divinely stays dead. The other gods made sure of that — Bane's sentence, Helm's chains, Loviatar's blade. There is no loophole. There is no resurrection. I am gone."_

> _"But."_

> _"I wrote this Book. I planted that flower. I loved this city. And love — real love, the kind that makes you dance with strangers and sing when you're afraid and laugh at funerals because the dead would want you to — love doesn't need a goddess to survive. It just needs someone willing to carry it."_

> _"So here is my last gift. Not power. Not divinity. Something better."_

> _"Permission."_

> _"You have permission to be joyful. In the dark. In the danger. When the Accord says kneel and the Inquisitor says confess and the world says that happiness is too small a thing to die for."_

> _"It isn't small. It was never small. It is the biggest thing there is."_

> _"Now close this Book. Not because joy should be shut away — but because you don't need it anymore. You never did. Everything I wrote in these pages, you already knew. I just reminded you."_

> _"Go. Dance. Sing. Laugh. Defy every god who ever told a mortal that joy was dangerous."_

> _"They were right. It is."_

> _"That's the point."_

### Step 5: The Wave

> _The golden light erupts. Not from the Book — from everything. The altar. The flowers. The candles. The walls. The floor. The ceiling. The Echo on the altar blazes white-gold and EXPANDS — not an explosion but a wave, a tide, rolling outward from the temple through the doors and into the streets of Joybright._

> _The Sorrowsworn at the threshold dissolve. Not killed — transformed. The Lonely unravels into wisps of shadow that thin and fade. The Lost stop stumbling, stand still, and simply cease to exist, like grief that's finally been acknowledged. The Wretched swarm breaks apart into individual motes of light that rise and scatter._

> _The wave keeps going. Through the Festival Square. Through the eastern residences. Through the harbor. Every building it touches blooms — golden light in the cracks, scarlet flowers erupting from stone, the sound of laughter filling the streets. Not ghostly. Not echoing. ALIVE._

> _Soldiers in the garrison feel it hit. Some weep. Some laugh. Some do both. Captain Delia Sworn, standing at her desk reviewing duty rosters, feels warmth flood through her chest and sits down hard, because for the first time in three years she's smiling and she doesn't know why._

> _The Echo is spent. The golden sphere on the altar is gone — not destroyed, but distributed. It's in the Lastbloom now, and in the Book, and in the stones of Joybright, and in every person who was in this room when it spoke._

> _The city is quiet for the first time since they arrived. Not silent. Quiet. Peaceful. The kind of quiet that comes after music ends and everyone is still smiling._

**Mechanical Effects:**

- All Sorrowsworn in Joybright are destroyed
- All creatures in the city are healed 3d8+5 HP
- All fear, charm, and despair effects in the city end
- The Book reaches its Final State — The Open Book. It cannot be closed
- Joybright is now a **permanent sanctuary** (see Book stat block for details)
- Every soldier in the garrison must make a **DC 18 WIS save** or be overcome with joy for 1 hour (not charmed — genuinely, involuntarily _happy_)

# ACT 5: THE AFTERMATH — PAUL'S CHOICE

## The Quiet After

The fight is over. The city is changed. Give the table time to breathe.

**What they see:**

- Scarlet flowers blooming everywhere — from stone, from rubble, from the cracks in damaged buildings
- The fountain in the Festival Square running clear, warm water
- Festival lanterns relighting — phantom flames, golden, steady
- People appearing at the edges of the city — pilgrims, refugees, followers of Lliira who were drawn here. They heard something. They felt something. They came
- Soldiers sitting on the ground, dazed, some crying, some laughing

**Sera Coldwell** approaches the party. Weapon sheathed. Hands empty.

> "What was that? What _are_ you people?"

She's not hostile. She's shaken to her foundations. Everything she was taught about Joybright — that it's dangerous, that the laughter is a threat, that the Accord's mission here is righteous — just crumbled.

**Captain Delia Sworn** arrives 15-20 minutes later. Three years of commanding this garrison. Three years of hearing laughter and being told it was the enemy. She walks into the Festival Square, sees the flowers, sees the light, sees her soldiers sitting peacefully for the first time since they were posted here.

> She looks at Paul. At the Book that won't close. At the Lastbloom blazing in Lil Moon's hands.

> "I was told to keep something contained. I think I've been keeping it prisoner."

**Her choice:** Delia can become an ally or an obstacle. If the party treats her with compassion, she defects. She sends the garrison home. She stays. She was never a true believer — she was a soldier following orders in a place that made those orders feel more wrong every day.

## Paul's Choice

The city needs a keeper. The Book is open. The sanctuary needs someone to tend the flame.

**Present this honestly. Don't force it.**

### If Paul Stays

> The Book of Exultation rests open on the altar of the Sunken Temple. Its pages display different passages to everyone who approaches — whatever they need most. Paul tends the temple. Pilgrims come. Refugees settle. Joybright lives again.

> The Accord cannot scry into the city. Joy radiates outward. Every day, more flowers bloom. Every night, the laughter is a little louder, a little less ghostly, a little more real.

> Paul isn't becoming a god. He's becoming a keeper — continuing a story that Lliira started and trusted someone else to finish.

**Campaign impact:** Joybright becomes the party's permanent safe haven. Paul becomes an NPC the party can return to. The Accord now has a problem they can't ignore.

### If Paul Goes

> The Book goes with him. The sanctuary remains — diminished without the Book's presence, but real. The flowers keep blooming. The laughter continues. But without a keeper, Joybright is vulnerable. The Accord can return. The garrison can be reinforced. Joy survives, but it's not safe.

**Campaign impact:** The party keeps Paul and the Book as an asset. Joybright is a temporary victory, not a permanent one. The Accord can reclaim it — and the party will have to decide if that matters enough to come back.

### If Someone Else Stays

Maybe Karsa finds purpose here. Maybe a refugee steps up. Maybe Sera or Delia chooses to guard the city from the inside. This is fine — the Book chose Paul, but the city doesn't require him specifically. It requires _someone who cares_.
