## Session Summary — Where We Are

Two ships drift side by side on the Mirthwater. The Helmite cutter is empty — its crew dead. The refugee vessel carries thirty broken souls, five adventurers, one highland healer, and one killer.

**Marren Brightwell is dead.** Killed by Karsa on the blood-soaked deck moments after turning the tide of battle with a pillar of divine light. The contract from Tonyult — three days to kill Marren — was fulfilled in one.

**The Book of Exultation is ownerless.** It fell from Marren's hands when he died. It still smells of summer festivals and honey wine.

**Tessa Brightwell has shut down.** The woman who built her identity on her husband's stolen faith just watched him murdered after performing the miracle she never could.

**Lil Moon carries the Lastbloom.** The flower pulses faintly below deck. It has found its bearer.

**The party is sailing north toward Joybright** — a ruined holy city behind a Forbidden Zone, guarded by a demoralized garrison that hears laughter at night.

# ACT ONE: BLOOD AND GRIEF

## "The Deck That Won't Dry"

### Opening Scene

The battle is over. The wind pushes both ships slowly north. Blood is drying on the planks. Marren's body lies where it fell.

**What the party sees:**

- Thirty refugees in various states of shock. Some weeping. Some silent. Children clinging to parents
- Tessa above deck, unresponsive. DaVonian standing over his father's body, shaking, not crying — too stunned for tears
- Karsa standing apart, weapon still in hand, blood on his arms. Not fleeing. Not apologizing. Just... standing
- Water Walt tending to the wounded, methodical, grim
- The Helmite cutter bumping gently against their hull. Empty. Drifting
- Lil Moon feels the Lastbloom pulse — once, twice — and then go still. Even the flower is grieving

**Let the players drive this.** Don't narrate past the silence. Let them decide what happens first.

---

### The Karsa Question

This is the session's first major decision point. The party knows (or will learn) that Karsa killed Marren to fulfill a contract with Tonyult. Water Walt and Lil Moon knew about the contract beforehand.

**If the party confronts Karsa:**

> Karsa doesn't flinch. Doesn't explain. Doesn't apologize. If pressed hard enough, he says only: "I owed a debt. The debt is paid. What you do now is your choice."

**If they demand to know why:**

> "Tonyult saved my life. The price was Marren. I told him no. He sent Seraph. You don't say no to Seraph twice."

**If they try to kill or punish him:** Karsa won't fight back. He'll drop his weapon. He's already dead inside. Whatever the party does, he accepts.

|Decision|Consequence|
|---|---|
|**Execute him**|The refugees watch. Some approve. Some are horrified. DaVonian might be the one who asks for it — or the one who begs them to stop|
|**Exile him**|Put him on the cutter. Let him drift. A poetic sentence|
|**Keep him**|Tense. Dangerous. But Karsa is the best fighter they have, and the road ahead is worse than the road behind. Pragmatism vs. justice|
|**Imprison him**|Below deck, bound. The refugees spit at him when they pass. This simmers for days|

**DaVonian's Reaction:** The fifteen-year-old just watched a man murder his father. However the party handles Karsa, DaVonian is watching. This shapes who he becomes.

- If the party shows mercy: DaVonian struggles with it but learns something about grace under impossible circumstances
- If the party is harsh: DaVonian hardens. A seed of vengeance plants itself
- If the party does nothing: DaVonian loses faith in them. Quietly. Permanently

---

### Marren's Body

Someone needs to decide what to do with the body.

**Options:**

- **Sea burial** — Traditional for sailors. Quick, practical. The refugees might want to sing a Lliiran hymn — but the hymns are banned, and singing them on the open water is an act of defiance. _This could be the Lastbloom's first awakening trigger — a moment of joyful defiance in grief_
- **Preserve for Joybright** — Bring his body to the holy city. Bury a prophet in his goddess's grave. Poetically devastating. Practically difficult — a body in this climate has maybe 3 days
- **The Book as shroud** — If someone suggests placing the Book on his chest or wrapping him in it... the Book reacts. Warmth. A single page turns on its own to a passage: _"The dancer's feet remember even when the music stops."_

**Lastbloom Interaction:** If Lil Moon is near the body while holding the Lastbloom, the flower glows faintly — not for Marren (he's gone) but for the grief around him. The flower senses thirty people who _need_ hope. It's the brightest it's ever been.

---

### The Book of Exultation — Who Claims It?

The Book is lying on the deck or clutched in dead hands. It needs a new bearer.

**Who reaches for it matters enormously.**

|Bearer|What Happens|
|---|---|
|**Tessa**|Strongest claim by familiarity. But the Book _knows_ her now. Marren held it and channeled real divine power — the Book felt the difference. If Tessa attunes again, she finds fewer pages legible than before. The Book is pulling away from her|
|**A PC**|The Book responds based on their relationship with joy. Anyone with genuine compassion feels warmth. The margins reveal Marren's handwriting — personal, vulnerable, real. A careful reader (DC 14 Investigation or Religion, 1 hour) realizes the annotations don't match Tessa at all|
|**No one**|The Book lies where it fell. Leaving an artifact unclaimed on a bloody deck is a statement in itself|
|**Near the Lastbloom**|Both artifacts resonate. The flower blooms slightly fuller. The Book's pages flutter to: _"Joy is not owned. Joy is shared. Those who hoard it find only the memory of warmth."_|

---

### Tessa's Silence

Tessa is below deck. She hasn't spoken since Marren fell.

**If a PC goes to her:**

She's sitting on the floor, back against the hull, staring at nothing.

> **First approach:** She doesn't acknowledge the PC. If touched, she flinches. If spoken to directly, a long pause, then: "He was always the better one. I knew that. I always knew that."

> **If pressed:** "Thirty people followed me here because I read his words and pretended they were mine. And the one time he read them himself — the one time they heard the real thing — he died for it. What does that make me?"

> **If the PC is compassionate:** The mask cracks fully. No warmth, no poise, no Prophet. Just Tessa — ordinary, grieving, terrified, _small_. "I can't go up there. They'll look at me and I'll see it in their eyes — they heard him. They _felt_ him. They know I'm not that."

**What Tessa does next depends on the party:**

- If they prop her up: She puts the mask back on. Shakier. Thinner. But functional
- If they expose her: Devastating for morale. The refugees lose their prophet AND their matriarch in one day
- If they ignore her: She emerges eventually, hollow-eyed, resumes the role on autopilot. Worst outcome — a lie maintained by inertia

|Medical supplies|Healer's kit (10 uses), 2 potions of healing|

**The Navigation Charts — CRITICAL FIND:**

- The Forbidden Zone boundary around Joybright's coast
- Patrol schedules for the Weeping Watch's two cutters
- **A gap in coverage** — 4 hours every two days when both cutters dock for resupply at the Watch garrison
- The approach to Greyshore is NOT covered by patrols (considered too far north to matter)

**The Sending Stone — DANGEROUS OPPORTUNITY:** If the party listens, they hear routine Accord naval traffic. Check-ins, weather reports, cargo manifests. Boring — until they hear:

> _"All vessels, priority alert. Cutter Seven, last position southern Mirthwater, missed scheduled check-in. All patrols increase vigilance. Possible weather incident or hostile engagement. Report any sighting."_

That's their cutter. The clock just got louder.

**Decisions about the cutter:**

|Option|Benefit|Risk|
|---|---|---|
|**Scuttle it**|No evidence. Clean break|Lose all supplies, intelligence, second vessel|
|**Strip and scuttle**|Get the goods, lose the evidence|Takes 2-3 hours of time they may not have|
|**Keep it**|Second vessel. Decoy potential|A missing cutter will be hunted|
|**Sail it as decoy**|Send it one direction, refugees go another|Need someone to crew it|
|**Fly the Chains**|Bluff past checkpoints as Helmites|Capital offense. Works until it doesn't. Uniforms are right there|

**Smart play they might not think of:** Use the charts to find the patrol gap, then sink the cutter in deep water far from their heading. No wreckage near their route. Looks like it sank in a storm. No bodies = no murder confirmed — just a lost patrol.

### Destination Details

**JOYBRIGHT — The Holy Ruin (Most Likely)**

_Grid E3-F3. Western shore of the Mirthwater, facing Accord's Rest across ~15-20 miles of water._

2-3 days sail from current position.

**What It Is:** The holy city that was. Lliira's greatest temple city. The Festival of Endless Joy drew pilgrims from across Faerûn every decade. The music never stopped. The music stopped twenty-three years ago.

**What It Looks Like Now:**

- Buildings still stand. They don't decay — nobody knows why
- Dust doesn't settle the way it should. Paint doesn't peel. Festival lanterns still hang from hooks, unlit, unrotted
- The Weeping Watch garrison (~60 soldiers, Captain Delia Sworn) prevents pilgrimage
- Punishment posting. Soldiers hear laughter at night. Transfer requests pile up

**The Secret (DM ONLY):** Joybright isn't just haunted — it's _protected_. Lliira's death released a final gift. The buildings don't decay because joy preserves them. The laughter is real — echoes of ten thousand festivals. Something is waking up. Not Lliira — she's truly dead. But divinity leaves marks. The Accord's garrison isn't there to keep people out. **It's there to keep something in.**

If the Lastbloom reaches Joybright, if the party kindles genuine joy in the city, the something might fully awaken. A new entity? A fragment of Lliira reborn? A spirit of joy without a god's form but with a god's power? **This is endgame territory.**

**The Forbidden Zone:**

- Waters sealed. Patrol ships sink unauthorized vessels
- Two cutters patrol on rotating schedule (the captured charts show this)
- 4-hour gap exists every two days during simultaneous resupply

**Notable Locations Near Joybright:**

|Location|Description|
|---|---|
|**The Weeping Stones**|Coastal cliffs with salt-stain "tears." Secret pilgrim site. Accord knows but can't guard every cliff|
|**The Last Festival Ground**|Only uniform grass grows. Divine sensitivity hears distant music|
|**The Sunken Temple**|Partially collapsed. Helmite wards on doors replaced four times — something keeps breaking them from inside|

**Campaign Hook:** A Weeping Watch soldier deserted, raving about something in the ruins. Hiding in Greyshore.

---

**GREYSHORE — The Staging Point (Practical)**

_North of Joybright on the coast. ~1,100 population._

2 days sail from current position.

- Fishing village. Poor catch since the execution. "The sea itself is grieving"
- Low Accord presence. Quiet. Forgettable
- Good staging point: leave refugees here, strike team goes to Joybright overland
- **Risk:** If the Accord watches Joybright's approaches, Greyshore might be lightly monitored
- **The deserted soldier** from the Weeping Watch is hiding here — raving about what's inside Joybright. Finding this NPC gives crucial intelligence

**Water Walt's Recommendation:** This is what the highland healer would suggest.

**THE HIGHLANDS — Water Walt's Home (Alternative)**

_The Geast Range. Western interior of Ghandara._

Reachable by landing on the western coast and traveling inland. 1-2 weeks overland.

- Clans have never been truly conquered by the Accord
- **The Weeping Pass** connects highlands to Joybright coast, bypassing all checkpoints. Water Walt knows it or knows someone who does
- Potential allies: Clan Aeris (openly defiant), Clan Fenn (mysterious, old ways)
- Thane Braghad Mor of Clan Mor — politically savvy, nominally compliant while agreeing to almost nothing
- **Long-game option.** Park refugees in highland territory. Build alliances. Approach Joybright overland with clan support
- **Death's Edge seed:** Water Walt can mention strange activity at the northeastern mines. Workers go in, fewer come out, Banites won't say why. Plant this — don't force it

Not realistic with refugees. Only if they park refugees somewhere safe and go as a strike team.

- The Beneath (Maskarran underground) could shelter a small group

# POSSIBLE COMBAT ENCOUNTERS

### Encounter 1: Sorrowsworn Emergence (Hard)

**Trigger:** If emotional tension on the ship reaches a breaking point — Karsa confrontation, Tessa's breakdown, refugees arguing about turning back. The oppression of grief literally spawns a monster.

**What Happens:** The air below deck grows cold. Shadows deepen. The sound of weeping echoes from nowhere. Something is feeding on thirty people's concentrated despair.

**A Sorrowsworn (The Lonely)** manifests in the hold among the refugees.

_The Lonely (Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes):_

- AC 12, HP 112, Speed 30 ft
- Harpoon Arm: +7 to hit, 2d10+4 piercing. If Medium or smaller, pulled 30 ft toward it
- Sorrowful Embrace: +7 to hit, 4d6+4 psychic. Target grappled, takes 4d6 psychic at start of each turn
- **Psychic Leech:** While grappling, regains HP equal to psychic damage dealt

**The Horror:** It doesn't attack the armed adventurers first. It reaches for the most grief-stricken refugee — probably DaVonian, or the old woman who still prays to a dead goddess.

**How to Beat It:** Damage works, but the creature regenerates while feeding on sorrow. The _real_ weapon is joy. If someone breaks the despair — a joke, a song, the Lastbloom's touch, a genuine human connection — the Sorrowsworn weakens. **Disadvantage on all attacks and saves while any creature within 30 feet is experiencing genuine joy.**

**Narrative Purpose:** The Accord's oppression has supernatural consequences. Suppress joy long enough and the negative emotions _birth monsters_. Bringing joy to Joybright isn't symbolic — it's survival.
