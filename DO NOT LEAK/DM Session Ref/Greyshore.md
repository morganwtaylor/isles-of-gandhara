# ACT TWO: GREYSHORE

## "A Town That Knows How to Not See Things"

### Arrival

Greyshore sits on a rocky stretch of coast north of Joybright. Low stone buildings, a small harbor with fishing boats, nets drying on racks, the smell of salt and old wood. It's the kind of place people forget exists, which is exactly why it's useful.

**First impression:**

> The harbor is small — built for fishing boats, not pilgrim ships. Your vessel barely fits. A few fishermen on the docks stop mending nets and watch you come in. Nobody waves. Nobody runs. They just... look. The way people look at something they'd rather not have seen but know they can't un-see.

**Accord presence:** Moderate on paper, minimal in practice. Greyshore has a single **Helmite waystation** — a converted fisherman's cottage at the north end of the village with a garrison of exactly **four soldiers**. They rotate monthly from the Weeping Watch garrison. It's considered an even _worse_ posting than Joybright itself — at least the garrison has walls.

**The waystation soldiers:**

- 2 are on patrol along the coastal road (gone until evening)
- 1 is asleep (night shift)
- 1 is sitting outside the waystation, drinking, watching the harbor

**The drinking soldier** sees the ship dock. He's not going to do anything about it immediately. He's been here three weeks and has stopped caring. But if the party does something obviously suspicious (unloading weapons, flying scarlet banners, shouting about Lliira), he'll eventually rouse himself.

### The Village

**Population:** ~1,100. Fishermen, net-makers, salt-workers, a few farmers on the bluffs above town. Everyone knows everyone. Strangers are noticed instantly but not necessarily reported — Greyshore has a long tradition of _not seeing things_.

**Key locations:**

|Location|Description|
|---|---|
|**The Saltstone Inn**|Only tavern. Run by **Eadra Finn**, a widow in her 50s. Stone walls, low ceilings, fish stew that's surprisingly good. Six rooms upstairs. The place where everything happens|
|**The Harbor**|Small, rocky, 8-10 berths. Fishing boats mostly. The harbormaster is **Old Cael** — ancient, one-eyed, knows every ship on this coast by silhouette|
|**The Net-House**|Large storage building at the south end of the harbor. Nets, rope, tackle. **Where Pvt. Aldren Marsh is hiding**|
|**The Waystation**|Converted cottage, north end. Four Helmite soldiers. Minimal effort. A small sending stone for emergencies (paired with Weeping Watch garrison)|
|**The Weeping Stones Trail**|A path leading south along the coastal cliffs toward Joybright. Passes the Weeping Stones (secret pilgrim site). Locals know it well. ~1 day walk to Joybright outskirts|
|**The Bluff Chapel**|A tiny Eldathite shrine on the cliffs above town. Officially approved worship. The priestess, **Sister Corrine**, tends it. She knows more than she lets on|

### Key NPCs

#### Eadra Finn — The Innkeeper

_Human woman, late 50s, weathered, sharp-eyed, warm when she trusts you_

Eadra has run the Saltstone since her husband drowned twelve years ago. She's the real authority in Greyshore — not through title, but through the fact that everyone drinks at her inn and she knows every secret in town.

**What she knows:**

- The soldier hiding in the net-house. She's been leaving food for him. "Poor boy. Whatever he saw down there broke something in him"
- The Weeping Watch garrison is falling apart. Three desertions this quarter. Soldiers who stay are drinking heavily
- Ships sometimes pass in the night, heading south toward the Forbidden Zone. They don't come back. She doesn't ask
- The fish aren't coming. Haven't been for years. "The sea is sick near Joybright. Sick or mourning. The old-timers say the fish left when the music stopped"
- Secret pilgrims still visit the Weeping Stones. She doesn't report them

**What she wants:**

- These strangers to not bring trouble to her village
- Honest payment for rooms and food
- If pressed: "I don't care who you worship. I care about my town. If the Accord comes here because of you, my people pay the price. Keep that in mind"

**Disposition toward refugees:**

> She looks at the children. Her expression changes. "How many?" she asks. Not 'why are they here' or 'who sent you.' Just how many.

If told 19 (13 adults, 6 children):

> "I can't house them all. But the old salt-house hasn't been used since the catch dropped. It's dry, it's warm enough. The children can sleep in the chapel — Sister Corrine won't turn them away." A pause. "How long?"

**If the party is honest about going to Joybright:**

> She goes very still. "South. You're going south." It's not a question. She pours herself a drink — the first time anyone in the inn has seen her drink on shift. "You know what's down there?"

> "Yes, I know the city still stands. Everyone here knows. We don't talk about it. We don't think about it. Because the moment we do, we start _hoping_, and hope is the most dangerous thing on this coast."

#### Old Cael — The Harbormaster

_Human man, 70s, one eye, weathered beyond recognition, knows every ship on the Mirthwater by shape_

Cael has been harbormaster for forty years. He saw the Festival of Endless Joy as a young man. He watched the sky change the day Lliira died. He hasn't been the same since.

**What he notices:**

> He takes one look at the ship and squints his remaining eye. "That's a Chultan-built hull. Deep water keel. Not a fishing boat. Not a trader." He looks at the party. "And you've got Helmite tackle in your rigging." He means equipment from the captured cutter. "Interesting combination."

**What he knows:**

- He can tell them the Forbidden Zone patrol schedule from memory — he's watched the cutters pass for years. Confirms the 4-hour gap from the charts
- He's seen the fog that rolls out from Joybright at night. "Used to just be mist. Last few months it's been _warm_. Smells like my grandmother's kitchen during festival week. I'm seventy-three years old and that smell still makes me want to cry"
- He knows about the Weeping Stones trail — shortest overland route to Joybright. "Half a day if you're quick. Full day if you're careful"

**If asked about Pvt. Marsh:**

> "The soldier?" A long pause. Cael takes his time with everything. "South end. Net-house. Been there about two weeks. Talks to himself. Or talks to something none of us can hear." He holds up a hand before the party asks. "I don't care who he's running from. A man who's that scared is running from something real."

#### Sister Corrine — The Bluff Priestess

_Human woman, 40s, Eldathite, gentle, quiet, keeping more faith alive than the Accord knows_

Corrine tends the Eldathite shrine — approved worship, gentle and harmless. Or so the Accord thinks. In reality, Corrine's chapel is a waypoint for secret pilgrims heading to the Weeping Stones. She doesn't actively recruit, doesn't organize, doesn't resist. She just... leaves the chapel door unlocked at night and doesn't ask who uses it.

**What she knows:**

- The Weeping Stones trail and its significance as a secret pilgrim site
- Something is changing at Joybright. "I tend a shrine to the goddess of peace and still waters. But the water hasn't been still for months. Something is waking, and Eldath weeps for what's coming — not from fear. From _anticipation_"
- She recognizes the Lastbloom immediately if shown. She won't say anything for a long moment. Then: "So it's true. Something survived." Her hands are shaking. "I think I need to sit down"

**What she can do:**

- Shelter the children. No hesitation. No questions
- Provide basic healing supplies and a safe place for refugees to rest
- Bless the party before they go south. It's an Eldathite blessing — peace, calm, still waters — but in the shadow of Joybright, even that feels subversive

**If the Lastbloom is shown:**

> She kneels. Not to the party. To the flower. "Lliira's ember. I always thought it was a story the old pilgrims told themselves to keep going." She looks up. Her eyes are bright. "Take it south. Take it home. Whatever is waking in that city needs this. And we need whatever wakes."

### Private Aldren Marsh — The Deserter

## "He Won't Stop Talking About It"

**Finding him:** The net-house at the south end of the harbor. If the party asks around, they find him within an hour. Everyone in Greyshore knows he's there. Nobody has reported him.

**First impression:**

> The net-house smells of brine and old rope. It's dark. The fishing nets hang from ceiling hooks like curtains, making the interior a maze of fiber and shadow.

> You find him in the back corner. A young man — early twenties, sandy hair, Helmite-issue trousers but no armor, no weapon, no insignia. He's sitting against the wall with his knees drawn up, muttering. His eyes are open but unfocused.

> When he sees you, he doesn't run. He doesn't fight. He just says: "Are you real? You look real. The ones in the city looked real too but they weren't. I don't think they were. I hope they weren't."

### Pvt. Aldren Marsh

_Human man, 22, former Weeping Watch garrison soldier, psychologically broken by what he witnessed in the Sunken Temple_

**Aldren's stats (if needed):**

- **AC** 11 (no armor)
- **HP** 16 (3d8 + 3) — he's been barely eating
- **STR** 13 (+1) **DEX** 12 (+1) **CON** 12 (+1) **INT** 10 (+0) **WIS** 8 (-1) **CHA** 9 (-1)
- Currently has 2 levels of exhaustion (disadvantage on ability checks)
- Condition: frightened (of returning to Joybright), indefinite madness (hallucinations)

**His story (told in fragments — he can't tell it linearly):**

Getting the full story requires patience. He cycles between lucidity and confusion. DC 12 Persuasion to calm him enough for coherent conversation. The Lastbloom's Memory of Joy touch (automatic success — it shows him his happiest memory, breaks through the fear for a few minutes).

**What happened to Aldren:**

He was posted to the Weeping Watch six months ago. Punishment for falling asleep on duty at Helmgate. For the first three months, it was just miserable — bad food, no company, the constant sound of laughter from a city that was supposed to be empty.

Then Captain Sworn sent a patrol to re-ward the Sunken Temple. The wards had broken again — fourth time. Aldren was on the patrol.

**What he saw (delivered in fragments — intercut these with moments of confusion):**

> **Fragment 1:** "The doors were open. The wards were on the ground — not broken. _Dissolved_. Like something had just... breathed on them and they came apart."

> **Fragment 2:** "Inside it was warm. It shouldn't have been warm. The temple's half-collapsed, open to the sky. But it was warm like summer. Like standing in a crowd of people. Except there was nobody there."

> **Fragment 3:** "There's light inside. Golden. Coming from the altar. Not candles — the altar itself. I thought it was enchantment. Some kind of trap. Sergeant Hollis said to leave it, file a report. But I..." He stops. Swallows. "I touched it."

> **Fragment 4:** "I saw _her_. Not — not a woman. A _feeling_. A memory of a feeling. Joy so big it didn't fit inside me. I was laughing and crying and I couldn't stop. Sergeant Hollis pulled me away. I could hear her even after we left. She was — she was _humming_. Inside my head. She's still humming. Right now. Can you hear it?"

> **Fragment 5 (the key intelligence):** "After that night, I couldn't stop hearing it. The music. The laughter. But it wasn't just memories anymore. It was getting _louder_. Like something was waking up. Sergeant Hollis said the same thing. The Captain — Sworn — she pretends she doesn't hear it. But she does. She stands at her window at night and I've seen her _sway_. Like she's dancing. Then she catches herself and her face goes white."

> **Fragment 6:** "I left because..." Tears. Not from sadness. "Because it was going to be _beautiful_. Whatever is waking up in that city is going to be the most beautiful thing anyone has ever seen. And I knew — I _knew_ — that when it happened, the Accord would come and kill it. The way they killed her. And I couldn't watch that happen again."

**What Aldren provides the party:**

|Intel|Detail|
|---|---|
|**Garrison layout**|~57 soldiers remaining (3 desertions). Weeping Watch compound is a mile inland on the main road. Stone walls, wooden gates, watchtower. Not built for siege — built for garrison duty|
|**Patrol schedules**|Ground patrols sweep the harbor twice daily (dawn, dusk). Coastal road patrols twice weekly. The patrols are sloppy — soldiers rush through to get back inside the walls|
|**Captain Sworn**|"She's not evil. She's not even angry. She's _tired_. Three years of hearing joy in a place where joy is illegal. It's eating her alive. She drinks at night. She doesn't sleep. If you came to her with something real — something that explained what she's been hearing — I think she'd listen. Maybe"|
|**The Sunken Temple**|Location, condition of the wards (crumbling), the golden light on the altar, the warmth inside. "Whatever's in there, it's not a trap. It's not an attack. It's a _gift_. But the Accord will call it a weapon"|
|**The city at night**|"The buildings light up. Not all of them. But some nights, lanterns just... ignite. Festival colors. It lasts about an hour. Then they go out. Like the city is rehearsing for something"|
|**Approach route**|The best path from Greyshore avoids the main road entirely. Follow the coastal cliffs south to the Weeping Stones, then cut inland through the old pilgrim orchards. Comes out at the eastern edge of Joybright, away from the garrison. "The patrols don't go there. The orchards make them uncomfortable"|
|**The wards**|"Don't bother with the main temple doors. There's a collapsed wall on the south side. The wards don't cover it. Hollis and I went in that way the second time. I don't think the Captain knows about it"|

**After the conversation:**

> Aldren looks at the party with the first clear expression he's worn since they found him. "You're going in there, aren't you." Not a question. "Take me with you."

**If they agree:** He's a liability in combat (exhaustion, frightened, barely functional). But he knows the terrain, the patrol routes, and the back way into the temple. And something in him _needs_ to go back. The Echo touched him and he ran. He wants to finish what started.

**If they refuse:** He nods. He understands. "Then... tell her I'm sorry. I ran." He means the city. The presence. The warmth that still hums inside his head. He ran from joy because he was afraid of losing it, and he hasn't stopped running.

### Settling the Refugees

The 19 survivors need somewhere to be while the party goes south. Greyshore can absorb them — barely.

**Where they go:**

- **The old salt-house:** Dry, sturdy, empty since the catch dropped. Fits 10-12 adults on makeshift bedding. Eadra provides blankets
- **The bluff chapel:** Sister Corrine takes the 6 children without hesitation. Warm, safe, with an actual roof and real beds
- **The Saltstone Inn:** 2-3 rooms available for whoever can pay (or whoever Eadra decides needs it most)
- **Fishing families:** Some villagers, reluctantly or quietly, take in individual refugees. Greyshore may not be brave enough to fight the Accord, but it's decent enough to feed hungry people

**The old woman** (the praying elder from Session 1) stays with the children. She's the closest thing they have to family now.

**The tension:** Greyshore can hide 19 refugees for a few days. Maybe a week. After that, the waystation soldiers will notice. Questions will be asked. Reports will be filed. The party needs to either resolve the Joybright situation quickly or find a permanent solution.

**Water Walt stays with the refugees** (unless the players insist otherwise). He's a healer. They need a healer. And he's carrying too much guilt about the lightning shield to be useful in the field right now. This gives him a purpose — tending the people who survived — while the party goes south.

# ACT THREE: THE ROAD SOUTH

## "The Pilgrim's Path, Unwanted"

### Route Options

|Route|Distance|Time|Risk|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Coastal cliffs (Aldren's route)**|~8 miles|Half day|Low — avoids patrols, passes Weeping Stones|
|**Main road**|~6 miles|4 hours|High — passes directly through garrison territory|
|**Inland (pilgrim orchards)**|~10 miles|Full day|Very low — nobody patrols here, but longer|

**Aldren's recommended route:** Coastal cliffs south to the Weeping Stones, then cut inland through the old pilgrim orchards to approach Joybright from the east. Avoids the garrison entirely. Takes about 6-8 hours.

### Travel Events (Pick 2-3)

#### The Weeping Stones

About two hours south of Greyshore, the coastal cliffs are streaked with white salt deposits that look exactly like tears running down the rock face.

> The cliffs weep. Not metaphorically — the salt creates perfect tear-tracks down gray stone, converging in pools at the base that taste of the sea and something else. Something sweet. Pilgrims have been coming here for twenty-three years to mourn in the one place that mourns with them.

**What they find:**

- Fresh flowers at the base of the cliffs. Someone was here recently
- Names carved into the rock — hundreds of them. Loved ones lost to the Accord. A memorial that the authorities have never found because it's hidden in plain sight among natural salt formations
- A small carved alcove with a single scarlet ribbon, faded but intact. Someone tied it there years ago and it hasn't rotted
- If the Lastbloom is present: the salt tears _glow faintly_. For a moment, the cliff face looks like it's weeping light

#### The Pilgrim Orchards

The old approach to Joybright from the east was lined with orchards — fruit trees planted by generations of Lliiran faithful. Pilgrims would arrive with empty hands and leave with full ones.

> The orchards are still alive. Twenty-three years with no tending, and the trees still bear fruit. Apples, pears, something that looks like a plum but glows faintly scarlet in the right light. The ground between the trees is covered in wildflowers — scarlet, gold, white. The colors of Lliira's ribbons.

> A DC 13 Nature check reveals: these trees shouldn't be alive. No pruning, no pest control, no watering beyond rain. Something is sustaining them. The same something that preserves the city.

**If someone eats the fruit:**

> It tastes like the best thing you've ever eaten. Not because it's magic — because it's _real_. After days of hardtack and grief, fresh fruit from trees planted in love by people who believed in joy. It's nourishing in ways that go beyond calories.

> Any creature that eats the orchard fruit regains 1d4 hit points and feels a faint emotional warmth for the next hour. Mechanically insignificant. Narratively enormous.

**If Aldren is with them:**

> He stops walking. He's staring at the trees. "I used to come here on my night off. Just... sit. Eat an apple. Listen to the quiet." Tears again. But these are clean ones. "This is the only place I've been since the Watch where I felt like a person and not a uniform."

#### The Garrison in the Distance

From the elevated orchards, the party can see the Weeping Watch garrison compound — about a mile to the west, sitting on the main road.

> Stone walls, maybe twelve feet high. A wooden gate facing north (toward Greyshore). A watchtower — someone's in it, but they're looking north, away from you. A few buildings inside. Smoke from a cookfire. It looks less like a military installation and more like a village that accidentally got surrounded by walls.

**DC 14 Perception from this distance:**

- ~20 soldiers visible in or around the compound
- The gate is open — they're not expecting trouble
- A patrol of 4 is heading north on the main road (away from the party)
- Captain Sworn can be seen on the watchtower platform. She's standing very still, facing south. Toward Joybright. She does this every evening

#### The Edge of Joybright

The orchards thin. The wildflowers don't — they intensify, the scarlet deepening until the ground looks like it's blushing. And then the trees part, and they see it.

> Use the Joybright arrival description from the main Session 5 reference — the preserved buildings, the lanterns, the laughter, the silence that follows, the hymn. All of it. This is the set piece.

**The difference with the overland approach:** They come in from the east, through the residential quarter. Not the harbor — the _homes_. They walk through streets where people lived. Past doorways where families ate dinner. Past a playground where children played.

> A swing set. Wooden. Handmade. Two swings, side by side, parent and child. The ropes haven't rotted. They sway slightly in wind that isn't blowing. One swing is child-sized. A scarlet ribbon is tied to its chain.

**If Aldren is with them:**

> He stops at the edge of the city. He's shaking. But when the hymn begins — the one that's been living in his head for two weeks — he closes his eyes, and for the first time since the party found him, he stops trembling. "I'm back," he whispers. Not to the party. To the city. To whatever is humming inside the stones. "I'm sorry I ran. I'm back."

# OVERLAND COMBAT ENCOUNTERS

## Encounter: The Pilgrim's Shade

### "Not Everything That Walks This Path Is Alive"

**Trigger:** While passing through the orchards, as dusk approaches. Optional — use if you want a combat encounter before reaching Joybright.

**Setup:** The pilgrim orchards aren't empty. Centuries of faithful walking this path left impressions — emotional echoes of hope and devotion. Most of the time, these echoes are harmless. But the Accord's corruption has soured some of them. One pilgrim died on this path — murdered by Helmites during the early enforcement period. His echo didn't fade. It _curdled_.

**What happens:**

> The temperature drops. Among the trees, a figure appears — translucent, walking toward Joybright as if on a journey that ended twenty years ago. A middle-aged man in pilgrim's clothes, carrying a pack, humming. He looks peaceful.

> Then he turns and sees the party. His expression changes. He sees the Helmite equipment (if anyone is carrying it). His face twists. The humming becomes a _scream_.

### The Bitter Pilgrim

_Medium undead, chaotic neutral_

- **AC** 13 (natural armor)
- **HP** 67 (9d8 + 27)
- **Speed** 0 ft., fly 40 ft. (hover)
- **STR** 6 (-2) **DEX** 16 (+3) **CON** 16 (+3) **INT** 12 (+1) **WIS** 14 (+2) **CHA** 15 (+2)
- **Damage Resistances** Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Thunder; Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from nonmagical weapons
- **Damage Immunities** Necrotic, Poison
- **Damage Vulnerabilities** Radiant
- **Condition Immunities** Charmed, Exhaustion, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained
- **Senses** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 12

**Traits:**

- **Incorporeal Movement.** Can move through creatures and objects as difficult terrain. Takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside an object
- **Murdered Devotion.** The Pilgrim is not hostile to anyone who carries genuine faith or joy. If the Lastbloom is visible, or if a creature shows a holy symbol of Lliira, the Pilgrim hesitates (does not attack that creature on its first turn). If a creature is carrying or wearing Helmite insignia, the Pilgrim attacks that creature with advantage
- **Dying Prayer.** When the Pilgrim is reduced to 0 HP, it doesn't attack. It reaches toward Joybright. Its last words, audible to all: _"I was almost there."_ Anyone who hears this must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom save or gain the _frightened_ condition for 1 minute (frightened of the Accord, not the ghost)

**Actions:**

- **Bitter Touch.** Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (3d6+3) necrotic damage. Target must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution save or have its HP maximum reduced by the damage taken. This reduction lasts until the creature finishes a long rest. _The touch feels like grief — not the ghost's, but the target's own_
- **Wail of the Unfinished Journey (Recharge 5-6).** Each non-undead creature within 30 ft. must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom save or take 10 (3d6) psychic damage and be frightened until the end of their next turn. On a success, half damage and not frightened

**How to truly defeat it:**

Combat works — it's a ghost. But if someone _completes his journey_ — speaks his purpose aloud, carries his memory into Joybright, or uses the Lastbloom to show him that joy survived — he doesn't need to be destroyed. He _resolves_. His expression softens. He turns south. He walks into the city, fading with each step, and the last thing they see is a smile.

**If resolved peacefully:** Each party member gains inspiration. The orchards bloom slightly brighter.

**Loot (if destroyed):** A spectral pack becomes real on destruction. Contains a pilgrim's journal (written 18 years ago, describes the journey to Joybright, the author's hope for their daughter's healing), a handful of coins (30 gp in old mint), and a small carved dancing figure — Lliira's sacred pose. It's warm to the touch.

## Revised Cliffhanger Options

**Option A — The Edge of the City:** The orchards part. The wildflowers blaze scarlet. The buildings of Joybright rise before them, beautiful and impossible and silent. Then the laughter begins.

**Option B — Aldren's Return:** Aldren steps past the party, into the city. His exhaustion seems to lift. His eyes clear. "I'm back," he whispers. And from somewhere deep in the streets, a voice that isn't a voice answers: a single note of music, warm and welcoming, as if the city has been holding its breath.

**Option C — The Garrison Stirs:** From their vantage in the orchards, they watch a rider approach the Weeping Watch garrison at full gallop. A messenger. The sending stone wasn't enough — someone sent a rider. The gate opens, the message is delivered, and they can see — even from this distance — soldiers scrambling. The search has reached this coast.

**Option D — The Children Sing:** Back in Greyshore, while the party is already on the road south, Sister Corrine sends word (through Water Walt's connection): the children have started singing. The same hymn the city hums. They don't know the words — they're making them up. But the melody is _right_. And in the bluff chapel, for just a moment, the candles burn scarlet.
