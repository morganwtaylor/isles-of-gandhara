# PART 1: RESOLVE THE CONVERGENCE

## Path A — Lil Moon Places the Flower (Full Convergence)

**The instant the Lastbloom touches the altar:**

The sphere descends into the flower. Ember meets voice. The Book stops draining — the circuit completes. The Unwritten Chapter fills with golden ink. Paul reads Lliira's final words (use the full text from Session 6 ref).

**The Wave** erupts outward from the temple through the entire city:

- All Sorrowsworn in Joybright unmake — dissolved, not killed
- All creatures in the city healed 3d8+5 HP
- All fear/charm/despair effects end
- Garrison soldiers drop weapons, overwhelmed with involuntary joy
- The Book reaches Final State — cannot be closed
- Joybright becomes a **permanent sanctuary**

**After the Wave — what the party sees:**

- Scarlet flowers blooming from every surface — stone, rubble, cobblestones
- Festival lanterns relighting with phantom golden flames
- The fountain in Festival Square running warm and clear
- Pilgrims appearing at the city edges — drawn by what they felt
- Soldiers sitting on the ground, dazed, some crying, some laughing
- Across the Mirthwater, Accord's Rest can see Joybright glowing gold

**Skip to Part 2: The Aftermath.**

## Path B — Lil Moon Doesn't Place the Flower (Failed Convergence)

**The timer runs out. The Book empties.**

> The last words fade from the page. The golden ink sinks into the altar stone and vanishes. The Book goes still. Warm leather. Blank pages. The heartbeat stops.

> The altar absorbs everything — centuries of divine authorship, Lliira's voice, her prayers, her love letters to mortals. It all sinks into the foundations of Joybright. Not lost. _Dispersed_. Diluted into the bones of the city like medicine dissolved in an ocean.

**What happens to the city:**

- The Sorrowsworn weaken and scatter — not dramatically, just slowly, like fog burning off
- Joybright gets a permanent low hum of warmth. The flowers still grow. The laughter still echoes. But faintly
- No sanctuary. No anti-scrying. No consecrated ground. Just a wounded city that's slightly harder to kill
- The garrison can stay. The Accord can reinforce

**What happens to the Book:**

- Blank. Warm. The leather is still scarlet. But the pages are empty
- It's not destroyed — it's _spent_. A vessel that poured itself out
- Paul still has a connection to it. Over weeks/months, faint text may begin reappearing — Paul's words, not Lliira's. The Book is becoming his. Slowly. This is an Act 2 arc
- **Mechanically:** Paul loses all Book spellcasting immediately. Over Act 2, abilities return one at a time as the Book refills with his story. Dormant state by mid-Act 2, Awakened by Act 2 climax

**What happens to the Lastbloom:**

- Still in Lil Moon's hands. Still powerful. Still Awakened
- But it _aches_. Lil Moon can feel it grieving. It was so close to home. The ember is still burning but it missed its chance to merge with the voice
- The Lastbloom's next advancement trigger changes — it can no longer advance through the Joybright Convergence. It needs a NEW path (Wintervale? Death's Edge? Something Lil Moon earns personally?)

**What happens to Lucian's mark:**

- Still warm. Still reacts. The half-Convergence still cracked the planes open enough for the spark to take hold
- No change to his abilities

**Skip to Part 2: The Aftermath.** The emotional tone is different — bittersweet instead of triumphant. They saved the city a little. They could have saved it a lot. That tension lives in the party now.

## THE AMALGAMATION (Either Path — deploy if fight drags)

If the Sorrowsworn pressure peaks before the Convergence resolves (or if you want one final threat):

> The Sorrowsworn at the threshold stop clawing individually. They go still. Then they start _merging_. The Lonely's long arms wrap around the Lost. The Wretched crawl up and into the mass. The shadows blend. The shapes lose definition.

> Something fills the doorway. Not multiple creatures anymore. One thing. Massive. A body made of every grief this city has produced since the day it was murdered.

### The Amalgamation

**AC 15 | HP 200 | Speed 20 ft**

|STR|DEX|CON|INT|WIS|CHA|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|20 (+5)|8 (-1)|20 (+5)|4 (-3)|10 (+0)|3 (-4)|

**Resist:** bludgeoning, piercing, slashing **Immune:** psychic, frightened **Size:** Large (fills the doorway)

**Grief Given Form.** The Amalgamation can't fit through the temple doors. It reaches inside. Anything within 15 ft of the doorway is in reach.

**Absorb.** When a Sorrowsworn within 30 ft is destroyed, the Amalgamation regains 20 HP.

#### Actions

**Multiattack.** 2 Slam attacks.

**Slam.** +9, reach 15 ft. **3d8+5 bludgeoning + 2d6 psychic.**

**Wail of the City (Recharge 5-6).** 30 ft cone from the doorway into the temple. DC 16 WIS save or **stunned 1 round and take 4d6 psychic.** Half damage on success, no stun.

**Engulf (Recharge 6).** One creature within 15 ft. DC 17 STR save or pulled into the Amalgamation's mass. **Restrained, blinded, 3d8 psychic at start of each turn.** DC 17 STR to escape. The Amalgamation heals = psychic damage dealt.

#### Joy Weakness

**Genuine joy within 30 ft = disadvantage on everything AND loses resistance to physical damage.**

If the Convergence Wave hits while the Amalgamation is active, it **instantly unmakes**. No save. No HP. The wave of joy is absolute against concentrated grief. Describe it dramatically — the mass of shadow unraveling from the inside out, faces appearing briefly in the dissolving form, each one peaceful for just a moment before they're gone.

Give the table time. Don't rush into the next thing. Let them walk out of the temple and see what they did (or didn't do).

**Refugees/Pilgrims:**

- **Path A:** Trickling into the city. Singing. Crying. Settling in. Joybright is becoming a community.
- **Path B:** Some come. Fewer. The warmth draws them but there's no sanctuary protection. It's a squatter camp in a haunted ruin, not a beacon.

## Paul's Choice (Path A only)

If the Convergence succeeded, the city needs a keeper. The Book is open on the altar. Present the choice:

**Stay:** Paul becomes Joybright's guardian. NPC going forward. The party loses the Book's combat power but gains an unassailable home base with anti-scrying, consecrated ground, and a growing community of faithful.

**Go:** Paul takes the Open Book. Joybright keeps its sanctuary status but it's vulnerable without the Book's presence. The party keeps Paul and his spellcasting. The Accord can potentially reclaim the city.

**Someone else stays:** Karsa, a refugee, Delia Sworn, Sera. The city needs _someone who cares_, not specifically Paul. But the Book chose him.

**Don't force this tonight.** It can be the opening decision of Session 8 after the player has time to think.

# PART 3: THE PIVOT TO ACT 2

## Seeding Death's Edge

However the Convergence resolved, the campaign needs to move forward. The party needs a new objective. Plant these seeds during the aftermath:

### Seed 1 — Water Walt's Intel

Water Walt (in Greyshore or arrived with refugees) pulls the party aside:

> "My people in the highlands have been watching Death's Edge for months. The Banites sealed the deep tunnels six months ago. Miners go down, fewer come back. The cargo going in — alchemical sealing agents, divine warding components — that's not mining equipment. They found something down there. Something old."

> "Inquisitor Thorne has been going back and forth between Accord's Rest and Death's Edge. Whatever's down there, Bane wants it badly enough to send his best."

### Seed 2 — Lucian's Mark

Lucian's mark reacts. Not to Joybright — to something far away. Northeast. It pulses with a low, deep warmth. Different from the joyful warmth of the temple. Older. Darker. The mark is sensing something ancient and powerful at Death's Edge.

> "Whatever gave you that mark — it knows what's in those mines. And it's afraid of it."

### Seed 3 — The Sending Stone (Path A)

If the Convergence succeeded, a sending stone crackles. Not the garrison's. A new frequency. Cold. Precise:

> "Weeping Watch, this is Inquisitor Thorne. I understand something interesting has happened in your jurisdiction. Maintain containment. I will assess personally upon completion of current operations."

> "Current operations" = Death's Edge. Thorne isn't coming to Joybright _yet_ because the weapon is more important. But he will come. The clock is ticking.

### Seed 4 — Lil Moon Wants to Kill a God

Lil Moon's stated goal. Death's Edge has a weapon that can do exactly that. Whether the Convergence succeeded or failed, Lil Moon has a reason to go.

### Seed 5 — Murdock's Vendetta

Murdock wants revenge against the Helmites. Death's Edge is a Banite operation with Helmite support. The mine workers are being exploited. There are people to save and Accord officers to fight. This is Murdock's kind of mission.

## THE ACT 2 PITCH

When the party is ready to look forward, the situation is:

**The Goal:** Get to Death's Edge before Thorne extracts the god-killing weapon. If Bane gets it, no dissenter god is safe. Lliira was killed by coalition — with this weapon, Bane can do it alone.

**The Stakes:** If Bane gets the weapon, the campaign shifts from resistance to survival. Tymora, Mask, Selûne, Ilmater — all targets.

**The Route:** Death's Edge is in the far northeast of Ghandara. Briarholt Expanse, edge of the Northern Reaches. Options:

- Overland through the highlands (Water Walt's connections, clan allies)
- By sea around the northern coast (faster, more exposed)
- Through Briarholt and the Thornwood (Timber Guild territory, smuggler routes)

**The Party's Advantages:**

- Joybright as home base (Path A) or a fragile foothold (Path B)
- Highland clan connections through Water Walt
- Lucian's mark pointing the way
- Lil Moon's motivation
- Murdock's vendetta
- The Accord doesn't know they're coming — Thorne thinks Joybright is the party's endgame

**The Complications:**

- Thorne is already at Death's Edge. Head start
- The weapon is in a sealed pre-human chamber. Ancient, dangerous, trapped
- Banite guards, miners, and whatever Thorne brought with him
- The weapon itself is not a simple object — it's a _fragment_ of something that predates the current gods. It may have its own agenda
- If the party gets the weapon, they have to decide: use it, destroy it, or hide it?
