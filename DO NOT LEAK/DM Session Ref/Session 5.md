# ACT ONE: THE AFTERMATH

## "Blood and Silence"

### Opening the Session

The deck is a slaughterhouse. Twenty bodies. Thorns still growing from the planks where Lil Moon's magic tore through flesh. The fog has lifted. The sky is clear. The silence is the loudest thing any of them have ever heard.

**Immediate problems to address:**

1. **Wolfric** — Is he alive? Death saves or stabilized? Water Walt's healing?
2. **The bodies** — Twenty dead on a ship. What do they do with them?
3. **The children** — Six children just watched their families die. Someone needs to deal with this
4. **Karsa and Paul** — Tension. Karsa is a man without purpose who just had his last anchor cut
5. **Water Walt's guilt** — His lightning shield killed DaVonian and Wolfric. He was trying to protect Karsa

### Water Walt's Reckoning

Water Walt placed that shield on Karsa to protect him. It killed a fifteen-year-old boy and put down the paladin who had just performed a miracle to save that boy. Walt has to live with that.

**If approached:**

> Walt is sitting against the gunwale, staring at his hands. His voice is flat. "I put the shield on him because he was going to die. That was the right call. That was..." He stops. Starts again. "The boy hit him. I didn't think about the boy hitting him. I should have thought about the boy hitting him."

**If blamed:**

> He doesn't argue. Doesn't defend himself. Just nods. "Aye. I did that." Highland stoicism masking devastation. He won't apologize — not because he isn't sorry, but because he thinks apology is inadequate for what happened.

**If forgiven:**

> A long silence. Then: "I'll tend whoever needs tending. That's all I'm good for now."

### Karsa's Edge

Karsa is a hollow man. His contract with Tonyult is fulfilled (Marren is dead). The woman he helped empower is dead. The boy he killed is dead. Paul has angered him (how? — clarify with players).

**Karsa's state of mind:** He has nothing left to fight for, nothing to protect, nowhere to go. This makes him either incredibly dangerous or surprisingly open to something new. The party's treatment of him determines which.

**If confronted aggressively:**

> Karsa meets their eyes. No expression. "You want to kill me? Do it. I've been dead since Thornhaven. You'd just be making it official."

**If offered purpose:**

> A flicker. Barely visible. He's been a weapon his entire adult life. If someone gives him something to protect — genuinely protect, not a contract — it might be the first real thing he's felt in years.

**If left alone:**

> He sits. He watches. He cleans his weapon. At some point he quietly starts helping move bodies. Not because anyone asked. Because the bodies need moving and no one else is doing it.

### The Children

Six children survived. They range from roughly 5-12 years old. They have just watched adults they knew and trusted kill themselves. Some of them watched their parents die.

**This is the session's emotional anchor.** Whoever handles the children defines the moral tone of what comes next.

A girl, maybe eight, is holding a younger boy's hand. She hasn't cried yet. She's watching the adults to figure out if it's safe to cry.

> If a PC approaches: She looks up. "Is it over?" She doesn't mean the fight. She means all of it. Everything since they left Chult.

**The old woman** (if she survived — she's been praying since Session 1) is trying to gather the children. She's shaking but functional. She's the closest thing to a caretaker left.

### The Bodies

Twenty corpses on a small ship. Practically: they need to go overboard or they'll rot. Emotionally: these were people with names and histories who trusted the party to keep them safe.

**Options:**

|Action|Effect|
|---|---|
|**Sea burial with prayer**|Paul reads from the Book. The words glow faintly. The Lastbloom pulses. Even in death, Lliira's light reaches for her faithful. The refugees weep but it feels like closure, not despair. **Potential Lastbloom advancement trigger**|
|**Unceremonious disposal**|Quick, practical, devastating for morale. Refugees see their dead treated like cargo. The children will never forget this|
|**Preserve them for Joybright burial**|Noble but impractical. Twenty bodies on a ship for 1-2 days. Disease, smell, space. But the symbolism — bringing the dead home to Lliira's city — is powerful|
|**Let the refugees decide**|Best option emotionally. The 13 adults deserve agency over their dead. Whatever they choose, the party supports it|

**If Paul reads from the Book during burial:**

The Book responds. Not to the dead — to Paul. A new passage appears that Paul hasn't seen before, written in the divine hand:

> _"Count them. Remember them. Every name a prayer. Every prayer a seed. I am buried in the same water. We will be neighbors."_

This is Lliira's voice. The Book is choosing Paul.

# ACT TWO: THE APPROACH

## "Into the Forbidden Zone"

### Navigation

The captured Helmite charts show the Forbidden Zone around Joybright. Two patrol cutters run rotating schedules. There is a **4-hour gap every two days** during simultaneous resupply at the Weeping Watch garrison dock.

**Key decisions:**

- Do they use the gap? (Requires timing — Water Walt or someone with navigation skills can calculate it from the charts, DC 12 Intelligence or Water Vehicles)
- Do they fly the Helmite flag on the cutter as a disguise?
- Do they sail both ships or abandon the cutter?
- Do they approach the harbor directly or anchor offshore?

### The Sending Stone Crackles

During the approach, the captured sending stone activates with an UPDATED alert:

> "Priority update. Cutter Seven confirmed not storm damage. Evidence of hostile engagement recovered at last known position. All vessels assume hostile actors operating in Mirthwater South corridor. Descriptions being circulated: civilian vessel, thirty-plus passengers, possible Lliiran dissidents. Armed and dangerous. Do NOT engage alone. Report and shadow. Reinforcements en route from Helmgate. Command out."

**The net has tightened.** They're not just missing — they're _hunted_. The description matches their ship. The cutter is evidence.

### The Lastbloom Reacts

As they approach Joybright, the Lastbloom escalates:

**Half a day out:**

> The flower is glowing constantly now. Not bright — a steady candlelight warmth. Lil Moon can feel something ahead. Not a voice. A _pull_. Like being homesick for a place you've never been.

**Approaching the coast:**

> The scarlet deepens to the color of arterial blood. The Lastbloom is wider than it's ever been — petals unfurling like a hand reaching. It's pulling northeast. Toward the coast. Toward something that's been waiting.

> Anyone with divine sensitivity (Wolfric if conscious, Paul with the Book, Lil Moon) hears it: laughter. Not from the ship. Not from the sea. From _ahead_. Distant, layered, hundreds of voices celebrating something none of them can see.

**Within sight of Joybright:**

> The Lastbloom opens fully. Every petal. Scarlet like festival ribbons, glowing like a small sun in Lil Moon's hands. The refugees who survived — the thirteen adults and six children who have lost everything — crowd the rail and stare at the coast ahead.

> Some are crying. Some are smiling. Most are doing both.

> The old woman falls to her knees. "Home. She brought us _home_."

### What They See

> The fog — silver-gold, warm, smelling of flowers and old wine and candle smoke — parts like a curtain.

> Joybright.

> It shouldn't be beautiful. It's been dead for twenty-three years. But the buildings stand as if time forgot them. Stone walls unmarked by weather. Rooftops intact. Festival lanterns hanging from hooks above streets where no one walks, their colors unfaded, their glass unbroken.

> The harbor is a crescent of stone piers built for pilgrim ships. Deep water berths, iron mooring rings, broad stone quays designed for hundreds of arrivals. It is the most perfectly preserved harbor any of you have ever seen.

> It is completely empty.

> And then the laughter stops.

> All at once. Like a held breath. The city goes silent. The fog stills. The Lastbloom dims — not dark, but quieter. _Listening._

> Then, softly — a single voice. Not words. A melody. A hymn. One of the old ones, banned three years ago, the kind the refugees have been singing in secret below deck.

> It's coming from inside the city. From everywhere and nowhere. And it sounds like it's been singing for twenty-three years, waiting for someone to hear it.

# ACT THREE: THE HARBOR

## "The Welcome No One Expected"

### Docking

The harbor is **unguarded**. The Weeping Watch garrison pulled back from it months ago — soldiers stationed there reported sleepwalking, hearing their names called, waking up on streets they didn't walk to. The garrison is now positioned roughly a mile inland on the main road.

**The party can dock freely.** No immediate military contact. But the garrison exists and will eventually notice a ship in the harbor.

**Timeline:** If they dock by night, 6-8 hours before a routine coastal check spots the mast. If by day, maybe 2-3 hours. Either way, they have a window.

### When the First Person Steps Ashore

> The stone pier is warm underfoot. Not sun-warmed — warm the way a living thing is warm. The harbor stones are clean. No barnacles. No seaweed. No algae. As if the sea itself has been gently cleaning them.

> The hymn continues — quiet, patient, inviting. It's coming from deeper in the city.

> The old woman is already walking. She doesn't wait for permission. The children follow her.

**If a PC tries to stop them:**

> The old woman turns. Her eyes are bright and clear for the first time since Session 1. "I've been praying for twenty-three years. Do you hear that? _She's answering._ You can come or you can stay. But I'm going home."

### The City Streets

Joybright is _wrong_ in the most beautiful way possible.

**What they see:**

- Streets swept clean by wind that isn't blowing
- Homes with doors ajar, furniture inside untouched by time or dust
- Market stalls still set up, goods long gone but structures intact
- A fountain in a square that's still running — clear water, no source anyone can find
- Festival decorations everywhere: scarlet ribbons, paper flowers, painted masks on hooks beside doors
- Lanterns that aren't lit but seem like they _could be_ — there's oil in them, wicks intact, as if someone serviced them yesterday

**What they feel:**

- Warmth. Not temperature — emotional warmth. A sense of being _welcome_
- The grief they've been carrying feels lighter here. Not gone — lighter. Like someone is helping them hold it
- Laughter at the edge of hearing. Not mocking. Not haunting. _Inviting_

**What they hear (DC 12 Perception or divine sensitivity — automatic for Lastbloom/Book bearers):**

- Snatches of conversation in a language they understand but can't quite catch
- Music — drums, flutes, strings — drifting from different directions, never the same song twice
- A child laughing, running, feet on cobblestones — but no child is there

**What's actually happening:**

Joybright is saturated with residual divine energy. Lliira's death released a final gift — the joy of ten thousand festivals, preserved in stone and air, echoing through time. The city is a vessel for emotion. It isn't sentient. It isn't Lliira. But it _remembers_, the way a house remembers the family that lived in it.

The Lastbloom's arrival has woken it further. The artifacts are converging. The song is getting louder.

# ACT FOUR: EXPLORATION ENCOUNTERS

## Key Locations in Joybright

### 1. The Great Festival Hall

The largest building in the city center. A vast open hall with a domed ceiling painted with dancers and musicians and revelers from every race and nation. The paint hasn't faded. The gold leaf still gleams.

**What they find:**

- A stage at the far end, set for a performance that never happened
- Festival masks on every seat — hundreds of them, each unique, each smiling
- The air here is thick with residual energy. The Lastbloom blazes. The Book's pages flutter
- **On the stage:** A single pair of dancing shoes, scarlet leather, child-sized. They're warm. They're _waiting for someone_

**If anyone puts on a mask:**

> DC 13 Wisdom save. On a failure — not harm. _Joy._ A vivid, overwhelming memory of a festival that happened decades ago. Dancing with strangers. Laughing until your sides hurt. The pure animal happiness of being alive and surrounded by people who are also alive and celebrating it. The player experiences five minutes of this in about six seconds. When they come back, they're weeping and they don't know why.

> On a success: Same vision, but gentler. A whisper rather than a shout. They can watch without being submerged.

**DM Note:** This is how the Lastbloom advances to Awakened. If someone puts on a mask and _chooses to feel the joy_ — not resisting it, not afraid of it, but accepting it — and the Lastbloom is present, **the flower advances.** This is joyful defiance: embracing happiness in a dead city where happiness was murdered. The Lastbloom recognizes it.

### 2. The Sunken Temple

Partially collapsed — the only structure in Joybright showing damage. Lliira's holy temple, the heart of her worship on the Isles. One wall has buckled inward, the roof sags, and the entrance is barred with heavy iron doors covered in Helmite wards.

**The wards have been replaced four times.** Something keeps breaking them from inside.

**Current state of the wards:** Cracked. Leaking golden light through the fractures. The humming from inside is audible from twenty feet away.

**If the party approaches:**

> The Lastbloom goes _wild_. It's pulling toward the temple with physical force — Lil Moon has to actively resist being dragged forward. The Book in Paul's hands opens on its own to a page that reads: _"Open the door. I've been knocking for twenty-three years."_

**If they break the wards (DC 16 Strength to force, or the Lastbloom can dissolve them on contact):**

The doors swing inward. Golden-scarlet light floods out. The air smells of summer and celebration. Inside:

> The temple interior is untouched. Pews draped in scarlet silk. An altar covered in fresh flowers — scarlet, gold, white — that should have died decades ago but haven't. Candles that burn without consuming their wax. And on the altar, hovering inches above the surface, a sphere of light the size of a fist.

> It's warm. It's pulsing. It's _laughing_.

**This is the Echo.** Not Lliira — she's truly dead. But a fragment of her divinity, preserved in the place where she was most loved, fed by twenty-three years of residual worship from secret pilgrims and the building's own memory. An ember so hot it's almost a flame.

**The Echo is not sentient.** It can't speak, think, or plan. But it can _feel_, and what it feels is this: the Lastbloom is here. The Book is here. People who need joy are here. And for the first time in twenty-three years, the ember has fuel.

**What happens when the Lastbloom and Book are brought into the temple:**

This is a major campaign moment. All three fragments converge — Lliira's voice (Book), Lliira's ember (Lastbloom), and Lliira's grave (Joybright). See **The Convergence** below.

### 3. The Last Festival Ground

An open field south of the city where the final Festival of Endless Joy was being held when Lliira was executed. Only uniform grass grows here now — no wildflowers, no weeds, nothing but a perfect green carpet.

**What they feel:**

> Walking onto the field is like walking into a memory. Anyone with divine sensitivity is staggered. The air is thick with the ghosts of ten thousand celebrations — not literal ghosts, but emotional impressions so powerful they're almost visible. You can smell the food. Hear the drums. Feel the press of bodies dancing.

**If the Lastbloom is brought here:**

> Small scarlet flowers begin to bloom in the grass. One. Then ten. Then a hundred. The field that has grown nothing but uniform grass for twenty-three years suddenly explodes with color. The refugees fall to their knees. The children start laughing — not because anything is funny. Because joy is infectious and this place _radiates_ it.

### 4. The Abandoned Homes

Throughout the city, homes stand open and preserved. These aren't set pieces — they're evidence that real people lived here, loved here, celebrated here.

**Exploration Table (d6 — roll per home explored):**

|d6|Discovery|
|---|---|
|1|A child's bedroom. Toys still on the floor. A painted mural of Lliira dancing across the wall. A small scarlet dress hanging in the wardrobe. The Lastbloom glows when the dress is touched|
|2|A kitchen with a table set for six. A meal was being prepared when they fled. Twenty-three years later, there's no rot, no mold — just a frozen moment. A recipe book lies open to "Festival Honey Cakes"|
|3|A workshop. A craftsman was making festival masks. Dozens line the walls in various states of completion. One, unfinished, seems to be looking at whoever enters. It has no face — just the suggestion of a smile|
|4|A temple annex. Personal prayer scrolls in a box. Reading them reveals ordinary people's prayers to Lliira: "Let my daughter laugh today." "Help me forgive my brother." "I'm afraid of dying. Help me not be." Devastating in their simplicity|
|5|A music room. Instruments hang on the walls — drums, flutes, a harp with strings still taut. If played, the sound carries further than it should. Other instruments in other houses _answer_, as if an invisible orchestra is waiting for the first note|
|6|A hidden cellar. Someone was stockpiling scarlet cloth, holy symbols, and copies of banned hymns. This was a safehouse — someone stayed behind after the evacuation, secretly maintaining Lliiran worship. A journal, half-filled, ends mid-sentence: _"They're coming. I can hear the boots. I'm going to keep singing until—"_|

# COMBAT ENCOUNTERS

## Encounter 1: The Weeping Watch Patrol

### "Soldiers Who Don't Want to Be Here"

**Trigger:** 2-3 hours after docking (daytime) or 6-8 hours (nighttime). Routine coastal sweep spots the ship.

**Setup:** A squad of 8 Weeping Watch soldiers approaches down the main road from the garrison. They're nervous. They hate being here. They hear things. The city _does things_ to people posted here.

### Patrol Composition

**Vigilant Sera Coldwell** — patrol leader

_Medium humanoid (human), lawful neutral_

- **AC** 17 (chain mail + shield)
- **HP** 58 (9d8 + 18)
- **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 16 (+3) **DEX** 13 (+1) **CON** 14 (+2) **INT** 11 (+0) **WIS** 13 (+1) **CHA** 10 (+0)
- **Skills** Athletics +5, Perception +3
- **Actions:**
    - **Multiattack.** Two longsword attacks
    - **Longsword.** +5 to hit, 1d8+3 slashing
    - **Heavy Crossbow.** +3 to hit, range 100/400, 1d10+1 piercing
- **Reaction: Parry.** +2 AC against one melee attack she can see

**Sera's deal:** She's 22. She was posted here four months ago as punishment for questioning orders. She's terrified of the city but too proud to request transfer. She hasn't smiled since she arrived — not because she's cruel, but because this place has drained every drop of happiness from her.

_She does NOT want to fight._ She wants to confirm they're not pilgrims, file a report, and go back to the garrison where at least the walls keep the laughter out.

- **HP** 22 (3d8 + 6) each
- **Speed** 30 ft.

- **Longsword.** +4 to hit, 1d8+2 slashing
- **Heavy Crossbow.** +2 to hit, range 100/400, 1d10 piercing

**Morale:** These soldiers break at 50% casualties or if something _weird_ happens (and in Joybright, it always does). DC 12 Intimidation causes two to flee. DC 15 and the whole squad routs except Sera.

**1 Helmite Watcher-Acolyte** — field medic / communications

- **AC** 14 (chain shirt)
- **HP** 27 (5d8 + 5)

- **Spellcasting (WIS, DC 12, +4):** Cantrips: _sacred flame, spare the dying_. 1st (3 slots): _healing word, shield of faith, command_

### Social Resolution Options

|Approach|DC|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Bluff — "We're shipwrecked"**|DC 14 Deception|Easier if they've hidden the cutter/Helmite gear. Harder with 19 refugees including children|
|**Bluff — Helmite disguise**|DC 12 Deception|If wearing Helmite uniforms. "Relief patrol from Helmgate." Sera will be suspicious but wants to believe|
|**Intimidate**|DC 14 Intimidation|These soldiers are already scared. Pushing them could cause a rout. Or panic. Panicked soldiers send distress signals|

|**The Lastbloom**|DC 10 (special)|If the Lastbloom is visible and active, Sera and the soldiers _feel_ it. Warmth. Hope. The first positive emotion they've had in months. This doesn't override their training, but it makes them hesitate. Advantage on all social checks|
|**Let the city handle it**|No check|If combat seems imminent, the city reacts. See **Joybright Intervenes** below|

### If Combat Occurs

**Round 1-2:** Soldiers fight nervously. The Acolyte immediately tries to use the sending stone. Stopping the message (grapple, silence spell, counterspell, or just killing the Acolyte) prevents reinforcements.

**Round 3+:** Something happens. The city doesn't want violence here.

### Joybright Intervenes (Lair Effect)

If blood is spilled on Joybright's streets, the city reacts:

> The ground trembles. Not an earthquake — a _vibration_, like a massive drum struck once. The festival lanterns overhead ignite simultaneously — scarlet flame, warm, not threatening. And the laughter returns. Not distant this time. _Close_. All around them. Everywhere.

> Every combatant — party AND soldiers — must make a **DC 14 Wisdom save** or be overwhelmed by a wave of pure, unfiltered joy. Not mind control. Not compulsion. Just... the memory of happiness so powerful it's hard to hold a weapon.

**On a failed save:** The creature drops what it's holding, stands stunned until the end of its next turn, and weeps. Not from pain. From a happiness so sudden and so profound that the body doesn't know what to do with it.

**On a success:** The creature resists but feels the pull. Disadvantage on attack rolls for 1 round. Hard to swing a sword when part of you would rather dance.

**The soldiers' reaction:** Most of them fail. Sera fails. The Acolyte fails. Hardened Helmites — people trained to suppress emotion, to serve duty above feeling — are _broken open_ by a wave of joy they haven't felt since before they were posted to this cursed city.

**Sera, on her knees, weapon dropped, weeping:**

> "What... what IS this place?"

This is the moment the encounter transforms. If the party is wise, they don't press the attack. They let the city speak for itself.

**If the party extends compassion to Sera after this:**

She's done. Not as a Helmite — as a person. She's been miserable for four months and just felt what this city used to be. She won't fight for the Accord here. She might not fight for the Accord ever again.

> "I won't report you. I can't — I don't even know what I'd say. That the city made me cry? They'll pull me off rotation and send me to Piety's Rest for re-education." A pause. "Don't hurt my soldiers. They're kids. Most of them are younger than me."

## Encounter 2: Sorrowsworn — The Lonely

### "Something Fed on Twenty Deaths"

**Trigger:** The concentrated grief from the ship has attracted something. Not in Joybright — the city's residual joy repels Sorrowsworn. This creature manifests on the _ship_ while the party is exploring the city. It comes for the refugees left behind.

**Setup:** If any refugees (especially the children) are left on the ship while the party explores, the ship becomes vulnerable. The deaths of twenty people created a wound in the emotional fabric. Something crawled through.

**How the party learns:** A child's scream from the harbor. Or the Lastbloom suddenly dims and pulses cold. Or a refugee comes running, wild-eyed: "Something's on the ship. Something _cold_."

### The Lonely — Modified

_Medium monstrosity, neutral evil_

- **AC** 12
- **HP** 90 (reduced from 112 — weakened by proximity to Joybright)
- **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 16 (+3) **DEX** 12 (+1) **CON** 17 (+3) **INT** 6 (-2) **WIS** 11 (+0) **CHA** 6 (-2)
- **Damage Resistances** Cold, Necrotic
- **Damage Vulnerabilities** Radiant
- **Condition Immunities** Frightened
- **Senses** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10

**Traits:**

- **Psychic Leech.** While grappling a creature, The Lonely regains hit points equal to the psychic damage dealt to that creature
- **Feeding Frenzy.** The Lonely targets the most grief-stricken creature it can reach. It ignores armed combatants to reach refugees unless blocked
- **Joy Weakness.** While any creature within 30 feet is experiencing _genuine joy_ (not forced, not magical — real happiness), The Lonely has disadvantage on all attack rolls and saving throws. The Lastbloom's touch, reading from the Book, a child's spontaneous laughter — these all qualify

**Actions:**

- **Harpoon Arm.** Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d10+3) piercing. If Medium or smaller, target is pulled up to 30 ft. toward The Lonely
- **Sorrowful Embrace.** Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (4d6+3) psychic. Target is grappled (escape DC 14). While grappled, the target takes 14 (4d6) psychic damage at the start of each of its turns. The Lonely regains HP equal to psychic damage dealt

**Behavior:**

Round 1: It grabs the most grief-stricken refugee — probably one of the adults who watched their spouse die. Harpoon Arm to pull them in, then Sorrowful Embrace.

Round 2+: It feeds. While feeding, it ignores others unless attacked. When damaged, it screeches — a sound like every mourner at every funeral, layered and horrible.

**How to really beat it:** Combat works but is slow — it heals by feeding. The real weapon is joy. If someone uses the Lastbloom's Memory of Joy on a refugee nearby, or Paul reads an uplifting passage from the Book, or someone makes a child laugh — genuinely, spontaneously — the creature _shrivels_. Disadvantage on everything. It can't feed when joy is present. Drive it out with happiness.

**Narrative purpose:** The Accord's oppression births monsters. Twenty deaths on a ship created this. Bringing joy to Joybright isn't symbolic — it's the only weapon that works against the darkness the Accord creates.

## Encounter 3: Captain Delia Sworn

### "The Woman Who Stopped Smiling"

**Trigger:** Only if the party is still in Joybright after the 2-hour reinforcement window (from Encounter 1) OR if they remain long enough for the garrison to organize a response (12+ hours).

**Setup:** Delia Sworn doesn't come with overwhelming force. She comes with twenty soldiers, yes, but she comes personally because she _needs to understand_. Three years running this garrison. Three years of laughter in empty buildings. Three years of soldiers losing their minds. And now a ship full of people docked in her harbor and something _changed_ — the city got louder, brighter, more alive, and she needs to know why before she files a report that brings an Inquisitor to her doorstep.

### Captain Delia Sworn

_Medium humanoid (human), lawful neutral — barely_

- **AC** 18 (plate armor)
- **HP** 90 (12d8 + 36)
- **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 18 (+4) **DEX** 11 (+0) **CON** 16 (+3) **INT** 14 (+2) **WIS** 16 (+3) **CHA** 12 (+1)
- **Saves** STR +7, CON +6, WIS +6
- **Skills** Athletics +7, Insight +6, Perception +6, Intimidation +4
- **Actions:**
    - **Multiattack.** Three attacks with longsword or two with longsword + Shield Bash
    - **Longsword.** +7 to hit, 1d8+4 slashing (1d10+4 two-handed)
    - **Shield Bash.** +7 to hit, 1d4+4 bludgeoning + DC 14 STR save or prone
    - **Commander's Strike (3/day).** As a bonus action, one allied creature within 30 ft. can use its reaction to make one weapon attack with advantage
- **Reactions:**
    - **Sentinel.** When a creature within 5 ft. attacks an ally, Delia can make a melee attack against that creature
    - **Shield Block.** +3 AC against one attack she can see

**Delia's Deal:**

She's 38. She's been a Helmite her entire adult life. She believes in order, duty, and the rule of law. She has watched the Accord's machinery grind good people into dust and told herself it was necessary.

Three years in Joybright has _ruined_ her. Not because the city hurt her — because it's _beautiful_. Because every night she hears music from a city that's supposed to be dead and it's the most wonderful thing she's ever heard. Because she can't reconcile "this place should be forbidden" with "this place makes me want to weep with happiness."

She hasn't smiled in three years. Not because she's cruel. Because if she smiles, she's admitting that joy lives here and everything the Accord told her about this city is a lie.

**Delia's approach:**

> She arrives in full plate, shield on her arm, twenty soldiers behind her. She's not charging. She's walking. Measured. Controlled. She stops fifty feet from the party.

> "I am Captain Delia Sworn of the Weeping Watch. You are trespassing in a restricted zone. I should arrest every one of you, put you in chains, and send for an Inquisitor."

> A pause. She looks past the party at the city. At the lanterns that are glowing now when they weren't this morning. At the scarlet flowers blooming in cracks in the cobblestones.

> "But first I need someone to tell me what you've done to my city."

**Social encounter — this is NOT primarily a combat.** Delia wants answers more than she wants arrests.

|Topic|Delia's Reaction|
|---|---|
|**"We're Lliiran refugees"**|Tenses. "Then you know this zone is forbidden. You know what happens to pilgrims." But she doesn't draw her weapon|
|**Show the Lastbloom**|She _stares_. Long silence. "Where did you get that?" She's felt it. Every soldier in this garrison has felt _something_ from this city. She never knew what it was. Now she's looking at it|
|**Show the Book**|Her hand goes to her sword hilt instinctively. "That's a Class One artifact. I should confiscate that and have you executed." She doesn't move. "I should." The word 'should' doing a lot of work|
|**"The city is waking up"**|"I KNOW." Almost a shout. Then quieter: "I know. It's been getting louder for months. My soldiers can't sleep. Three deserted this quarter. One of them is hiding in Greyshore — yes, I know where he is. I let him go because what was I going to tell him? 'Stop hearing things'? I hear them too"|
|**"What if everything the Accord told you about this place is wrong?"**|This is the knife. She doesn't answer for a long time. "If it's wrong... then I've been guarding a miracle for three years and calling it a threat." Her hand drops from her sword. "What do you want from me?"|
|**"Let us stay. Help us. Or just look the other way"**|"If I let you stay and the Accord finds out, I'm dead. My soldiers are dead. Our families are—" She stops. She's thinking. "How long do you need?"|

|**Combat**|She fights like a veteran commander. Her soldiers follow orders. This is a Hard encounter — Delia alone is dangerous, and her Commander's Strike turns ordinary guards into threats. ## Encounter 4 (Optional): The Echo's Test

### "Are You Worthy of What Grows Here?"

**Trigger:** The party enters the Sunken Temple and approaches the Echo.

**This is not a combat encounter.** It's a trial. The Echo — the fragment of Lliira's divinity — cannot think, but it can _test_. It tests the way fire tests: by touching and seeing what burns and what endures.

**The Test:** When the party approaches the altar, the golden light pulses and every PC must make a **DC 15 Wisdom save.**

**On a failure:** The PC is overwhelmed by a vision. Not pleasant — _real_. They experience the execution of Lliira from her perspective. The sky, the chains, the pain, the faces of her faithful watching from below. The knowledge that this is the end of joy, that no one will dance with abandon again, that laughter will become furtive and small. The vision ends with Lliira's final act: looking down at her worshippers and choosing, in her last moment, not to scream. To _laugh_. One last defiant, loving, impossible laugh.

The PC takes **11 (2d10) psychic damage** from the intensity but gains **inspiration** and, if attuned to the Lastbloom or Book, a permanent +1 to Wisdom saves.

**On a success:** The same vision, but experienced as a _choice_. The PC feels Lliira's last laugh not as something that happened _to_ them, but as something offered — a question: _"Can you laugh when everything is lost? Can you find joy in the darkest place? Will you carry this forward?"_

If they accept (player choice, no check): same rewards as failure, but no psychic damage. The Echo recognizes them.

**After the test:**

The Echo dims slightly. It's given what it can. It can't speak, can't guide, can't plan. But it's reached out and touched every person in the temple. The golden light settles into the Lastbloom and the Book — they absorb it, growing warmer, stronger.

**Mechanical result:**

- The Lastbloom advances to **Awakened** if not already (advantage on frightened saves, end frightened/charmed, Calm Emotions 1/day, Hymn of the Lastlight)
- The Book advances one tier
- The scarlet flowers that bloomed in the Festival Ground don't fade when the Lastbloom leaves. They're permanent. Joy has been planted in Joybright again
- The city's laughter is louder now. Not background noise — a _presence_. The Accord will notice eventually

# THE CONVERGENCE

## "Three Fragments, One Question"

If all three fragments — Lastbloom, Book, and Joybright — are united (specifically, if both artifacts are brought into the Sunken Temple), this is what happens:

> The golden light on the altar blazes. The Lastbloom opens wider than it ever has — not just a flower now, but something _more_, a living vessel of an emotion so powerful it borders on divinity. The Book's pages turn themselves, faster and faster, and words appear on the blank pages at the back — the Unwritten Chapter — in a hand that is neither Marren's nor Tessa's nor any human hand:

> _"You came."_

> _"I wasn't sure anyone would."_

> _"The dancer's feet remember even when the music stops. The music stopped. But I saved one note. You're holding it."_

> _"I can't come back. What dies divinely stays dead. But you can carry what I was. Not my power. Not my name. My question:"_

> _"Can joy survive?"_

> The light fades. The Echo is spent — not destroyed, but _distributed_. It's in the Lastbloom now, and in the Book, and in the stones of Joybright, and in every person who was in this room when it spoke. A seed planted in a dozen hearts.

> The city is quiet for the first time since they arrived. Then, from the Festival Ground, a sound: a child laughing. One of the refugee children, running through a field of scarlet flowers that weren't there yesterday, laughing because the flowers are warm and the air smells like celebration and for one moment — just one — everything is going to be all right.

**What this means mechanically:**

- Both artifacts advance one tier
- Joybright becomes a sanctuary: the Bloom effect (anti-divination, advantage vs fear/charm/despair) is permanent within the city limits
- The Accord's scrying cannot find the party while they're in Joybright
- But: the city is _awake_ now. The laughter is constant. The flowers bloom. The lanterns light themselves. The garrison will notice. The Accord will notice. The clock is running

# DM NOTES

## Tone

This session should feel like _hope_. After four sessions of escalating tragedy — execution, persecution, murder, betrayal, mass death — Joybright is where the campaign turns. Not easy. Not safe. But _hopeful_. The city wants them here. Lliira's echo wants them here. Joy _wants to exist_, and it's been waiting twenty-three years for someone to carry it back.

Let the players breathe. Let them explore. Let the children laugh. Let someone dance. Then remind them that the Accord is coming.

## Pacing

- **Act 1 (Aftermath):** 30-45 minutes. Emotional decompression. Don't rush this
- **Act 2 (Approach):** 15-30 minutes. Navigation, sending stone, Lastbloom reactions. Build anticipation
- **Act 3 (Arrival):** 15-20 minutes. The big reveal. Let the description land. This is a set piece
- **Act 4 (Exploration):** 60-90 minutes. This is the meat. Encounters, locations, NPC interactions
- **Cliffhanger:** 5-10 minutes. Plant the hook for Session 6

## Key Theme

**Joy as resistance.** Not naivety. Not denial. The deliberate, defiant choice to find happiness in a world that has outlawed it. The children laughing. The refugees dancing. The soldiers weeping because they felt something real for the first time in years. Every moment of genuine joy in Joybright is a political act, a spiritual act, and a weapon against the Accord.
