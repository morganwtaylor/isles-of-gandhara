# ACT ONE: THE ESCAPE
## "Run"

### Opening Scene: Scattered in the Streets

The party fled the Golden Chain in different directions. It's night. Thornhaven's streets are dim — oil lamps on major roads, darkness in alleys. The town isn't large but it's unfamiliar.

**Setting the Mood:** Thornhaven at night is a working town gone quiet. Shipwrights' hammers are silent. The smell of sawdust and brine. Distant sound of waves. The occasional Helmite patrol lantern bobbing down a main road.

**Getting Back to the Ship:**
- **Easy Route:** Main roads — well-lit, faster, but risk running into Helmite night patrol (2 Watchers walking a beat). They're not looking for the party specifically *yet*, but four people running through town at night draws attention
- **Hard Route:** Back alleys and the waterfront — darker, slower, requires Navigation (DC 12 Survival or DC 10 if anyone explored the town earlier). Safer from patrols but might stumble into dockside trouble (drunk sailors, stray dogs, a locked gate)
- **If they split up:** Run each group separately. Build tension. One group makes it easy; the other hits a complication

**Possible Complications:**
| Roll (d6) | Event |
|-----------|-------|
| 1 | Helmite patrol on main road — DC 13 Stealth to avoid, or bluff (DC 14 Deception) |
| 2 | Locked harbor gate — DC 12 Athletics to climb, DC 14 Thieves' Tools to pick |
| 3 | Drunk dockworkers blocking an alley — can be talked past (DC 10 Persuasion) or intimidated (DC 12) |
| 4 | A stray dog starts barking, drawing attention — DC 11 Animal Handling or just run |
| 5 | They hear screaming from the direction of the Golden Chain — Karsa? Nessandrle? Do they go back? |
| 6 | Clean run — they make it without incident |

**If they go back to the Golden Chain:** See "The Back Room" below.

Witnessed several merchants being caught stealing in Thornhaven — merchants who were heading toward Death's Edge with unusual cargo (specialized equipment, alchemical supplies, things a normal mine doesn't need).

**WHERE:** The healer encounters the party during the escape. Options:

### The Back Room — What Happened to Karsa and Nessandrle

After the party fled, Tonyult assessed the situation:
- Two bodies (one massive warrior, one Compliance Ministry agent) bleeding out in his back office

- The party — whoever they are — fled toward the harbor

Tonyult made a business decision. He had the new healer character (or one of his own people, if the healer hasn't been introduced yet) stabilize both Karsa and Nessandrle. Not out of kindness — out of investment.

**KARSA:** Stabilized, badly wounded (1 HP, multiple injuries). Tonyult's price for saving his life: a debt. Unspecified. To be called in later. Karsa, being a warrior of honor (whatever that means to his culture), accepted. He had no choice.

- Karsa is now conscious but in no condition to fight (wounded condition: half speed, disadvantage on attack rolls until he gets proper rest and healing — at least 2 long rests or magical healing equivalent to 30+ HP)
- He wants to get back to Marren. He's loyal to Marren above all else
- He can share information about Marren's escape (see "Marren's Story" below)
- He's angry, humiliated, and dangerous even when wounded

**NESSANDRLE:** Also stabilized. Tonyult has them in a room at the Golden Chain — not a prisoner exactly, but not free to leave either. Tonyult wants to know: Who sent you? Why did you fail? What's the Compliance Ministry's interest in these refugees?

**Options for Nessandrle's fate (Morgan's call):**

**Option A — Forced to Travel with Party:**
Tonyult delivers Nessandrle to the party as part of the "package" with Karsa. His logic: "The bard is a Ministry agent. They know things you need to know. And if they're with you, they're not reporting back to their handlers. Consider it a gift." If Nessandrle travels with the party:

- Their sending stone is either with Tonyult (he kept it) or on the body (lootable). Either way, the handler on the other end has been trying to make contact

**Option B — Killed:**

### The Marren-Tessa Scene
## THE BIG DRAMA — HANDLE WITH CARE

**Context:**
- Marren Brightwell is the real Lliiran priest. Genuine calling, genuine faith
- He possessed the original Book of Exultation — written (or at least transcribed and annotated extensively) by/for Lliira's faithful, with Marren's personal prayers and notes throughout
- Helmites captured him 10 years ago for possessing it
- Tessa found the hidden Book after his arrest
- Over 10 years, she gradually became his replacement — reading his prayers, repeating his words, building a following using his work
- The 30 refugees know only Tessa. They don't know the real Marren
- Tessa is terrified of exposure. Marren is exhausted, emotional, confused

**HOW TO PLAY THIS:**

**Phase 1 — The Reunion (Private, Below Deck)**
Marren and Tessa are alone (or think they are — a PC might overhear).

Marren is overwhelmed. He's been imprisoned/hiding for 10 years. He thought he'd never see her again. The first moments are genuine emotion — tears, holding each other.

Then he asks about the Book.

> **Marren:** "The Book — do you still have it? I hid it in the false panel before they—"
> **Tessa:** "I have it. I've been keeping it safe."
> **Marren:** "Thank the Joybringer. I was so afraid they'd find it."
> **Tessa:** *(beat)* "I've been... reading it. Sharing it."
> **Marren:** *(confused, then understanding)* "You've been leading services? With my— with the Book?"
> **Tessa:** "Someone had to. You were gone. They needed someone."
> **Marren:** "Tessa... what exactly have you told them about yourself?"

**Phase 2 — The Crack**
Marren goes up on deck and meets the refugees. They look at him blankly. They don't know him. They look to *Tessa* for guidance on who this stranger is.

Someone says: "Prophet, who is this man?"

Tessa's mask flickers. Just for a moment. A PC with high Insight (DC 14) catches it — a flash of panic in her eyes before the warm, maternal smile returns.

> **Tessa:** "This is my husband, Marren. He was taken from us ten years ago by the Helmites. Lliira has returned him to us."

She frames it as a miracle. She takes ownership of the narrative. Classic Tessa.

Marren watches this happen. He sees her step into the role he recognizes as *his*. He's not sure what to feel yet. He's too tired, too overwhelmed.

**Phase 3 — The Slow Burn**
Don't resolve this tonight. Let it simmer. Possible moments:

- Marren picks up the Book and reads a passage aloud. His voice is different from Tessa's — warmer, less polished, more real. A refugee says: "That sounds... different when you say it." Tessa's jaw tightens.
- Marren calls Tessa "my wife" to the group. Not "Prophet." Not "leader." *Wife.* The word hangs in the air.
- If a PC examines the Book (DC 14 Investigation or Religion, 1 hour of study): the handwriting throughout doesn't match Tessa. The annotations are personal, intimate, written by someone else. Marren's voice is all over it. Tessa's isn't.
- If the Lastbloom is present and gets near the Book — both resonate. The flower blooms fuller. Pages flutter to: *"Joy is not owned. Joy is shared. Those who hoard it find only the memory of warmth."*

**What Marren Reveals:**
When asked about Joybright (and he will be asked):

> **Marren:** "Joybright... you're sailing to Joybright?" *(long silence)* "The city fell twenty-three years ago. When She died. The buildings still stand, but... the joy left. The Accord garrisons the coast. No one gets in."

> "But I've heard things. In the places I've been — prisons, safe houses, the dark corners where people still whisper her name. They say something *lingers* in Joybright. Something the Accord can't kill. Soldiers posted there hear laughter at night. They request transfers within weeks."

> "I don't know what's there. But something is. And if this Book is what I think it is... maybe it's meant to go home."

  - Sail directly → risk Weeping Watch navy
  - Dock at Greyshore (fishing village north of Joybright, ~1,100 pop) → approach by land

- The capital, ~45,000 population. Total Accord control

- **Risk:** If the Accord is watching Joybright's approaches, Greyshore might be monitored

**THE SHATTERED ISLE (Safe)**
- Volcanic, lawless, smugglers and dissenters operate openly
- Safest option for the refugees — the Accord has minimal presence
- But it's a dead end narratively unless you plant something there
- Could be a "park the refugees here" option while the party does dangerous things elsewhere

# ENCOUNTER: GRISSEL
## The Reluctant Smuggler

**IF the party seeks out Grissel** (the unfriendly goblin from the Golden Chain):

**Finding him:** He's not at the Golden Chain anymore (too much excitement). He's at a dockside shack he rents, packing his things. He's planning to leave Thornhaven — too much heat.

**Approaching him:**
- He doesn't want to talk. Default response: glare, silence, "Go away."
- Pushing gets: "I don't know you. I don't want to know you. Leave."
- **What works:** Respect. Not pushing. Offering fair payment. Acknowledging his expertise without demanding it.

**The Pitch:**
> "We have thirty people who need to move quietly. You used to move people. We can pay."

**Grissel's Response:**
> "Used to. Past tense. People died last time. I'm done." *(beat)* "Thirty? You're insane. You can't move thirty people quietly. The Accord has eyes on every harbor from here to the capital."

**What convinces him:**
- Coin (he's practical — 50gp minimum, 100gp preferred)
- Admitting they're in over their heads (honesty works better than bravado with Grissel)
- Mentioning Marren by name — Grissel recognized him. He knows what Marren represents. He won't admit it, but he cares
- DC 15 Persuasion with good roleplay, DC 18 without

**What Grissel offers:**
- Old smuggling routes — partially outdated but the geography hasn't changed
- Knowledge of safe harbors: Greyshore, Saltmere (tidal market, outside jurisdiction), Anchor's Rest (retired sailors who know hidden coves)
- A contact: someone in Greyshore who still owes him a favor
- His assessment: "Joybright? You're walking into a garrison with thirty civilians. Dock at Greyshore. Leave the flock there. Go to Joybright light and fast, or don't go at all."
- He will NOT come with them. Probably. Unless the party does something that genuinely earns his respect. Then he'll come and complain the entire time.

# POSSIBLE COMBAT ENCOUNTERS

### Encounter 1: Helmite Harbor Patrol (Medium)
**Trigger:** Party tries to leave and the warrant has arrived, or they're spotted loading suspiciously at night.

- 4 Helmite Watchers (use Guard stat block, AC 16, HP 22, +4 to hit, 1d8+2 damage)
- 1 Helmite Vigilant (use Veteran stat block, AC 17, HP 58, multiattack, +5 to hit)
- They attempt to detain, not kill (at first). "Halt! By order of the Accord, this vessel is under inspection!"
- If the party fights, the Vigilant blows a signal horn — 2d4 additional Watchers arrive in 5 rounds

**Alternatives to combat:**
- Bluff (DC 16 Deception — harder now, they may have descriptions)
- Tessa uses Suggestion or Charm (she'll do this instinctively to protect her people)
- Bribe the harbor watch (DC 14 Persuasion + 25gp minimum — not all Helmites are incorruptible)
- Distraction (fire on the docks? Grissel causing a scene? Creative player idea?)

### Encounter 2: Tonyult's "Message" (Hard, Optional)
**Trigger:** The party goes back to the Golden Chain or tries to leave without collecting Karsa.

Tonyult sends 3 hired thugs (use Bandit Captain + 2 Bandits stat block) with a message:

> "The proprietor of the Golden Chain sends his regards. He's been caring for your large friend. The large friend is ready to be collected. There is, of course, a service fee. The proprietor suggests you come discuss terms in person."

This isn't an attack — it's a business negotiation enforced by muscle. Tonyult wants:
- Information (where is the party going?)
- A favor (to be named later)
- Karsa's debt acknowledged

### Encounter 3: The Sending Stone Crackles (Narrative)
If the party has Nessandrle's sending stone, it activates:

> A voice, calm and professional: "Nessandrle. Status report. You missed your window. Confirm operational. Confirm operational."

Silence. Then:

> "Acknowledged. Dispatching secondary asset. Maintain cover if able. If compromised, standard protocol. Quill out."

The party now knows: someone else is coming. The clock is real.
