
We kick off on the boat after the dust has settled. Wolfric is dead. The party seemly divided. 

But all quiets down.

The group continue to sails toward joybright navigating varefully to avoid patrols. They make it through barely but decide to avoid the forbidden zone near joybright and stop at a small fishing town greyshore.

Once there they notice helmite guard sitting there but he does nothings.

They bother the town drunkard Aldren and drag him to the local tavern the Saltstone Inn

At the tavern they meet Eadra Finn. The innkeeper and long time resident of greyshore. She objects to Private Aldren being there. Says hes run up a tab and hasn't paid. Hes run up a very large tab. It becomes clear his father is the reason she has let him slide.

Karsa has no worries fronting the bill for him and others. After some discussion an offer comes from a paladin at the bar. A drinking competition. Winner gets a small keg of some of the finest ale from eagles rest in the northern range. Or so they say. 

Karsa makes it 8 rounds finishing a bottle of cheap wine per round. He stands victorious as the paladin falls to his face on the floor.

During this time Paul becomes acquantid with a silent individual at the corner of the bar. A pale, skinny  man named Lucien (Ian). The group eventually all connects and talks of books and power and weaklings and plans for the future. They drink the fine ale won by Karsa and each are affected in some way, laughing, or dreaming of better times. 

Soon two figures approach the table, a small man and a large orc. The orc states his business asking for some of the fine ale. In exchange information. He is introduced as Sir Dalton. The party will hear none of it. Sir Daltons tale seem stretched and pointless.

Lucian who had walked away decided to fake a helmite presence from outside to spoke the orc. Both run away thinking they are being watched. Eadra runs outside to see what is going on but finds nothing. She stomps over to the guard shack demanding to know why her patrons are being bothered. The guards deny and with them in tow she returns to the bar. They focus on the newcomers. Demanding justice or coin. Lucian parts with his coin to appease them. They leave though it feels unresolved.

The party collects once more finally deciding what to do. The plan is to take a road Lucian knows along to the coast to Joybright. Avoid the seas and ship. The party agrees. They collect their stuff and leave the refugees and children on the ship. The ship attempts to begin sailing as soon as they walk away.

Now marching north along the shore the party makes good pace and finally come upon the weeping stones. A stone wall layered with white salt that resembles tears streaming down a face. A reverent place, and a place to rest the night. A small cave is there as well, though not much in found inside, just the bite of bugs and an ominous feeling.
