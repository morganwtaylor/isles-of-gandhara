# The Day Joy Died

_Session One_

---

_The morning began like any other in Kell's Landing._
## A Stranger Arrives

Lil Moon arrived at Kell's Landing after traveling far from his homelands. Almost immediately, a dockhand named **Corvin Ashdale** approached with an offer: a few silver to haul some cargo into town. Easy money. Then another job, this time for gold. Lil Moon took both, eager for coin in an unfamiliar city.
## At the Docks

Wolfric had been in the city for eight months now, long enough to know the rhythms of the harbor. This morning he watched a medium-sized vessel arrive, her sails salt-worn but well-maintained. The matriarch of the ship, a broad-shouldered woman named **Tessa Brightwell**, needed supplies for her crew of thirty. She asked Wolfric to escort her young charge, a teenager named Daquavian, into town to purchase what they needed.

They were sailing to Joybright, she said. The city of eternal festivals.

## The Market Square

Wolfric and Daquavian found the main foods dealer in the market. The merchant, sensing the teenager carried a heavy purse (a thousand gold pieces for the voyage supplies), tried to take advantage. Wolfric negotiated months' worth of provisions for two hundred gold.

But while the deal was being struck, Helmite guards moved through the crowd. One of them lifted Wolfric's coin purse with practiced ease.

Wolfric, Lil Moon, and Murdock all converged on the thief: **Vigilant Corus Thane**, a Helmite officer in polished armor bearing the Staring Eye. He showed no remorse, only contempt. "Move along," he said.

They walked away, seething but outnumbered.

_And then the sky changed._

## The Western Sky

Everyone looked west. They couldn't help it. Something was happening beyond the horizon, beyond sight, but the sky didn't care about distance.

Light bloomed. Gold first, dawn-colored and gentle. Then rose, like the blush of a cheek. Then scarlet, deep and rich, the color of poppies, of festival ribbons, of a goddess's favorite flowers.

It was beautiful. It was the most beautiful thing anyone in Kell's Landing had ever seen.

_It was wrong._

The colors rippled and folded, shot through with darkness that wasn't shadow. The gold curdled. The rose bruised purple. The scarlet _screamed_.

Not a sound. A feeling. The color itself was screaming, and everyone who saw it felt it in their bones, in their teeth, in the empty space behind their hearts where joy used to live without them noticing.

Those with sharp eyes or divine sight saw more: shapes moving in the impossible light. A figure in golden armor, weeping. A figure in gray steel, arms wrapped around something that shone. A figure in black iron, hand raised in judgment.

And in the gray figure's grip, a woman made of light and laughter, hair like fire ribbons, dressed in scarlet that flowed like water. She was reaching toward the world below. Toward _them_.

_Remember me. Remember joy. Don't let them take it from you._

The sword fell. She didn't scream.

**She laughed.**

One last sound, bright and defiant and heartbroken. Then the scarlet light detonated, and for one instant, every happy memory anyone ever had flashed through their mind simultaneously.

Then it was gone. The colors drained from the sky like blood from a wound. The sun still shone, but it didn't feel like it.

**Lliira, Goddess of Joy, was dead. Joybright was gone. The Accord had won.**

## The Harbor Massacre

Daquavian collapsed, his faith in Ilmater shaken by the cosmic trauma. Wolfric helped the boy back to the ship, where thirty souls wailed and wept. The whole crew had been followers of Lliira, sailing to Joybright for the festivals.

They never got there. And now they never would.

Helmites stormed the docks within the hour, going ship to ship, dragging away anyone bound for Joybright. Anyone who worshiped the wrong goddess. Families. Children. The old man who'd been humming a forbidden hymn.

When the patrol reached Tessa's ship, our party stood their ground.

## Blood on the Deck

Words were exchanged. Wolfric, Lil Moon, and Murdock tried to turn the guards away with persuasion, intimidation, anything. It almost worked. But then Vigilant Corus Thane stepped forward, the same man who'd stolen from them in the market, and he was not interested in talking.

The other Helmites watched but didn't intervene. None of them liked Corus Thane, it seemed. They let him face the party alone.

He killed three people on the ship before he went down. He nearly killed the party too. But in the end, Corus Thane lay dead on the bloodied deck, and thirty souls remained free.

_From the body:_ Half-plate armor bearing Helm's eye. A steel shield. A longsword inscribed "Duty Endures." Three sets of manacles. A signal horn. A silver holy symbol of Helm.

## The Broken Tower

With nowhere else to turn, Wolfric led the group to the Broken Tower, where followers of Ilmater maintained a hidden sanctuary. The keeper there was cold, suspicious. These were dangerous times to trust strangers.

To prove their intentions, Murdock offered to undergo the Oath of Ilmater.

_"I offer my hands to hold another's burden,"_ he spoke in the underground chamber, a sick child before him, bound by red cord. _"I offer my pain to spare another theirs."_

The keeper watched. The cord was tied. The vows were spoken. And when it was done, Murdock rose not as a servant, but as a bearer of light in dark places.

The keeper's suspicion faded. Resources were shared. The party returned to Tessa's ship with supplies and provisions, but also with something harder to carry: the knowledge of what they'd seen, what they'd done, and what the world had become.

## What Comes Next

Thirty lives saved. One Helmite killed. The Accord will not forget.

Somewhere in the rubble of this day, a single scarlet flower grows in a crack in the cobblestones. No one has noticed it yet. By tomorrow, it might be trampled or pulled up.

_But today, it's still there._ Waiting.
