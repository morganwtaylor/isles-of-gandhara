**Session 1: The Day Joy Died**

**Opening Scene: Kell's Landing, Morning**

Establish normalcy first. Give them 20-30 minutes of just _being_ in the world before you break it. They're in Kell's Landing for whatever reason brought them together—a job, a ship arriving, personal business. Let them:

- Meet an NPC or two they'll care about later
- Notice small details (a Helmite patrol, a street shrine to Sune that feels perfunctory, someone humming an old hymn)
- Get a sense of each other if this is their first session together

Don't rush this. The tragedy hits harder if they've had a moment to breathe.

**The Hook That Gathered Them**

Pick something simple that gets them in proximity:

- Hired as guards/crew for a merchant vessel arriving today
- Responding to a job posting at a tavern (pest removal, missing person, cargo escort)
- One of them has a contact here who asked them to come

What matters is they're together and outside when it happens.

**Midmorning: The Sky Changes**

No warning. Mid-conversation, mid-task, mid-drink—the sky goes wrong.

Describe it in layers:

1. **Light shifts**. The sun doesn't dim, but the quality of light changes. Shadows fall at angles that don't match the sun's position. Colors look slightly off.
2. **Sound drops out**. For a heartbeat, silence. No gulls, no waves, no voices. Then it rushes back, muffled.
3. **They feel it**. Something in the chest. Not pain—_loss_. Like a word you've forgotten, like a name on the tip of your tongue, like the feeling after laughter fades but worse. Anyone with divine connection (paladin, cleric, even vaguely faithful characters) feels it sharper.
4. **The sky above the western horizon blooms with light**. Gold and rose and scarlet, beautiful and wrong. It lasts for a long moment. Then it gutters. Goes dark. The colors drain from the world—not literally, but emotionally. The day feels grayer.

**Immediate Aftermath in Kell's Landing**

People react before they understand:

- An old woman drops to her knees, weeping, clutching a faded ribbon
- A child laughs at something and stops mid-sound, confused, suddenly frightened
- A Helmite patrol exchanges glances—they don't know yet either, but they're alert
- Dogs howl. Bells ring in no pattern. Someone screams in the distance.

Then the rumors start filtering in. Fishermen who were out on the water saw it clearer. A merchant ship limping into port with news. By afternoon, the word spreads:

_Joybright is gone. The goddess is dead. The Accord has won._

**The Paladin's Moment**

Sometime during the sky-change or immediate aftermath, pull focus to your paladin. What happens depends on their oath situation:

- If Lliira-sworn: they feel her die. Not words, but impression—warmth fading, a last breath of something like gratitude or sorrow or defiance, then nothing where something used to be. Their powers don't vanish (yet?) but the source feels... echoing. Empty.
- If Accord-aligned: they feel their god's _satisfaction_. A sense of rightness from their divine patron. How does that sit with what they just witnessed?
- If uncommitted: something reaches for them in that moment. A brush of something desperate and fading. A choice implied.

Give them a private moment to react before the party regroups.

**The Rest of the Session: What Now?**

The players have witnessed a cosmic trauma. Now they need something to _do_. Options for the back half of the session:

**The authorities clamp down.** Helmite patrols double. A curfew is announced. The Tyrran courthouse begins "processing" known Lliiran sympathizers. The party sees someone they met that morning being dragged away.

**Someone needs their help.** An NPC approaches them—maybe someone who clocked them as newcomers, outsiders, not yet known to the Accord. They need to hide something. Move something. Get someone out of town before the inquisitors arrive.

**A choice is forced.** They witness an arrest. A beating. A public loyalty oath being administered. Do they intervene? Look away? Comply?

End the session on a decision point, not a resolution. They've seen what the Accord does. What do they do now?

**Practical Notes**

- Have the two absent players be "elsewhere" during the execution—arriving on a later ship, delayed on the road, whatever. They can hear about it secondhand in session 2 and have their own reaction.
- Seed at least one NPC who'll matter: a friendly tavern keeper, a nervous dockworker, a child who was laughing before. Someone they'll remember.
- If they ask what Joybright was like, describe it in past tense. The city of eternal festivals. Strangers dancing with strangers. The place where sorrow went to heal. Gone now.

**One Last Detail**

Sometime during the session, describe a single scarlet flower growing in a crack in the cobblestones. No one's noticed it yet. By tomorrow, it'll be trampled or pulled up.

But today, it's still there.
