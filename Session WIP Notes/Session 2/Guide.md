### Stop 2 (Day 5-7): Thornhaven

**What It Was:** A market town, halfway point for pilgrims. Famous for its Lliiran dancers, its wine, its street festivals that lasted for days.

**What It Is Now:** The Accord's regional administrative center for the upper Mirthwater. A Tyrran courthouse dominates the town square. The old festival grounds are now a "penitent labor camp" where dissenter families work the fields. The wine still flows, but no one dances.

**Why Stop Here:** They need supplies. The refugees need rest—some are sick, elderly, struggling. Tessa insists they can resupply and move on quickly.

_Tension:_ This is the most dangerous stop. Accord presence is heavy. Tyrran judges, Helmite patrols, Banite "advisors" from the capital. The party has to move through town without drawing attention while Tessa handles the "official" business.

_The Highlands Player:_ **This is where they join.**

They're here already—not as a refugee, but for their own reasons. Maybe they're:

- Part of an underground network moving people _out_ of the upper Mirthwater
- Searching for someone taken to the penitent camps
- Gathering information about Accordfall for dissenter groups
- Drawn by dreams/visions toward the ruins of Joybright

They notice the party's boat. Recognize fellow travelers. Or they approach the party in town—a warning, an offer of help, a question about where they're headed.

_Complication Options:_

- A refugee is recognized by a Tyrran official—someone who processed their family years ago
- Tessa's performance is _too_ good; she draws admiring attention from a Banite priest who wants to travel with them
- The party spots wanted notices with their descriptions
- Someone in the penitent camp calls out to the refugees—a relative, an old friend
